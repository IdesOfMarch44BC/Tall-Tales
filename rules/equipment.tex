\chapter{Equipment and the Two Monies}

\section{Coins, Funds, and What Things Cost}

\textbf{Coins} are Rings --- the Concord's neutral metal, good everywhere, heavy in the pocket. \textbf{Funds} are promises: scrip, letters of draw, banked credit, a factor's goodwill --- worth their issuer's health, subject to the spread, and how anything expensive actually moves. The tables below price honest gear in Rings; anything marked \textbf{(F)} is a Funds purchase --- a negotiation, a relationship, and a paper trail, not a transaction.

A working guide to scale: a meal is a tenth-Ring; a night's bed, half; a week's wages for honest labor, 4--6 Rings; a revolver is a month's pay; a riding animal a season's; a frame chassis is a mortgage.

\section{Class Kits}

Each class starts with its kit and \textbf{10 Rings}:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Zealot:} saber or greataxe, duster, war shield \textit{or} riding leathers, iron lantern, the sigil.
    \item \textbf{Circuit Rider:} revolver, riding leathers, riding animal, circuit bag (Consumable cards stocked), the book.
    \item \textbf{Arcanist:} the chained book, derringer, duster, ward-chalk, Enclave ward-token.
    \item \textbf{Dynast:} weapon of the line (saber, bone-blade at Houses' discretion, or heavy revolver), riding leathers, signet.
    \item \textbf{Monk:} the blade is the kit. Duster, walking staff (club), bowl.
    \item \textbf{Tinkerer:} revolver, duster, Guild tools (proper), 2 storage cells, hall papers.
    \item \textbf{Sawbones:} revolver, duster, the bag (proper instruments; Consumable cards stocked), hall papers.
    \item \textbf{Captain:} carbine \textit{or} heavy revolver, riding leathers, the ledger, stock license, colors.
    \item \textbf{Bounty Hunter:} revolver \textit{and} knife, duster, manacles, seal-tag, one writ (the GM writes the name).
    \item \textbf{Shapeshifter:} no-iron kit (bronze knife, bone-tipped spear), pelt or walking clothes per tradition, relay tokens.
\end{itemize}

\section{Design Notes}

\textbf{Weapons are positions, not power levels.} A revolver and a rifle kill the same unarmored fool; the rifle's business is range and the AP point, the shotgun's is the doorway, the buffalo gun's is the one shot that must not bounce, and the knife's is the man already bleeding --- the stacking spiral makes every light weapon a finisher. Damage values were set against the class thresholds so that first hits on hard targets land \textit{minor} and the fight escalates through the spiral, while soft targets skip straight to moderate: toughness is felt as \textit{time}, and time is what Momentum buys.

\textbf{Armor buys absences, not hit points.} Each save step roughly halves the wounds that ever land, which the simulation bears out --- leathers and Defend 1 turn a three-volley death into a ten-volley fight. AP exists to delete that comfort by exactly its number, Bulk prices the plate in inches, and Negation is kept scarce, expensive, and mostly \textbf{(F)} because it is the only wall the strange world respects.
