# Creating a Character

## The Eight Steps

1.  **Choose your species.** Take its trait and learn one PL 0 or PL 1 species power. (Chapter: *The Seventeen Peoples*.)

2.  **Choose your class and archetype.** Note your class stat line, Signature Skill, and starting class-power count below. Learn that many PL 0 or PL 1 powers from your archetype. Every class now has a complete list — Volumes 1–2 cover Witness through Sawbone, Volume 3 the Captain, Bounty Hunter, and Shapeshifter.

3.  **Choose your faction** — or none. Learn one PL 0 or PL 1 power from that faction or the Unaffiliated list. This choice is separate from the Drifter class.

4.  **Rate your Skills.** Distribute **20 points** across the twelve skills, **maximum 4** in any one. Then apply your class’s **+1 Signature Skill** bonus — the one place a starting character may stand at 5, already past the line the world calls supernatural influence.

5.  **Rate your Alignments.** Distribute exactly **10 permanent dots** across the five planes, **maximum 3** in any one at creation. Every dot is a relationship; the GM is invited to remember all of them. Make sure your distribution can meet the requirements of your chosen powers.

6.  **Write your Identity.** Demeanor (how you present), Drive (what moves you), and a Reputation the GM co-signs and thereafter owns. Leave the wanted-poster line blank if you dare.

7.  **Take your kit.** Each class lists a starting kit in the Equipment chapter; note your Armor line (X+/Y+), weapon lines, and 10 Rings walking money.

8.  **Record powers and reserves.** Copy your known powers. Write each Alignment as **available/permanent** and begin with every reserve full. You’re on the frontier.

## Class Stat Lines

Your class sets the combat line and the two damage thresholds. (Targets: lower is better. Defend subtracts attacker dice. Thresholds: damage at or past **Mod** is a moderate wound; at or past **Maj**, major.)


| **Class** | **Strike** | **Shoot** | **Defend** | **Move** | **Mod** | **Maj** | **Signature Skill** |
|:---|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--:|:---|
| Witness | 3+ | 4+ | 1 | 4" | 5 | 10 | Religion |
| Drifter | 4+ | 4+ | 1 | 5" | 4 | 9 | Survive |
| Arcanist | 5+ | 4+ | 0 | 4" | 3 | 7 | Study |
| Dynast | 3+ | 4+ | 1 | 5" | 5 | 10 | Intimidate |
| Mentalist | 3+ | 5+ | 2 | 5" | 4 | 8 | Study |
| Tinkerer | 5+ | 4+ | 0 | 4" | 4 | 8 | Knack |
| Sawbone | 4+ | 4+ | 0 | 4" | 4 | 8 | Study |
| Captain | 4+ | 3+ | 1 | 4" | 4 | 9 | Convince |
| Bounty Hunter | 4+ | 3+ | 1 | 5" | 4 | 8 | Survive |
| Shapeshifter | 4+ | 4+ | 1 | 5" | 4 | 9 | Folklore |


*Reading the spread:* the durable callings (Witness, Dynast, Mentalist) close and endure; the trigger professions (Captain, Bounty Hunter) shoot at 3+; the technical classes (Arcanist, Tinkerer, Sawbone) know more powers and are **fragile in the open** — an Arcanist at Mod 3/Maj 7 treats a pistol ball as a moderate wound from the first hit, which is the correct amount of afraid. Shapeshifter values are the walker’s own body; worn forms use the form’s line per the class rules.

### Starting Powers Known

| Class | Class powers at creation | Class powers learned at each later level |
|:--|:--:|:--:|
| Arcanist, Tinkerer, Sawbone | 5 | 2 |
| Drifter, Mentalist | 4 | 1 |
| Witness, Dynast, Captain, Bounty Hunter, Shapeshifter | 3 | 1 |

These are class powers only. Every character also begins knowing one species power and one faction or Unaffiliated power. All starting powers must be PL 0 or PL 1. Technical specialists learn more because their combat line assumes broad power access; weapon-focused classes receive stronger base statistics and begin with fewer powers.

**Species adjustments.** Species traits modify the class line where they say: Pelorim Move is 4" before other modifiers; Korr fly 6" within the trait’s limits; Gorûn and Serekh count as Large where equipment cares.

### Shapeshifter Forms

A Shapeshifter begins with **three specific Forms** earned through captured hearts, willing bonds, or equivalent rites. Name the creature and relationship; “a wolf” is not a Form, but “Grey-Muzzle, who yielded her heart at Northwatch” is. Choose the closest body template for each Form:

| Template | Move | Defend | Thresholds | Natural attack | Typical adaptation |
|:--|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--|:--|
| **Walker / Mimic** | Base | Base | Base | 2 dice, Damage 3 Blunt or the form’s obvious type | Hands, speech, or inconspicuous shape |
| **Stalker** | Base +2" | Base +1 | Base | 3 dice, Damage 4 Slash or Pierce | Scent, climbing, or night sight |
| **Brute** | Base –1" | Base –1 (min 0) | Mod +1, Maj +2 | 3 dice, Damage 5 Blunt or Slash | Large, hauling, or natural Armor 6+ |
| **Flyer** | Fly 6" | Base +1 | Mod –1, Maj –1 | 2 dice, Damage 3 Slash | Flight and far sight |
| **Swimmer / Burrower** | 6" in its medium; 3" otherwise | Base | Base | 3 dice, Damage 4 Pierce, Slash, or Blunt | Water breathing, tremorsense, or tunneling |

Shifting into a Form costs **1 Momentum**; returning to your own body is free during your side’s surge. Retain Skills, permanent Alignments and available reserves, Strike/Shoot targets, known powers, wounds, and Negation. Replace Move, Defend, thresholds, natural attack, and bodily capabilities with the Form’s values. Ordinary worn Armor and weapons merge or drop when you shift and cannot be used unless the Form can physically use them. Wounds do not disappear when changing shape.

After resolving a **major wound** in a Form, return to your own body unless an effect such as *The Skin Holds* prevents it. This reversion does not remove the wound or its effects.

Each Form also carries an **instinct** chosen with the GM—pursue fleeing prey, avoid open flame, protect the herd, hoard bright objects, and so on. The instinct creates roleplaying pressure, not loss of player control. Learn one additional Form at levels 3, 6, and 9, and whenever the GM awards one for a significant bond or hunt.

## Advancement

Characters begin at **level 1** with the creation benefits above; do not also apply advancement for level 1. The core progression runs through **level 10**. Whenever a character gains a later level, apply every benefit listed for that new level; bonuses are cumulative unless stated otherwise.


|  |  |
|:---|:---|
| **Every gained level (L2–L10)** | Learn the number of class powers shown above (PL ≤ level); +1 skill point (caps: 5, then 7 at L5+, 10 at L9+) |
| **Even levels** | +1 Deed rank (new deed at I, or upgrade one held) |
| **L3 / L6 / L9** | Move one permanent Alignment dot to another Alignment; the per-Alignment cap becomes 4 / 5 / 6 respectively. The total remains 10. |
| **Shapeshifter L3 / L6 / L9** | Learn one additional specific Form |
| **L4 / L8** | +1 Moderate threshold *and* +1 Major threshold |
| **L5 / L7 / L9** | +1 to one of Strike, Shoot (improve target by 1, min 2+) or Defend (max 3) — a different stat each time |


GM awards (deed ranks for deeds done and powers learned from the world) ride on top of the ladder, never instead of it. Learning a world power requires a meaningful source and cannot exceed the character’s level.

## Record Your Combat Profile

Copy these values to the character sheet:

- **Strike / Shoot:** target numbers; lower is better.
- **Defend:** dice removed from attacks against you.
- **Move:** inches moved by a 1-Momentum Move action.
- **Moderate / Major:** Damage thresholds for wound severity.
- **Armor / Negation:** d6 saves written as X+; use “—” if absent.
- **Weapons:** Attack Dice, To-Hit modifier, Damage, AP, range, type, and tags.
- **Alignments:** permanent ratings and currently available reserves.
- **Powers known:** name, PL, complete Alignment requirement, timing, and effect.

There is no health total. Care powers grant **Recovery**, which closes or reduces specific wounds according to *Combat and Wounds*.

## Design Notes: Where the Numbers Sit

The thresholds and the Equipment chapter’s weapon values were set together and tested against the wound tables’ lethality (majors kill 41.7% before saves). The tuning targets, verified by simulation: an **unarmored average character** (Mod 4/Maj 8, Defend 0) facing a revolver is at even odds of dying inside three volleys and near-certain inside five — the open street is death, and the game means it; a **hardened, armored veteran** (Mod 5/Maj 10, Defend 1, leathers) weathers ten; a **knife** almost never kills a healthy target quickly but rides the wound spiral viciously as a finisher; and **heavy plate defeats even a buffalo gun** until armor-piercing rounds come out, which is what they are for. Cover, Defend, armor, and going first are worth more than any weapon upgrade — exactly the economy a gunfight should have.
