# Creating a Character

## The Eight Steps

1.  **Choose your species.** Take its trait and learn one PL 0 or PL 1 species entry. A supernatural entry names one of the five power sources; an ordinary biological or cultural ability is a Trait. (Chapter: *The Seventeen Peoples*.)

2.  **Choose your class and power source.** Note your class stat line, Signature Skill, class features, and starting selection count below. Learn that many PL 0 or PL 1 entries from your class. What one selection means depends on the source: a Legend stance or exploit, a Magic spell, a Dynast patron gift or Shapeshifter form contract/clause, a Psionic technique, or a Superscience principle.

3.  **Choose your faction** — or none. Learn one PL 0 or PL 1 entry from that faction or the Unaffiliated list. This choice is separate from the Drifter class. As with species entries, it must name a source or say that it is a Trait, Training, Standing, or Access entry.

4.  **Rate your Skills.** Distribute **20 points** across the twelve skills, **maximum 4** in any one. Then apply your class’s **+1 Signature Skill** bonus — the one place a starting character may stand at 5, already past the line the world calls supernatural influence.

5.  **Rate your Alignments.** Distribute exactly **10 permanent dots** across the five planes, **maximum 3** in any one at creation. Every dot is a relationship; the GM is invited to remember all of them. Make sure your distribution can meet the requirements and simultaneous commitments of your chosen entries.

6.  **Write your Identity.** Demeanor (how you present), Drive (what moves you), and a Reputation the GM co-signs and thereafter owns. Leave the wanted-poster line blank if you dare.

7.  **Take your kit.** Each class lists a starting kit in the Equipment chapter; note your Armor line (X+/Y+), weapon lines, and 10 Rings walking money.

8.  **Record powers and reserves.** Copy your known stances, exploits, spells, contracts, psychic techniques, and construction principles. Record any starting devices and their Operating Requirements. Write each Alignment as **available/permanent** and begin with every reserve full. You’re on the frontier.

## Class Stat Lines

Your class sets the combat line and the two damage thresholds. (Targets: lower is better. Defend subtracts attacker dice. Thresholds: damage at or past **Mod** is a moderate wound; at or past **Maj**, major.)


| **Class** | **Strike** | **Shoot** | **Defend** | **Move** | **Mod** | **Maj** | **Signature Skill** |
|:---|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--:|:---|
| Witness | 3+ | 4+ | 1 | 4" | 5 | 10 | Religion |
| Drifter | 4+ | 4+ | 1 | 5" | 4 | 9 | Survive |
| Arcanist | 5+ | 4+ | 0 | 4" | 3 | 7 | Study |
| Dynast | 3+ | 4+ | 1 | 5" | 5 | 10 | Intimidate |
| Mentalist | 3+ | 5+ | 2 | 5" | 4 | 8 | Study |
| Tinkerer | 5+ | 4+ | 0 | 4" | 4 | 8 | Knack |
| Sawbone | 4+ | 4+ | 0 | 4" | 4 | 8 | Study |

| Bounty Hunter | 4+ | 3+ | 1 | 5" | 4 | 8 | Survive |
| Shapeshifter | 4+ | 4+ | 1 | 5" | 4 | 9 | Folklore |


*Reading the spread:* the durable callings (Witness, Dynast, Mentalist) close and endure; the trigger professions (Captain, Bounty Hunter) shoot at 3+; the power-focused classes (Arcanist, Tinkerer, Sawbone) start with more entries and are **fragile in the open** — an Arcanist at Mod 3/Maj 7 treats a pistol ball as a moderate wound from the first hit, which is the correct amount of afraid. Shapeshifter values are the walker's own body; assumed forms use the compact form profile below.

### Class Features

**Arcane Burden (Arcanist).** Every Arcanist casting leaves literal matter and energy behind in the caster. This **arcane mass** has real, measurable weight; it is not a metaphor, spiritual debt, or metaphysical encumbrance. The Arcanist's class profile abstracts carrying it, so do not track grams separately. An Arcanist cannot benefit from an Armor save granted by worn gear, although they may use shields and Negation. A Witness's power comes from a higher force that bears the mass of the working, so Witnesses do not have this feature.

**Mind Weapon (Bounty Hunter).** A Bounty Hunter may manifest either the mind blade or mind gun profile in the Equipment chapter once per surge as a Free action, dismissing any previous form. This is a class feature: it consumes no Psionics selection, spends no Alignment, and requires no manifestation roll. The weapon needs no ammunition, cannot be conventionally disarmed, and deals Wyrd damage. An effect that suppresses Psionics dismisses it; it can be manifested again after the suppression ends.

### Starting Source Selections

| Class | Source | At creation | At each later level | One selection |
|:--|:--|:--:|:--:|:--|
| Captain | Legend | 3 | 1 | Stance or exploit |
| Drifter | Legend | 4 | 1 | Stance or exploit |
| Arcanist | Magic | 5 | 1 | Spell |
| Witness | Magic | 3 | 1 | Spell |
| Dynast | Occultism | 3 | 1 | Individual gift from an available patron list |
| Shapeshifter | Occultism | 3 | 1 | Creature form contract or deeper form clause |
| Bounty Hunter | Psionics | 3 | 1 | Psychic technique |
| Mentalist | Psionics | 4 | 1 | Psychic technique |
| Sawbone | Superscience | 5 | 1 | Construction principle |
| Tinkerer | Superscience | 5 | 1 | Construction principle |

These are class selections only. Every character also begins with one species entry and one faction or Unaffiliated entry. All starting entries must be PL 0 or PL 1. Technical specialists start broader because their combat line assumes power access; weapon-focused classes receive stronger base statistics. After creation, every class gains the same lean one selection per level. A practitioner's real breadth is learned from the world, so two characters of one class and level should differ mainly in where they have been.

**Starting Superscience projects.** A Sawbone or Tinkerer begins with up to two simple devices made from their known PL 0 or PL 1 principles. Design them with the GM and record each function's Operating Requirement, action, one-use or persistent duration, fuel, maintenance, and failure condition. Construction does not tie up the maker's reserve. Consumable devices begin with the uses supplied by the class kit; whoever operates or primes a device supplies its Alignment.

**Species adjustments.** Species traits modify the class line where they say: Pelorim Move is 4" before other modifiers; Korr fly 6" within the trait’s limits; Gorûn and Serekh count as Large where equipment cares.

### Dynast Patrons

A Dynast begins with one PL 0 or PL 1 patron contract provided by lineage and confirmed through a personal bargain. The patron relationship is a class feature and does not consume one of the Dynast's three starting selections. Record its identity, PL, Bond, Terms, Need, active Tell, Breach, and coherent gift list. The three selections are individual PL 0 or PL 1 gifts learned from that list.

Every learned patron gift must name a **Manifestation**: a permanent physical alteration such as scales, horn, metal, pallor, script, glass, changed proportions, or stranger anatomy. It remains visible or tangible while the gift is inactive, suppressed, or unavailable through Breach. Later selections learn individual gifts from any available patron list. Making a new patron contract through play opens that patron's list; it grants a gift only when the reward explicitly says so.

### Shapeshifter Forms

A Shapeshifter's three starting selections are **three specific creature contracts** earned through captured hearts, willing bonds, or equivalent rites. Name the creature and relationship; “a wolf” is not a contract, but “Grey-Muzzle, who yielded her heart at Northwatch” is.

For each creature record its PL, a Bond whose dots total that PL, Terms, Need, Tell, instinct, Breach, and release condition. Then choose the closest body template:

| Template | Move | Defend | Thresholds | Natural attack | Typical adaptation |
|:--|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--|:--|
| **Walker / Mimic** | Base | Base | Base | 2 dice, Damage 3 Blunt or the form’s obvious type | Hands, speech, or inconspicuous shape |
| **Stalker** | Base +2" | Base +1 | Base | 3 dice, Damage 4 Slash or Pierce | Scent, climbing, or night sight |
| **Brute** | Base –1" | Base –1 (min 0) | Mod +1, Maj +2 | 3 dice, Damage 5 Blunt or Slash | Large, hauling, or natural Armor 6+ |
| **Flyer** | Fly 6" | Base +1 | Mod –1, Maj –1 | 2 dice, Damage 3 Slash | Flight and far sight |
| **Swimmer / Burrower** | 6" in its medium; 3" otherwise | Base | Base | 3 dice, Damage 4 Pierce, Slash, or Blunt | Water breathing, tremorsense, or tunneling |

Opening a form contract costs **1 Momentum** and stakes its Bond dots from the Shapeshifter's available reserve. Returning to the walker's body is free during the character's side's surge. On an ordinary release, the staked dots become eligible to refresh when that side next takes Momentum. On a breach, the contract says when they return.

Retain Skills, permanent Alignments, Strike/Shoot targets, known entries, wounds, and Negation. Replace Move, Defend, thresholds, natural attack, and bodily capabilities with the form's values. Ordinary worn Armor and weapons merge or drop when shifting and cannot be used unless the form can physically use them. Wounds do not disappear when changing shape.

After resolving a **major wound** in a form, return to the walker's body unless a contract clause prevents it. This reversion does not remove the wound or its effects and counts as an ordinary release unless the contract's Breach says otherwise.

An **instinct** might be pursue fleeing prey, avoid open flame, protect the herd, or hoard bright objects. It creates roleplaying pressure, not loss of player control. Later class selections may add a new creature contract or deepen an existing one with a defining adaptation. The GM may also award a contract for a significant relationship, service, or hunt.

## Advancement

Characters begin at **level 1** with the creation benefits above; do not also apply advancement for level 1. The core progression runs through **level 20**, in four bands: levels 1–2 are armed and competent professionals, 3–6 action-movie heroes, 7–12 clearly superhuman, and 13–20 city-ending threats. Whenever a character gains a later level, apply every benefit listed for that new level; bonuses are cumulative unless stated otherwise.

Maximum learnable Power Level is **half character level, rounded up**, equivalently level **(2 × PL) − 1**: PL 1 at levels 1–2, PL 2 at levels 3–4, and so on through PL 10 at levels 19–20.


|  |  |
|:---|:---|
| **Every gained level (L2–L20)** | +1 class source selection at an unlocked PL; +1 skill point (caps: 5, then 7 at L5+, 10 at L9+) |
| **Even levels** | +1 Deed rank (new deed at I, or upgrade one held) |
| **L3 / L6 / L9 (then L12 / L15 / L18)** | Move one permanent Alignment dot to another Alignment; the per-Alignment cap becomes 4 / 5 / 6 and holds at 6 thereafter. The total always remains 10 — a life's ten dots are re-aimed, never multiplied. |
| **Shapeshifter L3 / L6 / L9** | Learn one additional specific Form |
| **L4 / L8** | +1 Moderate threshold *and* +1 Major threshold |
| **L5 / L7 / L9** | +1 to one of Strike, Shoot (improve target by 1, min 2+) or Defend (max 3) — a different stat each time |


GM awards (deed ranks for deeds done and source entries learned from the world) ride on top of the ladder, never instead of it. World-learning has no numeric cap, but every entry requires a meaningful source and cannot exceed half the character's level, rounded up.

## Record Your Combat Profile

Copy these values to the character sheet:

- **Strike / Shoot:** target numbers; lower is better.
- **Defend:** dice removed from attacks against you.
- **Move:** inches moved by a 1-Momentum Move action.
- **Moderate / Major:** Damage thresholds for wound severity.
- **Armor / Negation:** d6 saves written as X+; use “—” if absent.
- **Weapons:** Attack Dice, To-Hit modifier, Damage, AP, range, type, and tags.
- **Alignments:** permanent ratings and currently available reserves.
- **Source entries known:** name, source, PL, Alignment requirement and use, activation, effect, duration or release, and source risk.
- **Devices and open commitments:** Operating Requirements, staked Bonds, maintained stances, primed or running devices, and which reserve dots remain unavailable.

There is no health total. Care effects—spells, psychic techniques, form gifts, and Superscience devices among them—grant **Recovery**, which closes or reduces specific wounds according to *Combat and Wounds*.

## Design Notes: Where the Numbers Sit

The thresholds and the Equipment chapter’s weapon values were set together and tested against the wound tables’ lethality (majors kill 41.7% before saves). The tuning targets, verified by simulation: an **unarmored average character** (Mod 4/Maj 8, Defend 0) facing a revolver is at even odds of dying inside three volleys and near-certain inside five — the open street is death, and the game means it; a **hardened, armored veteran** (Mod 5/Maj 10, Defend 1, leathers) weathers ten; a **knife** almost never kills a healthy target quickly but rides the wound spiral viciously as a finisher; and **heavy plate defeats even a buffalo gun** until armor-piercing rounds come out, which is what they are for. Cover, Defend, armor, and going first are worth more than any weapon upgrade — exactly the economy a gunfight should have.
