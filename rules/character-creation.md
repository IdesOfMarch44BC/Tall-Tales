# Creating a Character

## The Eight Steps

1.  **Choose your species.** Take its trait; note its 5-card pool. (Chapter: *The Seventeen Peoples*.)

2.  **Choose your class and archetype.** Note your class stat line (below), your Signature Skill, and choose 10 cards from your archetype’s pool (level-1 cards; duplicates as marked).

3.  **Choose your faction** — or none. Take the 5 faction cards, or the Drifter set, and know who your people are.

4.  **Rate your Skills.** Distribute **20 points** across the twelve skills, **maximum 4** in any one. Then apply your class’s **+1 Signature Skill** bonus — the one place a starting character may stand at 5, already past the line the world calls supernatural influence.

5.  **Rate your Alignments.** Distribute **10 points** across the five planes, **maximum 3** in any one. Every point is a relationship; the GM is invited to remember all of them.

6.  **Write your Identity.** Demeanor (how you present), Drive (what moves you), and a Reputation the GM co-signs and thereafter owns. Leave the wanted-poster line blank if you dare.

7.  **Take your kit.** Each class lists a starting kit in the Equipment chapter; note your Armor line (X+/Y+), weapon lines, and 10 Rings walking money.

8.  **Assemble the deck.** 20 cards. Shuffle. Holster. You’re on the frontier.

## Class Stat Lines

Your class sets the combat line and the two damage thresholds. (Targets: lower is better. Defend subtracts attacker dice. Thresholds: damage at or past **Mod** is a moderate wound; at or past **Maj**, major.)

*Reading the spread:* the durable callings (Witness, Dynast, Mentalist) close and endure; the trigger professions (Captain, Bounty Hunter) shoot at 3+; the technical classes (Arcanist, Tinkerer, Sawbone) are dangerous through their decks and **fragile in the open** — an Arcanist at Mod 3/Maj 7 treats a pistol ball as a moderate wound from the first hit, which is the correct amount of afraid. Shapeshifter values are the walker’s own body; worn forms use the form’s line per the class rules.

**Species adjustments.** Species traits modify where they say (Pelorim Move 4" base and quadruped rules; Korr fly 6" per their trait’s limits; Gorûn and Serekh count as Large where equipment cares). Older trait text written in *tiles* reads 1 tile = 2".

## Advancement

<div class="center">

|  |  |
|:---|:---|
| **Every level** | +2 cards (any of your pools, card level $`\le`$ your level); swap 1 card; +1 skill point (caps: 5, then 7 at L5+, 10 at L9+) |
| **Even levels** | +1 Deed rank (new deed at I, or upgrade one held) |
| **L3 / L6 / L9** | +1 Alignment point (cap 5, then 7 at L9+) |
| **L4 / L8** | +1 Moderate threshold *and* +1 Major threshold |
| **L5 / L7 / L9** | +1 to one of Strike, Shoot (improve target by 1, min 2+) or Defend (max 3) — a different stat each time |

</div>

GM awards (deed ranks for deeds done, cards for techniques earned in fiction) ride on top of the ladder, never instead of it.

## Wounds, Healing, and Old Cards (Conversion Sidebar)

The wound system replaced hit points; two conversions keep every earlier document current until their next revision:

**Healing.** Where a card says *heal N HP*: each 2 points of that healing **clears one minor wound**; each 3 points **reduces one wound a severity step** (major$`\to`$moderate$`\to`$minor$`\to`$closed). *Stabilize* effects halt Bleeding/Dying as written. Effects that said *"drops to 1 HP instead of 0"* now read *"you are not Dying/downed by this wound; it lands one severity lower."*

**Deathproof (deed) reads as:** **I** +1 Moderate threshold; **II** +1 Major threshold and +1 die on saves while carrying a major wound; **III** unchanged in spirit — once per scene, when a wound would leave you Dying, it leaves you disabled instead; **IV** unchanged — once per session, a Death result is Dying instead. *("Too mean to bury" survives translation intact.)*

## Design Notes: Where the Numbers Sit

The thresholds and the Equipment chapter’s weapon values were set together and tested against the wound tables’ lethality (majors kill 41.7% before saves). The tuning targets, verified by simulation: an **unarmored average character** (Mod 4/Maj 8, Defend 0) facing a revolver is at even odds of dying inside three volleys and near-certain inside five — the open street is death, and the game means it; a **hardened, armored veteran** (Mod 5/Maj 10, Defend 1, leathers) weathers ten; a **knife** almost never kills a healthy target quickly but rides the wound spiral viciously as a finisher; and **heavy plate defeats even a buffalo gun** until armor-piercing rounds come out, which is what they are for. Cover, Defend, armor, and going first are worth more than any weapon upgrade — exactly the economy a gunfight should have.
