# How the Game Works

## The Shape of Play

You play one of the frontier’s rare capable people — the sort the factions recruit, the factors hire, and the ground remembers — and the sheet measures you in three currencies.

**Skills** are what you have learned: twelve trained competences, rated 0–10 and rolled in dice. **Alignments** are what you can call on: ten permanent dots distributed across the five great natures of the cosmos. **Powers** are reusable capabilities known through class training and discoveries made in the world. Every power uses Alignment, but its source determines whether those dots are spent, committed, staked, or built into a device's operating requirement.

Everything in an encounter runs on **Momentum** — a shared, swingy pool of action your whole side spends together — and the rhythm of play is simple: your side surges, spends, and passes; theirs answers; and the fight is decided as much by how each side spends its luck as by what any one character can do.

One thing to know before the numbers: none of this machinery is arbitrary. The five Alignments are the five planes — the Heavens, the Machine, the Shadow, the Wild, and the Wyrd — and their pentacle on your sheet is a map of the actual cosmos. When you mark a dot in one, you are recording a relationship. When you use one, you are briefly conducting a power that holds the world’s spinning top upright. The top is stable only in motion. So are you. That is what Momentum is.

## Rules at a Glance

| When you… | Do this |
|:--|:--|
| Attempt a task | Roll Skill dice; each 4+ is a success; meet required successes. |
| Augment a task | Add up to your Alignment rating in dice; the plane may complicate the result. |
| Invoke a plane | Take automatic successes equal to the Alignment rating and always accept a planar price. |
| Make an attack | Roll weapon Attack Dice minus Defend; each die meeting Strike or Shoot is a hit. |
| Resolve a hit | Weapon Damage + existing-wound bonus → severity → Armor/Negation → location and effect. |
| Use a power | Follow its source procedure: Legend, Magic, Occultism, Psionics, or Superscience. |
| Recover | Spend Recovery immediately: 1 clears a transient effect, 2 closes a minor wound, 3 reduces a wound one step. |

The three dice procedures are deliberately different: **Skills use a fixed 4+**, **attacks use class target numbers**, and **Armor/Negation use equipment save numbers**.

## Dice and Ratings

The game uses six-sided dice, rolled in pools, and one rule underneath everything:


**A die succeeds on a 4, 5, or 6. Count your successes.**


The target never moves. What moves is how many successes a task requires, and how many dice you bring.

**Ratings.** Skills and Alignments are rated **0–10**, and the scale means something in the world:


| **Rating** | **Standing**               |                                      |
|:-----------|:---------------------------|:-------------------------------------|
| 1–2        | Capable                    | An ordinary practitioner             |
| 3–4        | Professional               | The person the town sends for        |
| **5–7**    | **Supernatural influence** | Past what flesh and practice explain |
| **8–10**   | **Masterful**              | The handful per generation           |


The thresholds are not flavor. At 5+, a rating has crossed into territory the setting treats as supernatural — the Guild examiner’s impossible eye, the duelist the Corvara would call quick, the tracker the network vouches without a second’s pause — and effects, powers, and traits will reference the thresholds by name. At 8+, you are a fact of the region.

**Task resolution.** Roll dice equal to your Skill; count 4+ successes; compare to what the GM asked for:


| **Required successes** | **Feels like**                    |
|:-----------------------|:----------------------------------|
| 1                      | A real task with real stakes      |
| 2                      | Hard; the frontier’s usual answer |
| 3                      | A professional’s bad afternoon    |
| 4–5                    | Stories get told                  |


Extra successes beyond the requirement are **margin**: faster, cleaner, quieter, *more*. The GM converts margin into advantage, and players should feel free to propose what their margin buys. Situational edges and burdens (good tools, darkness, a Serekh’s spines, a stolen pelt) add or subtract **dice**, not successes — the 4+ line is bedrock.

**Zero rating.** You may usually still try: roll a single die, and the GM may charge an extra required success. Some checks are trained-only.

**Automatic information.** Some traits (Uln Icecunning, Brindel Brushcunning) grant automatic success on specific Perceive checks. Inside the trait’s territory, the dice concede. **Survive** follows a trail; **Perceive** notices one.

### The Twelve Skills

**Mobility**  
— **Athletics** (running, climbing, swimming, force in motion — and the fuel gauge of Korr flight); **Knack** (hands and tools under pressure: locks, knots, sleight, jury-rigs); **Nimbleness** (balance, evasion, squeezing, grace — the Tikka’s and Dóm’s home ground).

**Exploration**  
— **Investigate** (deliberate examination: scenes, documents, ledgers, lies already told); **Perceive** (the passive and the immediate: notice, spot, hear, read a room); **Survive** (weather, forage, trails, the country’s rules — and following what left sign).

**Knowledge**  
— **Study** (the learned disciplines: procedure, history, engineering, law); **Folklore** (what the road knows: customs, sign, stories that are load-bearing, which glyph means *do not*); **Religion** (the Heavens, the churches, the rites — and what the rites are *for*).

**Social**  
— **Convince** (honest persuasion: appeal, negotiation, testimony); **Deceive** (the crafted impression: lies, disguise, the face-taker’s second discipline); **Intimidate** (fear as an instrument — the Nol’s cultivated inheritance).

Your point allocation, class Signature Skill, species, powers, and Deeds determine which of these your life trained; the sheet does not care how you came by them.

## Alignments

The five Alignments are the world’s five natures, rated 0–10 like everything else, and arranged on your sheet as the pentacle they actually form.


|             |                                   |
|:------------|:----------------------------------|
| **Heavens** | Compassion, law, morality         |
| **Machine** | Cleverness, logic, technology     |
| **Shadow**  | Ambition, deception, ruthlessness |
| **Wild**    | Growth, physicality, vigor        |
| **Wyrd**    | Chaos, curiosity, emotion         |


An Alignment rating is a **relationship**, not a virtue score. Three Shadow does not make you wicked; it means you and the Shadow have an understanding, and the GM is invited to remember it. Every person distributes exactly **10 permanent Alignment dots** across the five planes. A rating can be 0: every person contains every nature, but not every nature answers strongly enough to be measured.

Record both the permanent rating and the currently **available reserve**, such as **Heavens 3/3**. At the beginning of every surge your side takes, restore each available reserve to its permanent rating, except for dots tied up in an ongoing stance, bond, or primed or running device. Using a reserve dot never lowers the permanent rating used for Augments, Invocations, or Alignment saves.

Alignment ratings and reserves govern four things.

**Power requirements (use reserve dots).** Every power names an Alignment requirement and tells you what happens to those reserve dots. Magic and Psionics spend them. Legend may spend them on an exploit or commit them to a stance. Occultism stakes them in a bond. A Superscience device makes its operator spend them for a one-use function or commit them for a persistent or primed function. None of these uses changes permanent Alignment ratings.

Outside a structured encounter, treat uncommitted reserve dots as full at the beginning of each meaningful exchange. Commitments and stakes persist. Dots released outside surge structure become available at the beginning of the next scene. If repeated use, pursuit, or another time pressure makes reserve order matter, enter surge structure. Forgetting, Breaches, Overruns, consumed inputs, and device failures persist whether or not Momentum is being tracked.

**Augment (add dice).** When a Skill roll genuinely expresses an Alignment’s nature, add any number of dice up to that Alignment’s rating. The fiction must run through the plane’s nature, and the GM arbitrates. Augmenting invites the plane: the GM may attach a complication in proportion to how many dice you used — three dice of Shadow in a lie is a whisper; eight is a partnership.

**Invoke (act outright).** When you set skill aside and act *through* the plane itself, you do not roll. You receive **automatic successes equal to your rating** — and it **always** costs you something, or causes another problem, as the GM determines. This is the game’s central bargain, meant to be felt every time: the planes are reliable in a way training never is, and they are never free, because you are drawing on something with its own nature and its own accounts.

The cost should suit the plane, and a GM in need of the right complication can reach for its character:

- **Heavens** collects in *obligation*: the mercy you must now extend, the rule you must now keep, the witness you must now be. Its power arrives with its limits attached — there are things it will not let you do *next*.

- **Machine** collects in *literalism*: the solution works exactly as specified and not one inch further; the time was real time; the logic that carried you now binds you — this plan cannot be improvised away from.

- **Shadow** collects in *trust*: someone saw, or suspects, or was spent; the ruthless path forecloses the honest one; and the Shadow’s oldest surcharge — what worked once now looks like the obvious tool for everything.

- **Wild** collects in *excess*: the vigor overshoots — collateral, noise, appetite, growth where you wanted none; the body remembers being let off the leash, and wants it again.

- **Wyrd** collects in *strangeness*: the emotion escapes its handler; the curiosity opens a second door; something noticed the ripple in the dreaming, and tonight your dreams are shared property.

**Alignment saves.** When an effect targets you through a plane’s nature, roll dice equal to your rating in that Alignment; each 4+ is a success. Meet the effect’s required successes. If an effect simply says **save**, use the Alignment that best matches its source and require **1 success** unless the effect states otherwise. Saves are forced, so they never incur an augment or invocation price.

## Momentum and the Flow of an Encounter

### Sides

When a situation turns into an **encounter**, the GM declares the **sides** — usually the party and the opposition. Sides alternate **surges**; beginning a surge is also called **taking the Momentum**. The GM decides who surges first, usually the side that created the advantage. One full cycle in which every side surges is a **round**. The whole encounter is a **scene** for effects that recharge “per scene.” Characters take actions during their side’s surge; characters do not have separate activations.

### The Surge

Whenever a side surges:

1.  **Refresh Alignment reserves.** Every character on the side restores the available dots in each Alignment to their permanent rating, less any dots currently committed or staked.

2.  **Start-of-surge effects.** Resolve Bleeding, Burning, Poisoned, and other effects that trigger at the start of this side’s surge.

3.  **Roll Momentum.** Roll **1d6**, add bonus Momentum, then add any effect that explicitly banked Momentum for this surge.

4.  **Spend.** The side spends Momentum on its characters’ actions in any order. A character may act more than once unless an effect says otherwise.

5.  **End the surge.** Resolve end-of-surge effects. Unspent Momentum is lost; it never carries between surges unless an effect explicitly says it does.

The d6 is the game’s weather. A surge of 6 is a side moving like one animal; a surge of 1 is the terrible moment when only one thing can be done and the side must agree, fast, on what.

### Actions and Costs

Unless stated otherwise, an action costs **1 Momentum**:

- **Move** — up to your **Move** rating in inches.

- **Strike or Shoot** — an attack (below).

- **Skill action** — any other Skill roll: grapple, perceive, persuade, disable, aid.

- **Invoke** — an Alignment action, automatic successes and price included. (Augmenting a Skill roll is not a separate action; it rides the roll it sharpens.)

- **Use a power** — follow its source procedure and printed activation. An encounter-scale activation costs 1 Momentum unless it is a Reaction, Free, a Legend exploit that rides another action, or an effect that says otherwise. Superscience principles are long actions measured in hours; using a completed device has its own operating action.

**Long actions** cost more — the GM prices them (hauling the gate shut: 2; rigging the charge properly: 3) — and a side may pay across multiple surges if the work can pause. **Free actions** cost nothing, but each character gets only one of each declared free effect per trigger; “free” never means infinitely repeatable.

**Opposed actions.** When two characters directly contest one another, both roll the relevant Skill. Higher successes wins; on a tie, the existing situation holds. An active character must exceed a passive defender to change the situation.

**Aid.** Spend 1 Momentum and describe relevant help. If success is uncertain, roll an appropriate Skill requiring 1 success. The aided character gains +1 die on their next related roll. At most two helpers can aid one roll. When a wound says it requires **aid**, an adjacent helper instead spends 1 Momentum and performs the stated physical assistance; no roll is required unless the circumstances make access uncertain.

### Combat: Strike, Shoot, Defend

Attacks use a weapon’s **Attack Dice** and the attacker’s **Strike** or **Shoot target number**:


**Weapon dice − Defend; each die meeting Strike or Shoot is a hit.**


Take the weapon’s Attack Dice, subtract the target’s Defend (minimum 1 die), and roll. Each die meeting or exceeding **Strike** for melee or **Shoot** for ranged is a hit. Apply the weapon’s To-Hit modifier to that target number: negative is better, positive is worse. Resolve every hit separately, in an order chosen by the attacker: add the target’s existing-wound bonus to the weapon’s Damage, determine severity, roll Armor and Negation, then roll the wound if neither save stops it. Later hits benefit from wounds inflicted by earlier hits. See *Combat and Wounds* for the complete procedure.

## Power Sources

A power is a reusable capability recorded on the character sheet. There is no hand, draw pile, discard pile, reshuffle, or duplicate copy. The five sources share PL, level gates, and Alignment, but they do not share one activation mechanic.

Every entry lists:

- **Power Level (PL), from 0 to 10.** Each PL above 0 unlocks at character level **(2 × PL) − 1**: PL 1 at levels 1–2 through PL 10 at levels 19–20. PL measures complexity and narrative reach; it does not set a universal numerical bonus.
- **Alignment.** A sourced entry's listed dots total its PL. The source says whether those reserve dots are spent, committed, staked, or carried into a device's Operating Requirement rather than assuming one universal payment.
- **Activation and effect.** These state the action, target, result, duration, and release condition.
- **Source risk.** This is forgetting for Magic, breach for Occultism, Overrun for Psionics, or a printed flaw or failure condition for a fabricated device.

You cannot partly pay or tie up an Alignment cost. Substitution changes which reserve supplies a dot only when an effect explicitly permits it. Using reserve dots is not an Augment or Invocation and creates no planar complication unless the entry says otherwise.

| Source | Classes | Known unit | Core procedure |
|:--|:--|:--|:--|
| **Legend** | Captain, Drifter | Stance or exploit | Commit dots to maintain a stance; spend dots to improve a core action. |
| **Magic** | Arcanist, Witness | Spell | Spend dots, resolve the spell, then risk forgetting it for the day. |
| **Occultism** | Dynast, Shapeshifter | Dynast patron gift or Shapeshifter form contract/clause | Stake dots in a creature bond and obey its terms. |
| **Psionics** | Bounty Hunter, Mentalist | Broad psychic technique | Spend dots, choose Push, and roll d100 for strength and control. |
| **Superscience** | Sawbone, Tinkerer | Construction principle | Spend hours fabricating a device; its operator spends or commits Alignment. |

The complete procedures, authoring formats, and discipline lists are in [Power Sources and Disciplines](../powers/disciplines.md).

### Legend Procedure

A **Stance** commits its listed reserve dots while maintained. Committed dots cannot refresh or pay another cost. Maintain only one stance unless an entry says otherwise. When the stance ends, its dots become eligible to refresh when the character's side next takes Momentum; they do not return immediately.

An **exploit** spends its listed dots and modifies a core action such as Strike, Shoot, Move, Defend, or Aid. The core action is part of the exploit rather than a second Momentum cost.

A one-use exploit affects only its action or round unless it says otherwise. A stance grants its benefits continuously while its dots remain committed. A PL 10 stance consumes all ten Alignment dots and must provide extraordinary, encounter-defining benefits worthy of that total commitment.

### Magic Procedure

Spend the spell's listed dots, meet or ignore its special **Observances**, and resolve it. Afterward roll d100. On a result equal to or below **5 × the spell's PL**, forget that spell until the next daily recovery. PL 0 spells have no ordinary forget chance.

An ignored Observance never makes the spell fizzle. The spell technically accomplishes its stated result, but the GM adds a proportional **Twist** arising from the omitted material, time, permission, focus, or condition.

An Arcanist's spellcasting deposits literal, measurable matter and energy in their body as **arcane mass**. The burden is represented by their class profile and inability to benefit from worn Armor; do not track a second mass resource. A Witness's higher force bears that mass instead, so Witnesses may use Armor normally. Both classes still make the forget roll.

### Occultism Procedure

An occultist records creatures and contracts, not an ordinary list of self-contained powers. Stake the contract's Bond dots, which total its PL, to assume a Shapeshifter form or make a Dynast patron's learned gifts available. The dots remain unavailable until the bond is released. Violating the Terms triggers the contract's printed Breach.

Shapeshifters contract relatively weak creatures and become them through compact form profiles. Dynasts receive patron access as a class feature, then spend source selections on individual gifts from that patron's list. A new patron found in play opens another list. Every learned gift leaves a permanent physical Manifestation on the Dynast, even when the patron is inactive or the gift is suppressed. A borrowed gift follows its native procedure; matching dots in the active patron Bond count toward its Alignment footprint, so only the unmatched remainder uses the borrowed source's verb.

### Psionics Procedure

Spend the technique's dots, choose a **Push from 0 up to the character's level (maximum 20)**, then roll **d100 + Push**.

| Total | Manifestation |
|---:|:--|
| 1–24 | Trace |
| 25–49 | Lesser |
| 50–74 | Full |
| 75–89 | Greater |
| 90–99 | Apex |
| exactly 100 | Perfect |
| over 100 | Overrun |

Every controlled result manifests; getting closer to 100 makes it stronger. On an Overrun, the technique manifests at Perfect magnitude or greater, but its printed clause gives the GM control over some combination of target, area, path, duration, or cessation. Choose Push before rolling.

The Bounty Hunter's **Mind Weapon** is a class feature, not a psychic technique. It consumes no selection or Alignment and makes no d100 roll; anti-psychic suppression can still dismiss it. Mentalists receive no automatic mind weapon and must use a learned technique such as Force Construct to create a weapon-like effect.

### Superscience Procedure

A principle is a construction operation, not an instant encounter effect. Its entry lists permanent Alignment requirements, physical inputs, hours of work, scale, output, and limits. The maker meets those requirements, provides the inputs, and applies each required principle for the listed hours; construction does not spend or commit reserve dots. A completed device records each function's operating action, Operating Requirement, one-use or persistent duration, fuel or charges, maintenance, and failure condition.

The operator must possess and supply the listed dots. A one-use function spends them and affects only its use or current round. A persistent function commits them while it runs; a primed trap commits them until it triggers or is disarmed. The maker's Alignment is not tied to a finished device, and another person can use it if they meet its Operating Requirement.

### Timing and Common Tags

| Tag | Rule |
|:--|:--|
| **Reaction** | Use only when its trigger occurs, even during another side's surge. It costs no Momentum, but all source costs still apply. |
| **Free** | Costs no Momentum. All other costs and frequency limits still apply. |
| **Stance** | Commit its listed dots and maintain its effect until released, disabled, or replaced. Maintain only one by default. |
| **Once per surge** | Use at most once during any one side's surge; it refreshes when the next side begins its surge. |
| **Once per round** | Use at most once until every side has surged since the effect was used. |
| **Once per scene** | Use at most once in an encounter; it refreshes when the next encounter begins. |
| **Once per session** | Use at most once in a play session; it refreshes when the next session begins. |
| **Consumable / Consumable ×N** | The device or preparation supplies one physical use, or N uses when numbered. Mark one when operated. Uses return only when the fiction permits restocking or rebuilding. |
| **Troop** | Deploys a simple allied unit using the troop rules in *Combat and Wounds*. |
| **Prepared** | Establishes a plausible earlier preparation or flashback. It cannot contradict an established fact. |
| **Sacrifice** | Offers a stronger effect when the player accepts the specific obligation, exposure, or harm printed on the entry. |
| **Psychic** | A psionic effect. Anti-psychic wards can suppress it; damaging Psychic effects deal Wyrd unless they name another type. |
| **Training / Standing / Access** | Faction tags. Training remains known after leaving. Standing works only while the reputation survives. Access becomes unavailable when the institutional relationship ends. |

**Timing rule.** If effects conflict, a specific entry overrides a general rule. If two effects resolve at the same time, the side whose surge it is chooses the order for its effects, then the other side chooses for theirs. No single trigger can cause the same named effect more than once.

**Duration language.** “This surge” lasts until the current side ends its surge. “Until your side's next surge” ends immediately before that surge's start-of-surge effects. “This round” and “for one round” are shorthand for that same full-cycle duration, even when used as a Reaction during another side's surge. “Once per round” refreshes only after every side has surged since the effect was used. The GM anchors each round to the established side order; “at the end of the round” resolves after the last side in that order finishes its surge.

**What “all rolls” means.** A bonus or penalty to all rolls modifies Skill pools, Attack Dice, Alignment-save pools, Magic forget rolls, and Psionic manifestation rolls only when it explicitly names the latter two. It never adds dice to Armor, Negation, Momentum, wound location, or wound effect rolls unless it names them explicitly.

**Stacking.** Ongoing numerical modifiers from different named sources stack. Repeating the same named effect refreshes its duration but does not add its value again unless it explicitly says it is cumulative. Immediate effects such as Damage and Recovery can resolve repeatedly. Penalties from separately recorded wounds are separate sources and stack.

### Starting Entries and Learning More

Each source organizes its entries into coherent **lists**: Legend traditions, spell traditions, patron gift lists and form relationships, Psionic disciplines, Superscience fields, and equivalent faction or species curricula. Your class lists begin **primary**. A list first opened through play is normally **adjacent** until deeper training makes it primary.

**Power Levels are gated by character level.** PL 0 is available from 1st level. Each PL above 0 unlocks at character level **(2 × PL) − 1**. No route — class list, cross-training, faction, species, or the world — reaches past this gate. Learning from an adjacent list treats the entry as one PL higher for eligibility; its printed PL, Alignment footprint, effect, and source procedure do not change.

| PL | Level | | PL | Level |
|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--:|
| 0–1 | 1st | | 6 | 11th |
| 2 | 3rd | | 7 | 13th |
| 3 | 5th | | 8 | 15th |
| 4 | 7th | | 9 | 17th |
| 5 | 9th | | 10 | 19th |

This curve is the game's power curve, and it names four bands of play. **Levels 1–2** are armed and competent professionals. **Levels 3–6** are action-movie heroes. **Levels 7–12** are clearly superhuman, past what conventional human enemies can answer. **Levels 13–20** are city-ending threats. A PL is a statement of scale within that arc: a PL 5 working is the peak of what heroes do, a PL 8 working is why the superhuman are spoken of carefully, and PL 9–10 are the reason certain names are not said at all.

**PL 0 entries are reliable fundamentals.** They require no Alignment dots and do not deplete reserves. They still obey their source procedure, printed action, inputs, contract, and frequency; outside a structured encounter, use them as often as the fiction permits.

**At creation**, learn the number of class source entries shown in *Character Creation* from your primary lists' PL 0 and PL 1 entries, plus **one PL 0–1 species entry** and **one PL 0–1 faction entry** (Unaffiliated characters choose the latter from the Unaffiliated list).

**Every level thereafter: +1 class source selection**, chosen from a primary or adjacent list at an unlocked PL. This grant is deliberately lean. It is the practice floor—what discipline and repetition alone yield—and it is not where a great practitioner's breadth comes from. PL 9–10 entries are the exception: they require a specific world reward rather than an automatic level selection.

**The world teaches the rest.** Most of what a seasoned practitioner knows was never issued by their calling; it was learned—from teachers and rivals, reliquary texts, factions, species elders, singular sites, captured techniques, sovereign creatures, patrons, and the housed dead. World-learning has **no numeric cap**. It is limited by the level gate, by permanent Alignment, by source access, and by what the world charges. To learn an entry from the world you need:

1. **A source.** Someone or something that holds the entry: a living teacher, text or reliquary, master observed at length, faction curriculum, site, creature, patron, or reproducible body of research.
2. **An opening.** The first entry from a list not yet available requires a real initiation: an apprenticeship accepted, oath sworn, grimoire stolen *and mastered*, creature relationship formed, patron bargain struck, or laboratory program completed. This normally opens the list as adjacent. A completed apprenticeship arc may open it as primary or raise a long-practiced adjacent list to primary. A character's spread of lists is their biography.
3. **Time.** Taught properly: **days equal to the entry's PL** (minimum one) of instruction and practice. Self-taught from a text or observation: **twice that**, plus an appropriate **Study, Folklore, or Religion test** needing successes equal to half the PL, rounded up—one attempt per downtime, with further study permitting retries. Contract negotiations and Superscience construction may demand additional fictional time.
4. **The price.** Whatever the source charges: Rings, favors, oaths, service, secrecy, blood. Use the scale below as guidance for fictional weight, not a price list.

| PL | Typical way it enters the character's life |
|:--:|:--|
| 0–1 | A lesson, observation, cultural practice, field manual, or one meaningful scene with a teacher |
| 2–3 | Focused downtime, repeated practice, a favor, a captured technique, or a test with consequences |
| 4–5 | A rare mentor, dangerous procedure, faction initiation, significant hunt, or dedicated adventure |
| 6–8 | A major campaign arc, singular site or creature, forbidden archive, transformation, or institution's deepest trust |
| 9–10 | A campaign-defining discovery or relationship that changes how the character and world understand one another |

World-learned entries never replace something known, and ordinary instruction normally culminates at PL 6–8. **PL 9–10 entries are only ever learned from the world**—the name taken from a sovereign, the rite completed at a singular site, the invention no school can repeat.

**What this adds up to.** A 13th-level Arcanist who chased knowledge might hold five spells from creation, twelve level-granted selections, and fifteen to twenty-five entries learned from the world—a grimoire of several dozen, most with a story attached. Two characters of the same class and level should differ mainly in *where they have been*.

**Building new powers.** The printed lists are examples, not the boundary of the world. When play teaches a power that does not exist yet, give it a PL from 0–10, assign requirements whose dots total that PL, and make every required Alignment part of how the power works — a mixed requirement is a statement of nature, not merely a balance cost. As a guideline, the largest single-Alignment dot in a requirement should be about **half its PL, rounded up**, so that deep powers remain payable by characters whose ten dots are bent, not broken, toward two planes.

Choose the source first, then write the new entry using that source's format and risk. Its Alignment footprint must total its PL, and every required Alignment must express part of the act's nature.

**Faction changes.** Training entries remain known. A Standing entry remains usable only if the reputation behind it survives. An Access entry becomes unavailable when the institutional relationship ends, though it remains recorded and can return if access is restored. Joining a faction grants no entries automatically; each is learned when the relevant training, standing, or access is actually earned.

## Identity, Deeds, and Possessions

The sheet’s remaining boxes are not bookkeeping; they are prompts the game will pull on.

**Demeanor, Drive, Reputation.** Three short lines: how you present, what moves you, and what the world says about you — written by the player, revised in play. Demeanor is yours; Drive is yours; **Reputation is not** — it is the word, carried mouth to mouth through the factors’ web and the station taverns, and the GM owns its updates. When your Reputation and your Demeanor disagree, that is not an error on the sheet. That is a campaign.

**Nickname / Wanted-Poster Name.** The name the frontier uses when it is not being polite — and, should it come to that, the name at the head of the writ. Players may leave it blank at creation. It rarely stays blank.

**Deeds.** Six slots hold ranked specializations and signature abilities. Deeds are earned through advancement or awarded for deeds done, as described in *Deeds*. Record the event beside the mechanical name: *Bulwark II — Held at Redwater*.

**Coins and Funds.** **Coins** are Rings in hand. **Funds** are banked credit, Company scrip, letters of draw, and other money whose value depends on an issuer and relationship. Equipment lists a Ring price for ordinary purchases and marks purchases requiring Funds with **(F)**.

## An Exchange of Play

*The party — Wren (Warden Shapeshifter), Ozanne (Lions Witness), Pike (Guild Tinkerer, Coilwright-trained) — is cornered at a relay post by three of the Rust Belt’s organized things. Two sides. The GM gives the enemy the first surge; it spends 4 Momentum putting one thing on the roof and two at the door.*

**GM:** Your side takes the Momentum. Refresh Alignment reserves, then roll it.

**Ozanne:** *(rolls)* A 5. Okay — Pike, the door?

**Pike:** One Momentum: I prime the **Capacitor Trap** I built at camp. I commit Machine 1 and Wild 1 to its Operating Requirement and clamp its leads to the door bar; the first thing through takes Damage 3 Electricity.

**GM:** The bar starts humming. Four Momentum left.

**Wren:** One for me: I open my contract with Grey-Wing and stake Wild 1 to take her hawk form. One more to **move** out the smoke-hole, six inches up. Eyes on the roof.

**GM:** Perceive — it’s dark and the thing is patient, so I want **two successes**.

**Wren:** Perceive 4, and I’ll augment with **Wild** — the hawk’s eyes are exactly what Wild means. Wild 3, so seven dice. *(rolls: 5, 4, 2, 6, 3, 4, 1)* Four successes — two over.

**GM:** You leaned on the Wild lightly, so it just purrs — this time. Two successes see it; your margin buys more: it hasn’t seen *you*, and it isn’t watching the post at all. It’s watching the *other two*. Like a sergeant.

**Ozanne:** Two Momentum left. I spend one — and not on a Skill. It’s forty souls behind me and a door. I **invoke Heavens**. Rating 4: four automatic successes to hold this doorway, and the prayer is honest.

**GM:** Four successes: you will hold, this round, whatever comes. And the Heavens collect: the thing at the door is *wounded already* — something got to it before you — and now that you have seen that, killing it has stopped being simple for you. One Momentum left?

**Ozanne:** Held. We keep it. —

**GM:** It’s lost — spin doesn’t bank. Enemy side surges...

*Note what the exchange shows: the side spent as one organism; Pike built the device beforehand but committed his own Alignment only when he primed it; Wren's staked dot remained unavailable while she wore the form; the augment added dice and drew only a warning, while the invocation bought certainty and trouble; margin became fiction; and the unspent Momentum died with the surge.*

## Character Creation (Summary)

1.  **Choose your species** — one of the seventeen peoples. Take its trait and learn one PL 0 or PL 1 species entry.

2.  **Choose your class and power source** — note its stat line, Signature Skill, and class features; learn the class's listed number of PL 0 or PL 1 source entries.

3.  **Choose your faction** — or none. Learn one PL 0 or PL 1 entry from that faction or the Unaffiliated list.

4.  **Rate your Skills** — distribute 20 points across the twelve, maximum 4 before your class adds +1 to its Signature Skill.

5.  **Rate your Alignments** — distribute exactly 10 permanent dots across the five planes, maximum 3 in any one.

6.  **Write your Identity** — Demeanor, Drive, and a Reputation the GM co-signs. Leave the wanted-poster line blank if you dare.

7.  **Take your class kit** — record weapon profiles, Armor, Negation, Move, thresholds, and 10 Rings.

8.  **Record powers and reserves** — copy each known stance, exploit, spell, contract, psychic technique, principle, trait, and device. Write every Alignment as available/permanent and begin with every reserve full; devices use their operator's dots only when run or primed.

## Rules Glossary

| Term | Meaning |
|:--|:--|
| **Adjacent** | Bases touch or are within 1". |
| **Within N inches** | Measure shortest edge to edge; the boundary counts. Unless text says “you and allies,” **ally** does not include the acting character. |
| **Encounter / scene** | The entire structured conflict. These words are interchangeable in recharge text. |
| **Surge** | One side's turn to refresh uncommitted Alignment reserves, resolve statuses, roll Momentum, and act. |
| **Round** | One surge from every side. “Once per round” refreshes when your side next begins a surge after every side has acted. |
| **Action** | Something paid for with Momentum. A character may take multiple actions in one surge. |
| **Attack Die** | One die in a Strike or Shoot attack pool. It is not a Skill die. |
| **Hit** | An attack die that meets the applicable target number. It is resolved into a possible wound. |
| **Damage** | The number compared with wound thresholds; it is not a pool of health. |
| **Physical roll** | An Athletics, Knack, or Nimbleness roll, or a Strike or Shoot attack. It does not include Armor, Negation, or other unlisted dice. |
| **Light item** | A one-handed object no bulkier than a revolver, knife, small tool, or similarly carried item. |
| **Condition / status** | An ongoing rule effect. A wound remains after its transient effect is cleared. |
| **Hidden / unseen** | Other creatures do not know your exact position and cannot target you directly. They may locate you with Perceive opposed by the hiding effect or your Nimbleness; attacking or entering clear view normally reveals you. |
| **Large** | A bodily scale tag used by effects, carrying, and the fiction. It grants no automatic attack, Damage, or Defend bonus beyond the trait that provides it. |
| **Difficult ground** | Each 1" crossed costs 2" of Move. Multiple sources do not multiply this cost. |
| **Rounding** | Round fractions down unless a rule says otherwise. Movement may be measured to the nearest half inch. |
| **Once per scene** | Refreshes at the beginning of the next encounter. |
| **Once per session** | Refreshes at the beginning of the next play session. |

**Areas.** A **burst N** is a radius of N inches around a point. A **line N** is N inches long and 1 inch wide unless stated otherwise. A **cone N** extends N inches and is N inches wide at its far edge. A creature whose base is touched by an area is in it. Areas require line of effect unless a power says otherwise.

## Design Notes (for the table, not the fiction)

**Momentum is a conversation.** The d6 forces the side to negotiate every surge — who needs it, who can wait, whether the long action is worth starting — and that negotiation *is* the party, mechanically enacted.

**Known sources make identity procedural.** What a character has learned remains part of them, but each source makes access feel different: Legend asks what they will maintain, Magic what they may lose for the day, Occultism whom they owe, Psionics how hard they dare Push, and Superscience what they prepared and who can operate it. The ten-dot reserve makes those relationships tactical without flattening them into one cost.

**Two prices, one theology.** Skills are safe and uncertain. Augments are sharp and *noticed*. Invocations are certain and *owed*. That ladder — from training, through borrowing, to conducting — is the setting’s cosmology expressed as a decision players make several times a session, and GMs should keep the prices specific, planar, and cumulative. A character who leans on the Shadow all campaign should end the campaign with the Shadow’s opinion of them; and the same, more comfortingly and no less bindingly, for the Heavens.
