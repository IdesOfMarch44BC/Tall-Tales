# Deeds

## What a Deed Is

The six lines under **Deeds** on your sheet are not a shopping list. They are the parts of your legend that have hardened into fact.

A Deed is a specialization or signature ability, earned in play and ranked **I through IV**. Each deed occupies one of your six slots at any rank — you write *Deadeye III* on the line, not three separate entries — so a career holds at most six legends, and which six is as much a portrait as your Demeanor. Ranks are upgrades in place: the deed deepens; it does not multiply.

**Earning deeds.** Gain one deed rank at every even character level: take a new deed at rank I or increase a deed you hold by one rank. The GM may also award a rank when the table agrees the character just did the thing. Rank III requires level 5+; rank IV requires level 9+. No deed can gain more than one rank from the same event.

**Stacking.** Deed bonuses stack with species traits, powers, and Alignment augments. You cannot apply the same deed to a single roll twice. When two effects set the same value rather than modifying it, use the more favorable value; do not combine them.

**Reading the ranks.** Every deed follows the same grammar. **Rank I** is a specialization — a die, an edge, a reliability, in the deed’s exact territory. **Rank II** widens it. **Rank III** is a signature: the active ability people describe when they describe you. **Rank IV** is a legend — usually once per session — the thing the stories exaggerate, except they don’t.

## Deeds of Arms

### Deadeye

*Earned by the shot nobody believed until they walked over and looked.*

I.  
Once per surge, reroll one missed die on a Shoot attack.

II\.  
Your Shoot attacks ignore 1 point of the target’s Defend.

III\.  
**Dead to Rights** (1 Momentum, once per scene): declare before rolling — Defend cannot reduce this Shoot attack below half the weapon’s Attack Dice, rounded up.

IV\.  
Once per session, after rolling a Shoot attack, every die counts as a hit.

### Duelist

*Earned by winning a fight you started by standing still.*

I.  
Once per surge, reroll one missed die on a Strike attack.

II\.  
Your Strike attacks ignore 1 point of the target’s Defend.

III\.  
**Riposte** (free, once per round): when an adjacent enemy attacks you and scores no hits, immediately make a Strike attack against them at no Momentum cost.

IV\.  
Once per session, one Strike attack ignores Defend and the Armor save; Negation still applies.

### Bulwark

*Earned by being the reason the count of the dead stopped where it did.*

I.  
+1 Defend during any round in which you have not moved.

II\.  
Allies adjacent to you gain +1 Defend.

III\.  
**Interpose** (free, once per round): when an attack targets an adjacent ally, you become its target instead.

IV\.  
Once per session, reduce one attack against you or an adjacent ally to zero hits after the roll.

### Deathproof

*Earned by being carried off the field, and back onto the next one.*

I.  
+1 Moderate threshold.

II\.  
+1 Major threshold, and gain +1 die on Alignment saves while carrying a major wound.

III\.  
Once per scene, when a wound would make you Dying, reduce that wound one severity; you remain Disabled until cleared normally.

IV\.  
Once per session, change a Death result against you to Dying, then immediately stabilize. You remain prone and visibly annoyed.

## Deeds of Tempo

### Quickdraw

*Earned by clearing leather while the other party was still deciding.*

I.  
Once per scene, when an enemy targets you, use one PL 1 or lower power you know as a Reaction before their action. Pay its Alignment requirement but no Momentum.

II\.  
Twice per scene.

III\.  
Your reaction may instead be a Shoot attack or a PL 3 or lower power you know.

IV\.  
**The Standoff** (once per session): when an enemy declares an attack against you, resolve a full Shoot attack against them first. If it drops them, their attack never happens.

### Pacesetter

*Earned by being, verifiably, the reason the whole side moved like that.*

I.  
Once per scene, add +1 to your side’s Momentum roll.

II\.  
Your side’s Momentum rolls of 1 count as 2.

III\.  
Your side gains +1 bonus Momentum on every surge you are conscious for.

IV\.  
Once per session, after your side rolls Momentum, set the die to 6.

### Ace in the Hole

*Earned by producing, at the fatal moment, exactly the technique nobody knew you had.*

I.  
Once per scene, use one PL 1 or lower power from your class lists that you do not know. Pay its requirement normally; this does not teach it to you.

II\.  
The borrowed class power may be PL 3 or lower.

III\.  
Instead choose any class, species, faction, or Unaffiliated power with PL no higher than half your character level rounded up, provided the fiction gives you some plausible exposure to it.

IV\.  
Once per session, use the power chosen through this deed without paying its Alignment requirement.

### Old Reliable

*Earned by doing the same beautiful thing so often it acquired your name.*

I.  
Choose one PL 1 or higher power you know as your *reliable*. The first time you use it each surge, reduce one required dot of your choice.

II\.  
Reduce two required dots instead of one.

III\.  
Once per scene, using the reliable costs no Momentum; pay its reduced Alignment requirement normally.

IV\.  
Once per session, use the reliable without paying Momentum or its Alignment requirement.

## Deeds of Wits

### Sharp Eye

*Earned by seeing the thing whose whole job was not being seen.*

I.  
+1 die on Perceive.

II\.  
Once per scene, after examining a place, person, or object, ask the GM one direct question about it and receive a true answer.

III\.  
You notice as though searching: within 20", hidden things, ambushes, and wrongness prompt a Perceive roll even when you are not actively looking.

IV\.  
**I Already Noticed** (once per session): retroactively declare that you clocked a detail earlier in the scene — the exit, the second knife, the face in the crowd. The GM makes it true within reason.

### Silver Tongue

*Earned by walking out of a room that everyone agreed you would be carried out of.*

I.  
+1 die on Convince and Deceive.

II\.  
Once per scene, reroll a failed social roll by genuinely changing your approach.

III\.  
**Parley** (1 Momentum, once per scene): one sapient enemy who can hear you will not act against you or your allies until your side’s next surge, or until any of you acts against them.

IV\.  
Once per session, one claim you make is believed by its audience until hard evidence contradicts it.

### Trailwise

*Earned by bringing everyone home through country that had other plans.*

I.  
+1 die on Survive.

II\.  
Companions traveling with you may use your Survive against travel hazards, weather, and forage.

III\.  
Sign speaks to you: automatically learn number, kind, gait, burden, and age from any trail you examine.

IV\.  
**There’s Always a Way** (once per session): declare a plausible route feature — a ford, a pass, a culvert under the line — and the GM places it within reach.

### Learned

*Earned by knowing the answer in the field, under fire, without the library.*

I.  
+1 die on one of Study, Folklore, or Religion (chosen when this deed is earned).

II\.  
Once per scene, recall a relevant, useful, true fact from your discipline — the GM supplies it.

III\.  
With time and materials, your research counts as two ranks higher, and takes half the time.

IV\.  
**The Briefing** (once per session): hold forth on a named threat or situation for a minute; your whole side gains +1 die on rolls against it for the rest of the scene.

## Deeds of the Planes

### Planewise

*Earned by standing in the current and not being carried.*

I.  
+1 die on all Alignment saves.

II\.  
Choose one plane: gain 1 automatic success on saves against it.

III\.  
Once per scene, reroll a failed Alignment save entirely.

IV\.  
Once per session, automatically succeed at one Alignment save, margin equal to your rating.

### Even Keel

*Earned by borrowing from all five natures and dying in debt to none.*

I.  
When you augment a Skill roll with 2 or fewer Alignment dice, the plane never complicates.

II\.  
+1 die whenever you augment (the plane leans in).

III\.  
Once per scene, augment a Skill roll *after* seeing its result.

IV\.  
Once per session, augment one roll with two different planes at once, both at full rating.

### Deep Accounts

*Earned by paying the planes’ prices in full, on time, with your eyes open.*

I.  
Once per scene, when you invoke an Alignment, gain +1 automatic success.

II\.  
Once per scene, when you invoke, defer the price: the complication arrives later this session, at a moment you choose (its content remains the GM’s).

III\.  
**Name the Terms** (once per session): when you invoke, the GM offers two different prices; you choose which you pay.

IV\.  
Choose one plane: your rating counts as 2 higher (max 10) when invoking it. Its prices scale accordingly. The plane knows your name.

### Iron in the Blood

*Earned by walking out of a thin place that kept everyone else.*

I.  
+1 die on Alignment saves against Wild and Wyrd effects.

II\.  
Your attacks count as cold iron and gain AP 1 against Wild- and Wyrd-aligned creatures.

III\.  
Once per scene, when a Wild or Wyrd complication lands on an adjacent ally, draw it onto yourself instead.

IV\.  
**The Still Spot** (once per session, free): for one full round, supernatural effects of the Wild and the Wyrd are suppressed within 6" of you. Things that live in those planes find you *extremely rude*.

## Deeds of the Road

### Storied

*Earned by having the tale arrive in town three days before you did.*

I.  
+1 die on social rolls with anyone who has heard of you (the GM decides who has; your Reputation line decides what they heard).

II\.  
**An Old Acquaintance** (once per session): declare a contact in any settlement of meaningful size — a factor, a Rider, a hand you once helped. The GM gives them a name, a debt or a grudge, and a door.

III\.  
Your word is credit: once per session, secure goods, passage, or hospitality on Reputation alone, up to the scale of a serious favor. It goes on the Funds line, not the Coins line, and it is *remembered*.

IV\.  
**Precede Yourself** (once per session): when you enter a scene, your legend enters first — the GM shifts every named NPC’s disposition one step (toward awe, fear, or welcome; your Reputation decides which).

### Steady Hands

*Earned by fixing, in the dark, under fire, the thing the manual said to replace.*

I.  
+1 die on Knack.

II\.  
**Jury-Rig** (once per scene): a repair, disable, or rig that would be a long action costs 1 Momentum instead. It holds exactly as long as it needs to.

III\.  
Your gear never jams, fouls, or fails at the worst moment, and improvised tools count as proper ones in your hands.

IV\.  
**Exactly the Tool** (once per session): produce from your kit precisely the mundane item this moment requires, up to 5 Rings’ worth. You packed it. Obviously you packed it.

### Cat-Foot

*Earned by being reported dead, absent, or never there at all — while present.*

I.  
+1 die on Nimbleness rolls to move unseen or unheard.

II\.  
Moving quietly no longer slows you, and you may hide as part of a Move action.

III\.  
**Vanish** (1 Momentum, once per scene): if any cover or shadow exists, you are unseen; enemies must beat your Nimbleness with Perceive to target you.

IV\.  
**Never There** (once per session): when you are revealed, targeted, or cornered, reposition up to your Move in inches to a place you could plausibly have reached unseen.

### Long Haul

*Earned by arriving — late season, bad road, wrong weather — anyway.*

I.  
+1 die against exhaustion, forced march, and privation, and you may travel 2 hours longer per day. (Humans: yes, it stacks. Nobody outlasts you.)

II\.  
You can carry a second load — a companion’s pack, a companion — without penalty.

III\.  
Your whole party treats travel conditions as one step gentler while you set the pace.

IV\.  
**In Time** (once per session): retroactively, you pushed through — the night, the pass, the flood — and you are where the story needs you at the moment it needs you there, winded and present.

## Table Notes

**Six slots is a life.** A veteran holds at most six deeds, and the choice of which legends to deepen versus which to begin is the advancement game: Deadeye IV is a different person from Deadeye II / Quickdraw II, and both are different from the six-at-rank-I generalist whom the frontier calls, with respect, *hard to plan around*.

**Award ranks out loud.** The best moment to grant a deed rank is thirty seconds after the table saw it earned — “write it down: that’s Bulwark II” — because a deed awarded at the table teaches everyone what the game celebrates. The even-level track exists so no one falls behind; the awards are where the system lives.

**Name the moment on the line.** The deed’s name is shared; its story is not. Players are encouraged to note the earning beside the rank — *Deadeye III, the Spur, 311* — because in this world, the Gorûn and the Ostrogar and the Book of the Seen are all correct about the same thing: what you have done is a stat, and it should be written where you were.
