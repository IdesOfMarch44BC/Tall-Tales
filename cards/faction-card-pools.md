# Faction Card Pools

Your faction cards are your standing, dealt out: the training the institution drilled into you (**Training** — yours forever), the person it made you to everyone else (**Standing** — converts if you leave), and the doors it opens (**Access** — which close behind the severed). \[PROPOSAL F1\] Read the tags: they are the answer, written in advance, to the question every campaign eventually asks — *what do you keep if you walk away?*

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Witnesses of the Lion

- **The Sigil Opens Doors** — 1 — L1 — Access — Any funded church: shelter, provisions, and backing for you and companions; +2 dice on social rolls with congregations. The Open Eye is a promise, and you are it.

- **Do Not Stop Seeing** — 1 — L1 — Training, Stance — +2 dice against fear, and no effect can make you look away, unsee, or abstract what is in front of you.

- **Keep the Vigil** — 1 — L1 — Training — After a hard scene, your side sits together and speaks honestly of what they saw: everyone heals 2 HP and clears one fear or despair effect. Pretense voids it, and the card knows.

- **The Circuit Knows** — 1 — L2 — Access — Ask the Riders’ web: one true piece of intelligence about any person, place, or pattern on a circuit — because a Rider noticed, three visits ago.

- **Answered from the Vapor** — 2 — L5 — One-Shot, Standing — Pray, badly and honestly, at real need: the GM sends aid proportionate to it — a true vision, a surge of grace (all allies heal 3), or, if the night is truly bad, something at the scene’s edge with the light steadying around it.

## Noble Houses

- **Recognized Blood** — 1 — L1 — Standing — Your name is in a book that cannot be counterfeited: +2 dice with any House, and your identity is provable anywhere the Concord’s word reaches.

- **The Demesne Stone** — 1 — L1 — Access — Within any allied demesne: sanctuary — writs stop at the stone, pursuit stalls, and the House’s intercession covers your party while you’re its guest.

- **Call the Sworn** — 1 — L2 — Access — Once per session, retainers answer: two sworn (Troops: HP 3, Strike 2, Defend 1) for a scene, or one specialist service (a medic, a notary’s clerk, a huntsman’s report).

- **Name-Cup Courtesy** — 1 — L2 — Standing — Invoke guest-right: a House-affiliated host must shelter your party this night and offer no harm beneath their roof. Both of you will remember which of you honored it better.

- **Intercession** — 2 — L5 — One-Shot, Access — The House formally stands between you and one institution: a writ suspended, a contract stayed, a pursuit called off — followed, in due course, by the House’s own accounting with *you*, which is not a card.

## Academic Enclaves

- **The Grey** — 1 — L1 — Standing — The credential that tells a nervous room what it is looking at: +2 dice with officials and scholars, and frightened communities treat you as the answer arriving, not the problem.

- **Protocol Training** — 1 — L1 — Training — Identify a supernatural effect with a Study roll; against effects you’ve identified, you and adjacent allies gain +2 dice on saves. Vellhame is why the drills exist.

- **The Informant Web** — 1 — L2 — Access — In any settlement: learn what the local informants know — the strange child, the cold room, the wrongness with a street address. The GM gives the true summary.

- **Registry Consultation** — 1 — L2 — Access — Query the case files: one documented answer about any supernatural phenomenon the Enclaves have ever recorded — instantly near a chartered hall, at correspondence speed otherwise.

- **The Attendance Owed** — 2 — L5 — One-Shot, Standing — Call in what the tradition owes you: an Enclave sends real help, once — a field Adept for a scene, the warding of a site, or the Office of Returns quietly taking one problem off your hands.

## Monastic Orders

- **The Gate Behind You** — 1 — L1 — Access — Any monastery of any Order: shelter, healing, and sanctuary for you and companions, absolute, fed from the first evening. The gate is open. It was always the oldest vow.

- **The Hours** — 1 — L1 — Training, Stance — The discipline, kept daily: +1 die against fear, deception, and mind-effects, and you wake instantly, completely, on cue.

- **Sit With It** — 1 — L2 — Training — Guide an ally through a quiet beat: clear one mental affliction, or grant +2 dice on their next save. Works on yourself, slower.

- **A Walker’s Introduction** — 1 — L2 — Access — Once per session, a walker or gate-friend of the tradition is within a day’s reach: a teaching, a message carried, or one plain favor, repaid in the tradition’s currency — which is more teaching.

- **The Examiners’ Seal** — 2 — L5 — One-Shot, Standing — Still Water vouches: one accusation, possession-claim, or question of mind-tampering concerning you or an ally is formally examined and settled — because they checked, and it is true, and everyone knows what their checking means.

## Landed Guilds

- **The Mark** — 1 — L1 — Standing — Your work carries a hall’s counter-stamp: +2 dice in commerce and negotiation over craft, and what you make sells at an honest price anywhere the Assay’s word runs.

- **Hall Rights** — 1 — L1 — Access — Any Guildhall: workshop and bench, materials at cost, a bed, and the notice board — the federation’s whole physical plant, open to your mark.

- **Journeyman’s Welcome** — 1 — L2 — Standing — Craft-kin everywhere: tradesfolk of any guild assist, gossip, and warn you, and with proper tools you gain +1 die on Knack and Study.

- **Case-Library Letter** — 1 — L2 — Access — Query the libraries: one validated-procedure answer — how to make it, fix it, neutralize it, and what it did to the last person who tried — instantly at a hall, by post otherwise.

- **The Table’s Weight** — 2 — L5 — One-Shot, Access — The federation leans, once, where you point: a supplier embargo, an Assay audit, a strike-hour — against any target of Guild-dependent scale. Sell to all, sworn to none; the Table will want this favor back.

## Mercenary Companies

- **On the Rolls** — 1 — L1 — Standing — Registered colors: +2 dice negotiating any paper, and the Convention’s protections — arbitration, parley custom, the honored surrender — apply to you and yours.

- **The Company Kit** — 1 — L1 — Access — Requisition against the contract: field gear, mounts, ammunition, and rations for an engagement’s duration, quartermaster’s glare included.

- **Read the Paper** — 1 — L2 — Training — Assess any contract, job, or standoff actuarially: the GM tells you the true risk, the coverage gaps, and who actually benefits. You cannot unlearn this. Most veterans have stopped trying.

- **Comrades in the Trade** — 1 — L2 — Standing — Once per session, colors you’ve served beside are near: intelligence, one night’s reinforcement, or a covered retreat — answered for, as you’d answer for yours.

- **The Incursion Pool** — 2 — L5 — One-Shot, Access — Declare a catastrophe of the scale the Pool exists for, with evidence: the industry answers — a funded relief column within days — and your name goes in the report, for better and worse.

## Trade Companies

- **Company Papers** — 1 — L1 — Access — Free passage on your Company’s lines, freight at cost, and the station clock’s certified time as your alibi.

- **The Factor’s Desk** — 1 — L1 — Access — In any town on the flow, the factor receives you: work, the local truth, and a small budget for problems — yours, if you’re the solution.

- **Read the Spread** — 1 — L2 — Training — Prices talk to you: from a chalkboard and an hour in the market, learn a settlement’s true condition — who’s solvent, who’s desperate, what’s about to break.

- **Expense Account** — 1 — L2 — Access — Draw operational Funds (GM-capped, scaled to your standing). It is audited, eventually, and the audit has a memory and a tone.

- **The Principal’s Ear** — 2 — L5 — One-Shot, Standing — One message reaches a principal directly, and is *read*: a warning heeded, a deal tabled, a door opened at the top of the register. What you said is now policy’s problem — and so, in a small permanent way, are you.

## Bounty Registries

- **The Seal-Tag** — 1 — L1 — Standing — Certified: +2 dice with lawfolk and officials, your testimony is evidence, and your captures are legal in every jurisdiction on your rings.

- **Board Privileges** — 1 — L1 — Access — Read writs before posting, reserve one against competitors, and pull the inquest file on any posted name.

- **Adjudicator’s Question** — 1 — L2 — Access — Once per session, a bench answers one question a Registry could know: names, priors, who posted what, and when the paper moved.

- **Escrow Draw** — 1 — L2 — Access — Advance against active paper: pursuit Funds now, settled at collection — and the bench’s arithmetic if there is no collection.

- **Exchange Reciprocity** — 2 — L5 — One-Shot, Standing — Invoke the full Exchange: a name flagged at every board in reach, a safe-conduct honored across jurisdictions, or a verified writ expedited to posting in a single day. Right name, whole road — and every bench watching how you use it.

## Frontier Wardens

- **Sign-Literate** — 1 — L1 — Training — Read and leave Warden sign in full: the public strokes, the network strokes, and the weight of a hand. The *do not* glyph has saved more lives than any wall; now it reports to you.

- **The No-Iron Manner** — 1 — L1 — Training — The kit and the carriage both: in wild country, creatures’ first response to you is assessment rather than attack, and you gain +2 dice on first contact with any Wild being.

- **Relay Access** — 1 — L2 — Access — The caches and the lines: resupply in deep country, and send messages that travel *whole* — unopened, unedited, unrefused — to anywhere the network reaches.

- **Vouched at the Gather** — 1 — L2 — Standing — The network answers for you: any Warden extends working trust on sight, and once per session a guide, contact, or bed at a relay post is within reach.

- **Treaty Words** — 2 — L5 — One-Shot, Access — Invoke a standing treaty’s protocols before a sovereign’s court or creatures: hostilities pause for one parley the Wild will honor — spoken on the network’s credit, which is three centuries of taking *no* for an answer and coming back polite. Spend it like what it is.

# The Drifter Pool

For the unaffiliated: five cards, and \[PROPOSAL F4\] **no Access among them** — that is the condition, priced honestly. What the loose world teaches instead is what no institution can revoke.

- **Beholden to None** — 1 — L1 — Training, Stance — +2 dice against coercion, recruitment pressure, and any social leverage that assumes you answer to someone. You don’t. It shows, and it costs them dice to keep forgetting.

- **Odd Jobs** — 1 — L1 — Training — You’ve done everything once: once per scene, treat a skill you have 0 rating in as rating 1, or gain +1 die on any genuinely improvised approach.

- **The Word Travels** — 1 — L1 — Standing — Your reputation is your only mark, carried mouth to mouth: +1 die on social rolls anywhere your deeds are honestly known — and the GM keeps the Reputation line, so keep the deeds worth carrying.

- **The Loose Web** — 1 — L2 — Standing — Once per session: a fixer, tavern-keeper, road-friend, or fellow freelancer knows you — a job lead, a warning, a floor to sleep on — paid in kind, remembered honestly, owed nothing else.

- **Nobody’s Flag** — 2 — L5 — One-Shot, Training — In a conflict between institutions, both sides accept you as the neutral hand: carry the parley, hold the stakes, walk between the lines untouched for a scene. No sworn, marked, chartered, or sigiled soul can ever play this card. It is the one thing being no one’s buys, and there are days on this frontier when it is worth more than everything it cost.

## Table Notes

**The tags are the drama.** Training/Standing/Access exists so that leaving a faction is a mechanical event the whole table can see: the severed Lion still cannot look away (*Do Not Stop Seeing* is theirs forever), still carries a stained version of the sigil’s meaning (Standing, converted), and finds the church doors shut (*Access, gone*). When a PC breaks with their faction, resolve the five cards on-screen, one at a time. It is the best scene the deck will ever hand you.

**Access is the GM’s throttle.** Every Access card is an invitation to the faction’s chapter — *The Circuit Knows* opens the Riders’ web, *Treaty Words* opens a sovereign’s court — and using one should always cost a little story: the favor noted, the ledger updated, the institution’s interest in the party incremented. Faction cards are how the factions watch their members back.

**The capstones are the institutions at full weight.** Each L5 signature is the faction doing the thing only it can do — the Table leaning, the Pool answering, the Exchange speaking with one voice — and each leaves a residue the chapters price in detail. A party that has played three of them owes three institutions, and a campaign can be steered for a year on which debts come due in what order.
