# Class Card Pools

## How Pools Work

Every class teaches more than any one member learns. A class’s full repertoire is divided into **archetype pools** — distinct traditions, trainings, or expressions of the class — and the archetype you choose at creation is the second-most defining choice you make after the class itself. \[PROPOSAL C1\] Your 10 starting class cards come from your archetype’s pool; from level 2 onward you may cross-train, buying sibling-archetype cards at one card level higher than printed — the techniques travel, but they were not built for your hands.

An archetype is not a subclass wall; it is a center of gravity. Two Zealots of different pools are recognizably the same calling and unmistakably different people, and the frontier knows the difference on sight — which is the point. The archetypes below are the ones the setting’s institutions actually produce, and each pool’s flavor is that institution’s fingerprint.

## Witness <span class="nodecor">(Witnesses of the Lion)</span>

Faith made practical. A Witness carries a **Spark of Faith**, shapes it through prayer, and may strengthen a prayer through **Sacrifice**: accept a fitting cost, exposure, obligation, or complication to improve the card’s effect. Witness cards express divine auras, grace, counsel, the Circuit, endurance, miracles, and the authority earned by carrying the word from community to community.

### The Line-Holder

*The formation’s anchor — the one who is not moving, so that others don’t have to be brave.* Playstyle: defense, protection, punishing enemies who ignore you.

Line-Holder prayers project the Witness’s divine aura. Cards marked **Sacrifice** may be strengthened by accepting their stated cost.

- **Hold Here** — 1 — L1 — Stance — While you don’t move, allies within 2 tiles gain +1 Defend and cannot be forced to move.

- **You Face Me** — 1 — L1 — One enemy within 3 tiles must include you in its next attack or lose 1 die from it.

- **The Door Does Not Open** — 1 — L3 — Reaction — When an enemy tries to pass within your reach, stop its movement and Strike it.

- **Redoubt** — 2 — L5 — One-Shot — Until your side’s next surge, attacks against allies within 2 tiles of you target you instead, and you gain +2 Armor.

### The Intercessor

*The formation’s honest voice — prayer as logistics, aid arriving because it was asked for truthfully.* Playstyle: support, healing, buying allies’ successes.

The Intercessor develops Touch of Grace and Counsel: faith carried through contact, truthful speech, and a willingness to bear part of another person’s hurt.

- **Spoken Plainly** — 1 — L1 — An ally within 5 tiles heals 2 HP or clears one fear or despair effect; you must say aloud, in character, why they will hold.

- **Borrowed Light** — 1 — L2 — An ally’s next roll this round gains +2 dice; if they tell the table what they’re afraid of, +3.

- **The Vapor Carries It** — 1 — L4 — One-Shot — Ask the GM one true question about the present danger; the answer arrives as vision.

- **Answered** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot — Celestial aid, briefly: choose one — all allies heal 3 HP; or one enemy of the Shadow takes hits equal to your Religion.

### The Lamplighter

*The burning end of witness — the one sent where the dark is thickest, to make it regret the attention.* Playstyle: striker against the Shadow’s works; light as a weapon.

The Lamplighter turns the Spark of Faith toward miracles, sacred presence, and carrying the word into places where ordinary prayer cannot reach.

- **Lamp Oil and Iron** — 1 — L1 — Your Strikes and Shoots this round ignore the Armor of undead and Shadow-aligned creatures.

- **Cast Light** — 1 — L1 — Illuminate 5 tiles: hidden things are revealed; things that hate light lose 1 Defend there.

- **I See You** — 1 — L3 — Name one enemy you can see: your side’s attacks against it gain +1 die until it drops. It knows.

- **Dawn, Early** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot — A burst of revealing light: every enemy within 3 tiles takes 2 hits (Shadow-aligned: 4) and everything concealed is not.

## Drifter <span class="nodecor">(Unaffiliated)</span>

The road’s self-made legend. A Drifter has no institution to supply an answer, only **Deeds** already done, the **Legend** those deeds have produced, and **Stances** learned by surviving. Drifter cards reward independence, danger sense, impossible timing, quick movement, stoicism, and the final stand; their best effects become stronger when the Drifter is isolated, outnumbered, or acting on a reputation the table has seen them earn.

### The Lone Wolf

*The survivor who learned not to expect rescue.* Playstyle: self-reliance, stoicism, and becoming harder to finish when isolated or outnumbered.

- **Lone Wolf** — 1 — L1 — Stance — While no ally is within 2 tiles, gain +1 Defend and +1 die on saves.

- **Stoicism** — 1 — L2 — Reaction — When you suffer fear, pain, or forced movement, ignore that effect; the wound or damage still lands.

- **Survivor’s Instinct** — 1 — L3 — When you enter a dangerous place, ask what will kill you first if left unanswered; the GM answers honestly.

- **Final Stand** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot — When a wound would leave you Dying, reduce it one severity and immediately take a full action without spending Momentum.

### The Living Legend

*A reputation with boots on.* Playstyle: invoke Deeds, force enemies to account for your Legend, and turn remembered exploits into present leverage.

- **I’ve Done Worse** — 1 — L1 — Invoke a relevant Deed: gain +2 dice on the action; if the table saw this Deed earned in play, gain +3 instead.

- **Reputation Arrives First** — 1 — L2 — Reveal your name or Legend; one NPC who knows it must hesitate, negotiate, or openly challenge you before acting against you.

- **Living Legend** — 1 — L3 — Stance — Once per surge, when you reinforce your Reputation, add 1 die to the action or move 2" free.

- **Mythic Wanderer** — 2 — L5 — One-Shot — Explain how a past Deed taught you the answer; establish one plausible route, weakness, contact, or escape the scene had not named.

### The Road Ghost

*Distance and bad timing have tried before.* Playstyle: danger sense, quick steps, evasion, impossible timing, and being gone before the trap admits it failed.

- **Road Legs** — 1 — L1 — Stance — You and adjacent allies gain +1 Move; difficult ground doesn’t slow you.

- **I Know a Way** — 1 — L2 — Move an ally within 5 tiles up to their Move (a shortcut, a boost, a shouted route).

- **Uncatchable** — 1 — L4 — Until end of scene, enemies cannot make free Strikes against your movement and natural difficult ground does not slow you.

- **Rider on the Ridge** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot — Help arrives: an NPC ally you plausibly know (GM’s choice from your circuit) enters the scene’s edge, favorably disposed.

## Arcanist <span class="nodecor">(Academic Enclaves)</span>

Erupted power, stewarded: every Arcanist carries a volatile **Spark** and shapes spell cards through training rather than innate safety. An Arcanist may use **Accelerants** to strengthen a spell at an added cost and develops an **Arcane Focus**—a battery, familiar, or lens—that changes how power is stored, delivered, or perceived. Higher cards concern ritual casting, arcane sight, efficient casting, overcasting, spell shaping, and becoming a living conduit.

### The Ward-Wright

*The useful Enclave, in one person — containment as a combat art.* Playstyle: barriers, zones, control; the battlefield does what you chalk.

- **Chalk Line** — 1 — L1 — Draw a 3-tile line: enemies crossing it lose 1 die from their next action.

- **Warding Circle** — 1 — L2 — A 1-tile circle: allies inside gain +1 Defend and +1 die on saves; lasts until you move.

- **Containment** — 2 — L4 — Box one enemy in force (Defend 2, HP 4 walls); it batters or bypasses, but not this round.

- **The Vellhame Protocol** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot — Suppress all supernatural effects in a 3-tile radius for one round — including your side’s. It exists for a reason.

### The Open Channel

*The eruption, ridden — output the protocols call inadvisable and the moment calls necessary.* Playstyle: blaster; push-your-luck damage that spends your own body.

- **Arc** — 1 — L1 — 3 hits to one target within 5 tiles; you may take 1 damage to make it 4.

- **Discharge** — 1 — L2 — 2 hits to every enemy in a 2-tile cone; take 1 damage per extra tile of reach you add.

- **Overdraw** — 1 — L4 — Stance — Your Channel cards deal +1 hit; you take 1 damage at the start of each of your side’s surges while this holds.

- **The Cascade, Glimpsed** — 2 — L7 — One-Shot — 6 hits, 3-tile radius, centered on you; allies included unless warded; the GM notes, quietly, that it felt easy.

### The Book-Bound

*The inheritance, leaned on — forty dead masters between two chained covers, consulted mid-fight.* Playstyle: versatility and deck manipulation; the answer is in the book.

- **Consult the Names** — 1 — L1 — Look at the top 3 cards of your holstered pile; load one into your hand (discard one from hand if over 5).

- **A Dead Master’s Trick** — 1 — L2 — Copy the printed effect of any card in your expended pile, at +1 cost.

- **The Book Remembers** — 1 — L3 — Reaction — When an enemy uses a supernatural ability, ask the GM what it is and one weakness; the book has seen it before.

- **Forty Hands on the Pen** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot — Play up to 3 cards from your expended pile this surge, paying their costs; the book is briefly, unmistakably awake.

## Dynast <span class="nodecor">(Noble Houses)</span>

The blood, expressed: every Dynast inherits a **House Lineage** whose **Manifestation** may be suppressed or expressed. Expression unlocks bloodline cards and strange anatomy but makes the lineage visible and harder to deny. Dynast cards build Influence, menacing and otherworldly presence, House favors, unnatural instinct, rapid manifestation, and the gradual integration of a body that was never entirely mortal.

### The Thirsting Line <span class="nodecor">(Corvara-pattern)</span>

*Speed past watching comfortably, and a debt the body collects.* Playstyle: skirmisher; hit-and-gone; blood as resource.

- **Arrival Without Transit** — 1 — L1 — Move your full Move and Strike; if the target hadn’t seen you this round, +2 dice.

- **The Taste** — 1 — L2 — After damaging a living creature in melee: heal 2 HP and learn one true physical fact about it (health, poison, lineage, expression).

- **Night’s Own** — 1 — L3 — Stance — In dim light or darkness: +1 Move, +1 Defend, and you see perfectly.

- **The Red Ledger** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot — Against a creature you have tasted: this Strike ignores Defend, and you know where it is for the rest of the session.

### The Grown Line <span class="nodecor">(Ostrogar-pattern)</span>

*Living armament — plate that thickens where you were struck.* Playstyle: juggernaut; grows harder as the fight goes on.

- **Blade-Ridge** — 1 — L1 — Stance — Your unarmed Strikes use grown edges: +1 die and lethal.

- **Answer in Bone** — 1 — L2 — Reaction — When an attack damages you, gain +1 Armor until end of scene (max +3); the plate remembers.

- **Horn and Weight** — 1 — L3 — Move up to your Move in a line and Strike; on a hit, push the target 2 tiles and knock it down.

- **The Seat, Refused** — 2 — L7 — One-Shot — Until end of scene you cannot be moved, knocked down, or reduced below 1 HP by attacks; you also cannot move. A wall that remembers why it stands.

### The Living Iron <span class="nodecor">(Ferrant-pattern)</span>

*Flesh of frame-metal — a body that banks the charge.* Playstyle: durable battery; stored vim spent as violence or utility.

- **Take the Charge** — 1 — L1 — Absorb a vim source (a Heart, a jolt-line, an ally’s cell): bank 3 *ebbs* on this card’s line (max 6).

- **Discharge Through the Hands** — 1 — L1 — Spend banked ebbs: 1 hit per ebb to a creature you’re touching, or power any device for a scene.

- **Ringing Bones** — 1 — L3 — Stance — +1 Armor; you are immune to vim/electrical harm and bank 1 ebb whenever it’s tried.

- **Kin to the Herd** — 2 — L5 — One-Shot — A spagyric frame or chassis within sight treats you as its handler for the scene; it gentles, obeys, or carries.

### The Drowned Line <span class="nodecor">(Ophara-pattern)</span>

*Scale, claw, and blood that fights back.* Playstyle: controller-bruiser; punishes attackers; owns the water.

- **Acid in the Veins** — 1 — L1 — Stance — Creatures that hit you in melee take 1 hit; your dead body is a House matter, and everyone can tell.

- **The Drowned Court’s Grip** — 1 — L2 — Grapple with claws: +2 dice, and a grappled foe takes 1 acid hit per round held.

- **Regrow** — 1 — L3 — Heal 3 HP; clear one lingering injury; over downtime, regrow what was lost.

- **Take It Under** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot — In or beside water: drag one enemy your size or smaller beneath; it spends its side’s next 2 Momentum surfacing or begins to drown.

## Mentalist <span class="nodecor">(Monastic Orders)</span>

The cultivated mind turned outward. A Mentalist learns **Psychic Disciplines**, manifests thought as force, and may **Overload** a psychic card for a stronger effect at the cost printed on it—usually strain, exposure, or a temporary loss of control. Many Mentalists externalize part of their practice through a psicrystal or similar focus. Their cards emphasize mind blades, telepathy, probing touch, composure, hidden manifestation, mind over matter, and the watching mind.

### The Mind Blade

*Thought given an edge.* Playstyle: manifest a psychic weapon, cut material and supernatural workings, and turn disciplined intent into close violence.

- **Mind Blade** — 1 — L1 — Stance, Psychic — Manifest a weapon of thought: Strikes with it use Study or Nimbleness dice (your choice) and are lethal.

- **Psychic Strike** — 1 — L2 — Your mind-blade Strikes this round ignore 2 Armor and can sever an object or psychic tether outright.

- **Overloaded Edge** — 1 — L4 — Overload — Strike a spell, ward, or supernatural effect within reach: on 2 hits, it ends. Take a minor mental wound to end it on 1 hit instead.

- **Mind Over Distance** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot, Psychic — Cut a door in distance: you and adjacent allies step up to 20", through nothing, arriving instantly.

### The Watching Mind

*The inward way — the still mind, and other minds, approached lawfully.* Playstyle: telepathy, probing touch, hidden manifestation, control, and insight.

- **Mental Discipline** — 1 — L1 — Stance — +2 dice on saves against fear, deception, and mind-effects; you cannot be surprised.

- **Probing Touch** — 1 — L1 — One creature you touch or closely observe: learn its intent, its fear, or its next action (GM’s honest answer).

- **Speech Without Voice** — 1 — L2 — Until end of scene, your side converses silently at any distance in sight; coordination cards and aid cost allies 1 less die of penalty from range or noise.

- **The Closed Door, Held Open** — 2 — L7 — One-Shot — With consent, enter a willing mind: heal one mental affliction, share one memory truly, or stand with them against something that got in. Never without consent. The examiners check.

### The Composed Self

*A mind trained until flesh, fear, and deprivation become negotiable.* Playstyle: mental discipline, sustenance, resilience, mind over matter, and calm support under pressure.

- **Sustenance** — 1 — L1 — Stance, Psychic — Ignore fatigue, hunger, thirst, and environmental exposure for the scene; recovery is still required afterward.

- **Refined Mind** — 1 — L2 — Use Study in place of Athletics or Survive for one action by directing the body with conscious precision.

- **Mind Over Matter** — 1 — L3 — Reaction — When you or an ally within 3 tiles is attacked, project a psychic brace and add your Defend to theirs.

- **The Composed Self** — 2 — L5 — One-Shot, Psychic — Until end of scene, you cannot be compelled, frightened, or rendered unconscious; communicate silently with willing allies you perceive.

## Tinkerer <span class="nodecor">(Landed Guilds)</span>

Engineering as frontier magic. A Tinkerer prepares repeatable **Principles**, chooses a technical specialization, and maintains a signature **Prototype Device**. Cards cover engineering insight, rapid and spectral construction, Discoveries, infrastructure networks, impossible materials, living machinery, and systems that grow from a clever tool into part of the world’s architecture.

### The Coilwright

*Energetics — vim charged, kept, and delivered on schedule.* Playstyle: zone damage, batteries, powering the party’s world.

- **Jolt** — 1 — L1 — 2 hits to a target within 3 tiles; constructs, frames, and Living Iron take none and bank it instead.

- **Storage Cell** — 1 — L1 — Consumable ×2 — A charged cell: the bearer may spend it for +1 Momentum for the side (their surge only) or to power a device for a scene.

- **Ground the Line** — 1 — L3 — Reaction — Redirect one vim, lightning, or Heart-driven effect targeting anything within 3 tiles into the earth. Bank 1 ebb; bill later.

- **The Dawn Clause** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot — At the procedurally correct moment (GM agrees the sky is right): your next Coilwright card triples its numbers. Nobody knows why. It replicates.

### The Crucible-Trained

*Alchemy — matter argued into new opinions.* Playstyle: grenades, acids, transmutation; the environment is ammunition.

- **Flask of Opinion** — 1 — L1 — Thrown, 5 tiles: 2 hits in a 1-tile burst, and the ground is briefly (choose) burning, slick, or fogged.

- **Solvent** — 1 — L2 — Destroy up to a door’s worth of material, or strip 2 Armor from a target until repaired.

- **Alloy on Demand** — 1 — L3 — Reforge or harden: an ally’s weapon gains +1 die, or their Armor +1, until end of scene.

- **Orichalcum Sliver** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot — Overcharge a weapon with the crown metal: its next attack adds hits equal to your Study. The sliver is spent, and the Crucible will ask where you got it.

### The Frame-Wright

*Spagyrics — the living machine, grown, gentled, and yours.* Playstyle: companion archetype; a small chassis fights beside you.

- **The Frame** — 1 — L1 — Troop — Deploy your chassis: HP 5, Strike 3, Defend 1, Move 2, carries loads. It re-musters between encounters; it mourns if you don’t.

- **Grown for This** — 1 — L2 — Stance — Choose the frame’s graft for the scene: +1 Strike (goring horn), +1 Defend (plate), or +1 Move (long limbs).

- **Good Beast** — 1 — L2 — Reaction — The frame interposes: an attack on you or an adjacent ally hits the frame instead.

- **The Yard’s Best Line** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot — The frame exceeds procedure: this scene it acts on 0-Momentum once per surge, and something in it is clearly, briefly, *choosing* to. File a report. Or don’t.

## Sawbone <span class="nodecor">(Landed Guilds)</span>

The Guild’s answer to the body. A Sawbone practices **Organic Science**, reads anatomy with frightening precision, and turns Discoveries into emergency treatment, surgical alterations, grafts, mutagens, hostile procedures, and constructed servants. Their work can preserve life, redesign it, or make the distinction professionally inconvenient.

### The Red Thread

*Chirurgeon-trained — surgery under fire, anatomy as a map.* Playstyle: the party’s true healer; precision offense in a pinch.

- **Field Surgery** — 1 — L1 — Heal an adjacent ally 3 HP and clear one bleed, poison, or injury; +1 HP if you narrate the procedure.

- **I Know Where It Hurts** — 1 — L2 — Your Strike targets anatomy: on any hit, the target also loses 1 die from its next action.

- **Triage** — 1 — L3 — Heal 2 HP split among up to 3 allies within 3 tiles; the case library note is free.

- **Not On My Table** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot — An ally at 0 HP or dead this scene is stabilized at 1 HP; the injury annex gains an entry with your name on it.

### The Preparatory

*Green Chain–trained — tinctures, stims, and poultices, brewed ahead of need.* Playstyle: pre-battle buffer; consumables as party economy.

- **Tincture Kit** — 1 — L1 — Consumable ×3 — Distribute before scenes: spend to heal 2 HP or clear fatigue.

- **Vigor Draught** — 1 — L2 — Consumable ×2 — Spend for +2 dice on one physical roll; the crash afterward is a role-playing opportunity.

- **Smelling Salts & Worse** — 1 — L3 — Reaction — An ally dropping to 0 HP stays at 1 and acts normally this round; they owe you a conversation.

- **The Season’s Brew** — 2 — L5 — One-Shot — Before a scene begins, every ally drinks: +1 die on all saves and +2 max HP for the scene.

### The Toxicologist

*Crucible-trained — the dose makes the poison, and you did the math.* Playstyle: debuffer; the enemy’s numbers quietly stop working.

- **Coated Edge** — 1 — L1 — An ally’s weapon (or yours) is dosed: its next 2 hits also cost the target 1 die from its next action.

- **Know the Antidote** — 1 — L2 — Reaction — Cancel one poison, venom, or toxin effect on anyone within reach; identify its source.

- **The Slow Argument** — 1 — L4 — Touch or hit: the target’s dice pools drop by 1 at the start of each of its surges (max –3) until treated.

- **The Numbers Person** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot — Declare a creature type you’ve studied this session: your side’s attacks against it gain +1 die and ignore 1 Armor for the scene.

## Captain <span class="nodecor">(Mercenary Companies)</span>

The tactician and the muster. A Captain builds **Legend** through Deeds and spends cards on commands: tactical coordination, swift orders, coordinated assaults, assessments, and authority that grows from a squad’s trust into strategic influence. **Prepared** cards are flashbacks—a tool, contact, route, or order established earlier because the Captain planned for this.

### The Creche Captain

*Cloned brothers — expensive, capable, and people, whatever the Rolls decide.* Playstyle: elite small squad; troops that flank, hold, and grieve.

- **Muster: Brothers** — 1 — L1 — Troop ×2 — Two Marchers: HP 3, Strike 3, Defend 1, Move 1 each. They use real names.

- **By the Numbers** — 1 — L2 — Two of your troops acting together this surge: their attacks gain +1 die and share hits against one target.

- **The Line Holds** — 1 — L3 — Stance — Your troops within 3 tiles of you gain +1 Defend and cannot be routed or frightened.

- **Ninth of His Line** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot — One troop acts as a full character for the scene (your class dice, its body) — and afterward, it asks you something.

### The Rite-Captain

*The leased dead — tireless, fearless, contract-bound, and buried properly at term.* Playstyle: attrition; the wall that does not eat, sleep, or exceed the order.

- **Muster: the Leased** — 1 — L1 — Troop ×2 — Two leased dead: HP 4, Strike 2, Defend 0, Move 1; immune to fear, poison, fatigue; *never* exceed orders.

- **Stand and Take It** — 1 — L2 — Stance — Your dead within 3 tiles gain +1 Armor and hold ground against any push.

- **The Terms Are Clear** — 1 — L3 — Reaction — When a troop of yours would be turned, commanded, or corrupted by an outside power: it isn’t. The lease is explicit.

- **Wages to the Heirs** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot — A destroyed troop rises once more until end of scene at full HP; at scene’s end you personally close its lease, and the table sees you do it.

### The Verdant Captain

*The grown line — patience, thorns, and fortifications that heal.* Playstyle: area denial; the map slowly becomes yours.

- **Muster: Thorn-Soldiers** — 1 — L1 — Troop ×2 — Two grown: HP 3, Strike 2, Defend 1, Move 1; enemies that Strike them take 1 hit.

- **Root** — 1 — L2 — A grown troop roots: Move 0, +2 Armor, +1 Strike, and its tile plus adjacent become difficult ground for enemies.

- **The Hedge Rises** — 1 — L4 — Grow a 3-tile thorn wall (Defend 1, HP 4 per tile); it regrows 2 HP per round.

- **Third Line’s Answer** — 2 — L7 — One-Shot — All your grown troops root as a free action and fight at +1 die for the scene; when you sound the recall, note carefully whether they come.

### The Chimeric Captain

*The beast-lines — bred for the work, directed by the hand.* Playstyle: mobility and scouting; the pack sees first and arrives first.

- **Muster: the Pack** — 1 — L1 — Troop ×2 — Two beasts: HP 2, Strike 2, Defend 2, Move 3; they flank by instinct (+1 die when paired on a target).

- **Loose the Wing** — 1 — L2 — A flyer scouts: reveal everything within 10 tiles of a point you choose; the GM answers as the bird saw it.

- **Bring Them to Ground** — 1 — L3 — A pack troop’s hit also knocks the target down or holds it in place (your choice).

- **Bred for One Night** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot — Deploy the kennel’s masterpiece for one scene: HP 6, Strike 4, Defend 2, Move 3, and a rider chosen at deployment (silent / climbing / carrying).

### The Free Captain

*Mortal colors — a handful of volunteers, and the trust nothing made can buy.* Playstyle: leadership; no troops — the party is the muster.

- **You Heard the Captain** — 1 — L1 — An ally acts immediately at –1 Momentum cost (minimum 0 for a Move).

- **Answer for Yours** — 1 — L2 — Reaction — When an ally within 5 tiles is hit, reduce the hits by 1; you flinch instead.

- **The Ledger** — 1 — L3 — Once per session per settlement: name a contact from your years on the roads (a factor, a Warden, an old sergeant); the GM makes them real and reachable.

- **Hold Fast, Damn You** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot — Your side’s Momentum this surge becomes 6 (do not roll). Afterward, everyone is spent: –1 die on everything for one round.

## Bounty Hunter <span class="nodecor">(Registries)</span>

The certified pursuit. Every Bounty Hunter names **Marked Prey**, reads its psychic wake, and manifests a **Mind Blade** or **Mind Gun** tuned to the quarry. Cards build psychic strike, the perfect draw, ghost steps, expanded marks, uncanny tracking, impossible pursuit, and the inescapable pressure of a hunter who can follow what a mind leaves behind.

### The Wake-Reader

*Depth as doctrine — the Hunter who knows the quarry better than the quarry does.* Playstyle: single-target supremacy; information becomes dice.

- **Take the Name** — 1 — L1 — Stance — Designate your quarry (true name or established alias): +1 die on all rolls to find, follow, perceive, or fight it.

- **The Itch** — 1 — L1 — Learn your quarry’s bearing and rough distance; through walls, weather, and lies.

- **Dreamt It First** — 1 — L3 — Reaction — Your quarry acts against you or an ally: you anticipated it — the affected creature gains +2 Defend or +2 dice on the save.

- **The Closing** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot — Against a quarry you’ve held for a full session: this scene, you act on its surges too (1 free action when its side activates). It dreams you back. Neither of you enjoys it.

### The Taker

*Terms: to answer, to hold — the endgame specialist who delivers them breathing.* Playstyle: ambush and capture; the fight is over before it’s a fight.

- **From Concealment** — 1 — L1 — Attack an enemy unaware of you: +2 dice and it can’t apply Defend.

- **Shackle-Work** — 1 — L2 — Against a downed, grappled, or surprised target: bind it (escape requires 2 successes vs your Knack).

- **Alive Means Alive** — 1 — L2 — Stance — Your attacks can always choose nonlethal, at no penalty, and you know exactly how close to the line you are.

- **Keeping the Appointment** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot — Confront your quarry alone-ish and speak: it must parley one full round — and you gain, from its face, one true answer about who’s behind the writ, if anyone is.

### The Veiled

*The counter-hunter — warded, masked, and rude to things that swim the dreaming.* Playstyle: anti-psychic and defense; hunts what hunts by familiarity.

- **Thin Your Name** — 1 — L1 — Stance — Supernatural attempts to locate, read, or target you specifically take –2 dice; mundane ones –1.

- **Feel the Read** — 1 — L2 — You know when anything is tracking you or an adjacent ally by unnatural means, and its rough direction.

- **Turn the Thread** — 1 — L4 — Reaction — Something reads you: follow it back — learn its nature and bearing, and it knows it was seen.

- **The Mask’s Old Purpose** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot — For a scene, you and adjacent allies are *unfamiliar*: creatures and effects that hunt by identity, name, or memory cannot hold you as targets. The Nol were right all along.

## Shapeshifter <span class="nodecor">(Frontier Wardens)</span>

Embodied transformation. A Shapeshifter learns a **Form** from a specific creature whose heart or willing bond they captured—never a generic animal selected from a list. The form brings its movement, natural weapons, senses, strengths, hungers, and limits. Cards develop bestial instinct, rapid transformation, wild adaptation, hybrid traits, impossible anatomy, and the long road toward perfect shape and living myth.

### The Pelt-Binder

*Few shapes, deeply held — the woman who is a wolf, and has been its colleague thirty years.* Playstyle: committed transformation; the bound shape at full power.

- **Don the Pelt** — 1 — L1 — Shift into a bound form as part of any Move; while worn, that form’s rolls gain +1 die.

- **The Skin Holds** — 1 — L2 — Reaction — Damage that would force you out of form doesn’t; reduce it by 2 besides.

- **Vouched For** — 1 — L3 — In your bound shape, Wild creatures of its kind and their kin read you as kin: passage, parley, or one favor, GM adjudicated.

- **Old Colleagues** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot — Your bound form fights beside you instead: it steps out (use the form’s full statline as a Troop for the scene) while you remain yourself. Do not discuss this with the Enclaves.

### The Hearth-Walker

*Many light guests — the cat for the town, the hawk for the sky, the horse for the road.* Playstyle: versatility; the right shape at the right instant.

- **Quick Change** — 1 — L1 — Shift as a free part of any action, once per surge.

- **Borrowed Instinct** — 1 — L2 — In any form: use one of its senses or movement modes for a round while otherwise yourself.

- **The Household** — 1 — L3 — Stance — Each round, your first shift refunds its Momentum; your forms’ passive traits linger 1 round after leaving them.

- **Many Things at Once** — 2 — L6 — One-Shot — For one round, layer two light forms: both statlines’ best values, both traits, and the unmistakable look of something that has been many things and is not entirely sure what it currently is.

### The Face-Taker

*The town is also a landscape — humanoid shapes, under the code.* Playstyle: infiltration and social control; the network’s inside work.

- **A Studied Face** — 1 — L1 — Wear a face you’ve observed for a scene: Deceive to maintain gains +2 dice; the code requires you log it.

- **Crowd-Shape** — 1 — L2 — In any settlement: become forgettably local — pursuit and Perceive against you take –2 dice while you do nothing violent.

- **Wear the Office** — 1 — L4 — Your face comes with its standing: doors, deference, and answers owed to whoever you appear to be, until tested against someone who truly knows them.

- **The Code’s One Exception** — 2 — L7 — One-Shot — Wear the face of someone present and willing: for a scene, you are them to every sense and system — writs, Reads, and recognition included. The network is told afterward. Always.

## Table Notes

**Archetype is silhouette.** A pool should be readable across a dark room: the Line-Holder plants, the Open Channel glows, the Rite-Captain’s wall does not breathe. When building the full pools out from these samples, protect each archetype’s verbs — versatility belongs to the Book-Bound and the Hearth-Walker *because* the Ward-Wright and the Pelt-Binder don’t have it.

**The pools carry the lore.** Every archetype above is an institution’s fingerprint, and several cards deliberately point at live setting questions — the frame that chooses, the thorn line and its recall, the brother who asks, the dead man’s lease closed on-screen. GMs should let those cards be doors: a Verdant Captain playing *Third Line’s Answer* has invited the Waking Made to their table, and the game intends exactly that.

**Sample, not census.** Three to four cards sketch each pool’s grammar; full pools want 15–20 cards across levels 1–9, with duplicates-permitted marked per card. The costs, numbers, and level placements here are tuning stakes in the ground \[PROPOSAL C7\] — the shapes are the design; the integers are negotiable.
