# Power Sources and Disciplines

> **Design authority:** This document defines the five-source power model. It replaces the earlier assumption that every supernatural ability is a fixed power in a single, universal discipline system. Existing class power lists remain useful as names and effect prompts until their entries are converted to the formats below.

## Core Premise

There are five power sources, and each one answers a different fantasy:

| Source | Classes | What the character masters | How Alignment is used |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Legend** | Captain, Drifter | Stances and exceptional uses of ordinary combat actions | Commit dots to maintain stances; spend dots on exploits |
| **Magic** | Arcanist, Witness | Spells that channel extraplanar forces | Spend dots to cast, then risk forgetting the spell |
| **Occultism** | Dynast, Shapeshifter | Relationships and bargains with nonhuman creatures | Stake dots in an active bond |
| **Psionics** | Bounty Hunter, Mentalist | A trained self extended beyond normal bodily limits | Spend dots, then risk control on a pushed d100 roll |
| **Superscience** | Sawbone, Tinkerer | Repeatable principles used to fabricate impossible devices | The operator spends dots for one-use functions or commits them to keep devices running |

These are not five damage themes laid over one activation rule. A spell, a stance, a pact, a psychic technique, and a laboratory principle should feel different on the character sheet and at the table.

## Shared Chassis

The sources share only the parts that benefit from being common.

The **powers-known** progression (see *How the Game Works*) is deliberately lean: class sets the starting breadth, then every later level grants **one** source selection from an available list. Everything beyond that floor is learned from the world without a numeric cap—through teachers, texts, sites, factions, species elders, sovereign creatures, patrons, and research. A tradition, discipline, patron list, or technical field is something in the world that can be opened, and the stories behind those openings are the practitioner's biography.

### Power Level

Every learnable power, contract, technique, or principle has a **Power Level (PL)** from 0 to 10.

- A character normally cannot learn an entry whose PL exceeds **half their level, rounded up**: PL 1 at levels 1–2, PL 2 at levels 3–4, and so on through PL 10 at levels 19–20.
- PL measures the complexity and narrative reach of the entry, not a universal numerical bonus.
- PL 0 entries are reliable fundamentals and normally have no Alignment cost.
- PL 9 and PL 10 entries, first available at levels 17 and 19, should change regions, institutions, wars, or the direction of a campaign. They are learned only from the world, never from the automatic level selection.
- A scalable entry remains one known entry. Its result ladder, form, contract clauses, or construction applications do not become several near-duplicate powers merely because its output grows.

### Alignment

Every person has ten permanent Alignment dots distributed among the five Alignments. A character's permanent dots determine what they are capable of learning and using. Their reserve tracks which dots are currently available.

The side that takes Momentum refreshes its members' uncommitted and unstaked reserve dots to their permanent ratings. Dots tied up in an ongoing stance, bond, primed device, or operating device do not refresh until released.

A sourced entry's listed Alignment dots total its PL; PL 0 therefore lists no dots. This total is the entry's **Alignment footprint**, not a universal instruction to spend. The source determines whether those dots are spent, committed, staked, or carried into a fabricated device's operating requirement. Mixed footprints still describe the act's nature: Heavens 1 + Wild 1 makes a PL 2 holy flame both holy and flame.

As an authoring guideline, the largest single-Alignment requirement should be about half the entry's PL, rounded up. High-PL powers should bend a character toward two or more natures rather than require breaking the normal rating cap of 6.

| Verb | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Require** | The character must possess the listed permanent dots. Requiring a dot does not make it unavailable. |
| **Spend** | Remove listed dots from the current reserve. They can refresh when the character's side next takes Momentum. |
| **Commit** | Set listed reserve dots aside while an effect is maintained. They cannot refresh or pay another cost. |
| **Stake** | Commit dots to a creature as the collateral of a bargain. A breach may delay their return. |

No temporary effect rewrites a character's permanent Alignment distribution unless it explicitly says that it does.

Outside a structured encounter, treat uncommitted reserve dots as full at the beginning of each meaningful exchange. Commitments and stakes persist across exchanges. Dots released outside surge structure become available at the beginning of the next scene. If repeated use, pursuit, or another time pressure makes reserve order matter, enter surge structure and use the ordinary refresh rule. Magic forgetting, contract Breaches, psychic Overruns, consumed inputs, and device failure persist regardless of whether Momentum is being tracked.

### The Known Unit

“Power known” is convenient shorthand, but what occupies a known selection depends on the source.

| Source | One known selection |
|---|---|
| Legend | One stance or exploit |
| Magic | One spell |
| Occultism | One Dynast patron gift, or one Shapeshifter form contract or deeper form clause |
| Psionics | One broad psychic technique |
| Superscience | One construction principle |

Class progression grants guaranteed selections. Characters may learn additional entries by interacting with the world: training under a legendary duelist, stealing a formula, accepting a pact, surviving a psychic breakthrough, or conducting original research. Such rewards still obey PL and Alignment requirements, but they need not consume the next class-granted selection unless the reward says otherwise.

Ordinary biological abilities, cultural practices, institutional Training, Standing, and Access are **Traits**, not a sixth source. A supernatural species or faction entry must identify one of the five sources and use its native procedure.

### Entry Format

Every entry should say, in this order:

1. **Name, source, and PL**
2. **Alignment:** the required distribution and the source-specific cost
3. **Learning:** any teacher, revelation, creature, laboratory, or story prerequisite
4. **Activation:** action, reaction, stance entry, pact opening, manifestation, or hours of work
5. **Effect:** what happens in game terms
6. **Duration and release:** when ongoing effects and tied dots end
7. **Source risk:** forgetting, breach, overrun, flaw, or other consequence
8. **Tags:** concise rules terms used by other mechanics

The source procedure supplies the rest. Individual entries should not repeat the entire source engine.

## Class Access

Class determines starting breadth. After creation, every class gains one source selection per level; each count below refers to the source's known unit.

| Class | Source | Starting selections | Selections at each later level |
|---|---|---:|---:|
| Captain | Legend | 3 | 1 |
| Drifter | Legend | 4 | 1 |
| Arcanist | Magic | 5 | 1 |
| Witness | Magic | 3 | 1 |
| Dynast | Occultism | 3 | 1 |
| Shapeshifter | Occultism | 3 | 1 |
| Bounty Hunter | Psionics | 3 | 1 |
| Mentalist | Psionics | 4 | 1 |
| Sawbone | Superscience | 5 | 1 |
| Tinkerer | Superscience | 5 | 1 |

A class list describes the easiest expressions for that class to learn, not an impermeable wall around the source. A Bounty Hunter and a Mentalist can learn the same psychic discipline while expressing it very differently.

In addition to the universal one-per-level selection, a Shapeshifter learns one bonus specific Form at levels 3, 6, and 9. These are creature relationships, not free generic stat packages.

## Legend

Legend is power achieved through action, nerve, reputation, and impossible mastery of ordinary violence. It belongs to the martial classes.

- A **Captain** turns formation, timing, command, and personal example into force.
- A **Drifter** turns mobility, instinct, marksmanship, and practiced self-reliance into force.

Legend never asks whether the character can throw a fireball. It asks how far a mortal can push Strike, Shoot, Move, Defend, Aid, and other core actions before the deed becomes a story people repeat.

### Stances

A stance is a sustained commitment to a way of fighting.

1. Enter the stance using the activation printed in its entry.
2. Commit its listed Alignment dots from the character's current reserve.
3. Gain the stance's benefit while those dots remain committed.
4. By default, a character can maintain only one stance.
5. The stance ends when the character releases it, becomes unable to act, enters another stance, or meets an ending condition in the entry.
6. Released dots become eligible to refresh when the character's side next takes Momentum. They do not return immediately, so rapidly switching stances has a real cost.

Stances must change decisions, not merely add a permanent numerical bonus. Good stances reward positioning, target choice, tempo, protection, or a recognizable sequence of core actions.

The commitment is the price of persistence. A one-use exploit affects only the action or round in which it is used unless its entry says otherwise; a stance keeps granting its benefits for as long as its dots stay committed. A PL 10 stance commits all ten Alignment dots a person possesses. It should therefore be an astonishing capstone method with several mutually reinforcing, encounter-defining benefits—not an ordinary bonus scaled upward.

### Exploits

An exploit improves or reinterprets a core action.

- Spend the listed Alignment when declaring the action.
- An exploit's spent dots total its PL; a stance's committed dots total its PL.
- The exploit uses the action and Momentum cost printed in its entry. If it modifies a core action, it does not also charge a generic “power activation” cost.
- Resolve the ordinary action with the exploit's changes.
- An exploit may set up, cash in, or temporarily alter a stance, but it should remain grounded in something the character physically does.

### Legend Traditions

These are content lanes, not separate subsystems:

| Tradition | Typical content |
|---|---|
| **Command** | Aid, formation, coordinated movement, directing allies |
| **Warcraft** | Reading a battlefield, exploiting tempo, breaking enemy plans |
| **Bulwark** | Guarding, interposing, enduring, controlling a threatened space |
| **Gunplay** | Aiming, drawing, trick shots, suppression, ammunition discipline |
| **Skirmish** | Mobility, ambush, pursuit, escape, fighting from bad ground |
| **Pathfinding** | Travel expertise turned into tactical advantage |
| **Legendry** | Signature deeds whose reputation gives them weight |

Some traditions suit one class more readily, but neither class owns a tradition absolutely.

### Legend Authoring Test

A proposed Legend entry belongs here when:

- it attaches to a core physical or social action;
- witnesses could describe the act without needing a theory of supernatural energy;
- its strongest effect comes from commitment, timing, or mastery;
- removing the character's gear may change how it is performed but does not erase the learned technique.

If the effect is primarily a spell, a borrowed creature's gift, a psychic projection, or a constructed device, use that source instead.

## Magic

Magic channels extraplanar energies into a desired result. An Arcanist may learn its shapes as formulae and a Witness may receive them through instinct or revelation, but neither fully domesticates the thing passing through them.

Magic is useful, mysterious, and intrinsically unsafe to remember.

### Casting a Spell

1. Choose a known, unforgotten spell and declare its intended result.
2. Pay its action cost and spend its listed Alignment dots.
3. Meet its listed **Observances**, or explicitly name the ones being ignored.
4. Resolve the spell. Ignoring an Observance does not make the spell fizzle.
5. After the spell resolves, make its forget roll.

### Forgetting Spells

After each casting, roll d100. If the result is equal to or less than five times the spell's PL, the caster forgets that spell for the rest of the day.

| Spell PL | Forget chance |
|---:|---:|
| 0 | 0% |
| 1 | 5% |
| 2 | 10% |
| 3 | 15% |
| 4 | 20% |
| 5 | 25% |
| 6 | 30% |
| 7 | 35% |
| 8 | 40% |
| 9 | 45% |
| 10 | 50% |

- The casting resolves before the roll, even when the spell is forgotten.
- A forgotten spell is still known. It simply cannot be cast again until the caster completes the next daily recovery, normally after a night's sleep.
- Roll after every casting, including reactions and casts that ignored Observances.
- PL 0 spells are fundamentals. They are not forgotten through ordinary use.
- Forgetting represents the extraplanar pattern slipping out of mortal thought; it is not mundane memory loss and cannot be cured by being reminded of the words.

### Observances and Twists

An Observance is a special requirement beyond actions and Alignment. It may be:

- a consumed material;
- a focus, place, celestial condition, or sympathetic link;
- an oath, gesture, spoken name, or prohibition;
- extra casting time;
- assistance from another participant;
- a target condition, such as invitation or true knowledge of a name.

A caster may ignore any Observance. The spell still accomplishes its stated purpose, but the GM adds a **Twist** for each materially ignored requirement.

A fair Twist follows three rules:

1. **Honor the result.** The spell must still technically do what the player asked it to do.
2. **Follow the omission.** The consequence should grow from the skipped material, time, permission, protection, or symbolic correspondence.
3. **Match the shortcut.** A trivial omitted courtesy creates a complication; ignoring the central price of a mighty spell can reshape a whole situation.

A Twist may expose the caster, choose an inconvenient route, include an unwanted target, attract an extraplanar notice, shorten or prolong the result, create a debt, or make the wording painfully literal. It should not secretly replace success with failure.

### Spell Traditions

Spell traditions organize discoverable content without changing the casting engine.

| Tradition | Questions it answers |
|---|---|
| **Evocation** | What force can be called across the planes? |
| **Grace** | What blessing, restoration, or sanctuary can be received? |
| **Lightbringing** | What truth can be revealed, purified, or made manifest? |
| **Pyromancy** | How can transformative flame be invited and shaped? |
| **Wardcraft** | What boundary, seal, or interdiction can be imposed? |
| **Lorecraft** | What hidden name, correspondence, or law can be known? |
| **Divine Might** | What overwhelming act can be performed through revelation? |

“Holy Fire,” for example, can require Heavens for its sanctity and Wild for its flame. Its PL gates when it can be learned; its Alignment line states how many Heavens and Wild reserve dots are spent each time it is cast.

### Arcanists and Witnesses

The classes differ in access, fictional method, and who bears the physical residue—not in whether Magic is dangerous to remember.

- **Arcanists** tend to learn more spells through notation, experiment, stolen formulae, and controlled exposure. Every casting leaves literal matter and energy in the caster. This **arcane mass** is real, measurable weight that accumulates across a career; the class's lower Move and inability to benefit from worn Armor abstract the burden, so no separate mass tally is required.
- **Witnesses** tend to receive fewer, broader revelations through devotion, ordeal, intuition, and contact with the beyond. Their higher power bears the mass of the working rather than depositing it in the Witness, so Witnesses do not gain Arcane Burden and may wear Armor normally.

Both roll to forget. A class feature may alter what happens around that roll, but no class makes high-PL Magic routine.

## Occultism

Occultism is not a conventional power list. It is the practice of making deals with creatures whose bodies, names, hungers, bloodlines, and jurisdictions give them powers mortals do not naturally possess.

An occultist's sheet records **who has agreed to what**.

### Contract Record

Every contract includes:

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Creature** | The being, lineage, swarm, spirit, monster, or intelligence involved |
| **PL** | The level gate and narrative magnitude of the relationship |
| **Bond** | Alignment dots that must be staked while the creature's help is active |
| **Terms** | What each side has promised |
| **Need** | What the creature wants often enough to drive play |
| **Gift** | A Shapeshifter form or a Dynast patron's list of granted powers |
| **Tell** | The visible, behavioral, or metaphysical sign of the bond |
| **Breach** | What happens when the occultist violates the deal |
| **Release** | How the active bond ends and when its stake returns |

The creature is an interested party, not a decorative explanation for a normal spell. Even a friendly creature has preferences and limits.

### Opening and Closing a Bond

1. Invoke the creature in the manner stated by the contract.
2. Stake the contract's Bond dots, which total the contract's PL, from the current Alignment reserve.
3. Receive the form or make the patron's learned gifts available.
4. Keep the dots staked until the form ends, the patron is dismissed, or the contract says otherwise.
5. On an ordinary release, the dots become eligible to refresh when the character's side next takes Momentum.
6. On a breach, follow the printed Breach clause. A creature may retain the stake, suspend a gift, demand restitution, or impose a story consequence until the relationship is repaired.

By default, a character can embody one Shapeshifter form or openly channel one Dynast patron at a time. Quiet obligations to other contracted creatures continue.

### Shapeshifters: Form Contracts

A Shapeshifter bargains with relatively weak creatures and receives permission to become them. “Weak” is relative: the creature is beneath a Dynast's patron, but its body may still be dangerous.

Each form is one known contract and supplies a compact form profile:

- Scale and movement modes
- Defenses and resistances
- Natural attacks
- Senses
- Two or three defining adaptations
- An instinct the Shapeshifter must manage
- A visible Tell
- Terms, Need, Breach, and release condition

Forms do not grant replacement primary ability scores, Hit Dice, or an entire monster stat block. They modify the game's current statistics directly. The form should feel like a particular creature with an agenda, not a menu of interchangeable biological upgrades.

Stronger forms are learned through stronger relationships, higher-PL creatures, or deepened contracts. A Shapeshifter can also earn a new clause that adds one adaptation to an existing form instead of learning another creature.

### Dynasts: Patron Contracts

A Dynast bargains with mighty entities: sovereign spirits, ancestral powers, imprisoned gods, genius loci, impossible machines, and other beings capable of granting a coherent suite of gifts.

The patron relationship opens access; it is not itself one of the Dynast's source selections. A starting Dynast receives one PL 0 or PL 1 patron contract through lineage and personal bargain as a class feature. A new patron encountered in play opens another list but does not automatically teach every power on it.

Each patron has:

- a Bond, Terms, Need, Tell, and Breach;
- a **gift list** equivalent in breadth and structure to a class power list or discipline from Legend, Magic, Psionics, or Superscience, or a bespoke list if no other source fits;
- individual PL 0–10 gifts learned through starting selections, advancement, service, negotiation, or discovery.

Each learned gift is one Dynast source selection. It records its own PL, Alignment footprint, borrowed procedure, and **Manifestation**: a permanent, tangible alteration to the Dynast's body. Horn, metal, pallor, script, glass, scent, altered proportions, or stranger anatomy remains even while the patron is dismissed, the gift is suppressed, or the contract is in Breach. An active patron's Tell is separate—it shows present attention, while the Manifestation shows a bargain that can never again be made private.

Opening the patron contract requires staking its Bond. An unlocked gift then follows the engine of the source it imitates:

- a granted spell spends the unmatched part of its footprint and makes the Magic forget roll;
- a granted stance commits the unmatched part of its footprint;
- a granted psychic technique spends the unmatched part and rolls for control;
- a granted principle follows the Superscience construction procedure; when the Dynast operates its device with that patron active, matching Bond dots satisfy that part of the operating requirement, while any other operator pays the full requirement.

Matching staked Bond dots count toward a granted gift's Alignment footprint; use the borrowed source's verb only for the unmatched remainder. The Bond remains staked after the gift resolves. If a borrowed stance, primed device, or operating device continues to rely on those dots, the patron cannot be released without ending it. This makes the patron relationship part of every gift without charging the same dot twice.

The Dynast is exempt from the borrowed source's class restriction, not its procedure or risk. If a patron's gift list is bespoke, the patron record must state its activation and risk explicitly.

### Deals as World Rewards

Occult abilities should be especially discoverable through play. Learning one means finding a creature, understanding what it can offer, becoming worth its attention, and reaching terms. A contract can be renegotiated, inherited, stolen, fulfilled, or broken; each is a relationship the GM can bring back into the fiction. For a Dynast, making a new patron contract opens its gift list; learning an individual gift still requires a selection or a specific world reward and always leaves its permanent Manifestation.

## Psionics

Psionics is the fundamental improvement of the self beyond normal physical bounds. The mind does not summon an outside force. It becomes capable of treating distance, matter, probability, flesh, and other minds as extensions of its own trained action.

- A **Mentalist** most obviously reaches beyond the body's limits: lifting at a distance, seeing elsewhere, or entering another mind.
- A **Bounty Hunter** turns practiced human abilities into psychic certainties: becoming supernaturally convincing, reading prey from traces, imposing pursuit, or making instinct into probability.

The fictional expression differs, but both use the same manifestation procedure.

The Bounty Hunter's **Mind Weapon** is a class feature outside the known-technique list. It uses no selection, Alignment, or d100 roll, though anti-psychic effects can suppress it. A Mentalist who wants a weapon-like construct learns an appropriate technique such as Force Construct.

### Manifesting a Psychic Technique

1. Choose a known technique and its legal target, scale, or mode.
2. Spend the technique's listed Alignment dots.
3. Choose a **Push** from 0 up to the character's level (maximum 20). Choose before rolling.
4. Roll d100 and add the Push.
5. Read the total on the technique's result ladder.

The closer a controlled total comes to 100, the stronger and more complete the effect becomes.

| Total | Result | General meaning |
|---:|---|---|
| 1–24 | **Trace** | A narrow, brief, or fragile expression |
| 25–49 | **Lesser** | Useful but limited expression |
| 50–74 | **Full** | The technique's expected effect |
| 75–89 | **Greater** | Increased scale, duration, precision, or force |
| 90–99 | **Apex** | The strongest safely repeatable expression |
| exactly 100 | **Perfect** | Complete control at the technique's limit |
| over 100 | **Overrun** | The power escapes the character's control |

There is no ordinary failure band. A low roll still manifests the technique at Trace strength.

Push creates the central psychic decision. It improves every likely result, but it also creates a band at the top of the die that will exceed 100. A level-20 character who chooses Push 20 is extraordinarily potent and loses control on a natural 81–100. A smaller Push creates exactly that many chances in a hundred of Overrun, so the player always chooses the risk.

### Overrun

Every psychic technique must print an **Overrun** clause suited to its nature. On a total over 100:

- the power manifests at Perfect magnitude or greater;
- the player no longer controls some combination of target, path, area, duration, or cessation;
- the GM follows the printed Overrun clause rather than simply declaring that nothing happens;
- spent Alignment remains spent.

Good Overrun clauses express excess: telepathy catches every nearby mind, regeneration refuses to stop growing tissue, teleportation carries an unwanted passenger, or probability selects an outcome nobody intended. Psychic backlash may be part of a technique's clause, but generic damage should not replace the more interesting loss of control.

### PL and Psychic Strength

PL gates the complexity of a technique and its available modes. The d100 total determines how powerfully that technique manifests on this use. Do not write three separate “minor,” “greater,” and “supreme” versions of the same act.

Each psychic technique is one broad known entry with:

- PL and an Alignment cost totaling that PL;
- legal targets and range;
- Trace, Lesser, Full, Greater, Apex, and Perfect results;
- an Overrun clause;
- optional modes unlocked at a stated character level.

This structure keeps psionic disciplines few, large, and meaningfully distinct.

## Superscience

Superscience is supernatural manipulation made stable enough to investigate, teach, reproduce, and engineer. Its practitioners do not cast effects directly. They learn procedures and spend hours applying them to matter, space, organisms, and energy until a device can perform the effect for an operator able to supply the right Alignment.

- A **Sawbone** tends toward bodies, reagents, living materials, surgery, and replacement.
- A **Tinkerer** tends toward power, structures, instruments, vehicles, routes, and interfaces.

Both can cross those habits through teachers, laboratories, and research.

### Principles, Not Ready-Made Powers

A Superscience selection teaches one **principle**: a repeatable construction operation such as:

- creating a particular class of power source;
- refining a supernatural material;
- giving a substance a programmed reaction;
- fixing a route between distant locations;
- growing a living component;
- detecting, storing, converting, or transmitting an impossible force.

A principle does not usually produce an encounter-scale effect when learned or invoked. It tells the character what they can build.

### Applying a Principle

Each principle includes:

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **PL** | Level gate and sophistication of the operation |
| **Alignment** | Permanent dots totaling the principle's PL; the maker must meet them, and they contribute to the finished function's Operating Requirement |
| **Inputs** | Materials, specimens, energy, information, or facilities consumed or required |
| **Work** | Hours required for one application |
| **Operation** | What the application changes, creates, or adds |
| **Scale** | How much material, area, throughput, or complexity one application covers |
| **Limits** | Instability, incompatibility, decay, or other engineering constraint |

If an older draft omits Work, one application takes a number of hours equal to its PL, with a minimum of one hour.

Applying a principle requires the maker to possess its listed permanent Alignment, but construction does not spend or commit reserve dots. Superscience uses reserve Alignment when the finished device is operated, not by attaching the maker's capacity to every object they have ever built.

### Constructing a Device

1. **Design the project.** State the intended function and list each known principle application needed to achieve it.
2. **Calculate each function's Operating Requirement.** For each Alignment, take the highest requirement among the principle applications needed for that function. Those maxima form its operating footprint; do not add repeated applications of the same requirement. A device with independent functions may list a different requirement for each.
3. **Meet the principles' requirements.** The maker must possess the permanent Alignment listed by every principle they personally apply. These dots remain available during construction.
4. **Provide inputs and facilities.** A principle cannot ignore its stated physical requirements merely because the maker has enough Alignment.
5. **Perform the work.** Each application takes its listed number of hours. Complex devices emerge by combining several operations in sequence.
6. **Record the device.** Write each function, its Operating Requirement and action, whether its effect is one-use or persistent, its fuel or charges, size, maintenance, and failure condition.

The device, not the principle, produces the field effect.

### Operating a Device

- The **operator**, not necessarily the maker, must possess the function's permanent Alignment and supply its Operating Requirement from their current reserve.
- A **one-use function** spends those dots when operated. Its benefit is immediate or lasts through the current round; it creates no continuing benefit unless its recipe explicitly changes it to a persistent function.
- A **persistent function** commits those dots for as long as it runs. If several functions run at once, the operator must commit each requirement in full unless the recipe explicitly combines them.
- A **primed function**, such as a trap or automatic injector, commits the priming operator's dots until it triggers or is safely disarmed. When it triggers, its effect resolves for that round and the released dots remain spent until the ordinary refresh.
- Fuel, charges, actions, maintenance, and operating conditions still apply in addition to Alignment. A machine with no operator cannot pay an Operating Requirement unless a specific principle supplies an Alignment-bearing operator; an ordinary battery is not a substitute for a person's dots.
- The maker's Alignment is never tied to a finished object merely because they designed or built it. Another person can carry, buy, steal, and use the device if they meet its requirements.

This makes preparation and procedure the Superscience advantage and price. A practitioner can produce transferable, reliable wonders, but every use still occupies an operator's finite Alignment, and every replacement or new design costs hours, materials, facilities, maintenance, and exposed apparatus.

### Fields of Superscience

Fields organize related principles. They are curricula, not separate activation engines.

| Field | Typical principles |
|---|---|
| **Alchemy** | Reagents, solvents, catalysts, energetic compounds, material conversion |
| **Spagyrics** | Living metals, purified essences, grafted qualities, composite substances |
| **Chirurgy** | Prostheses, cultivated organs, surgical interfaces, controlled regeneration |
| **Energetics** | Power sources, storage, conversion, transmission, impossible currents |
| **Artifice** | Frames, mechanisms, programmed responses, automata, containment |
| **Wayfinding** | Routes, spatial anchors, folded distance, navigation through impossible terrain |

A device often combines fields. A wayfinding engine might need an Energetics source, an Artifice control frame, and several Wayfinding applications. That combination is the point of the source.

### Superscience Authoring Test

A proposed Superscience entry belongs here when:

- a repeatable procedure matters more than a moment of inspiration;
- the operation consumes hours and identifiable inputs;
- its output persists as material, apparatus, or working infrastructure;
- another person can usually use the result without learning how it was made, provided they can meet its Operating Requirement;
- combining it with other principles produces new devices.

If the character simply gestures and an immediate effect occurs, the entry is probably Magic or Psionics wearing scientific vocabulary.

## Learning From the World

Class advancement is the reliable floor: one source selection per later level. The world is the uncapped and more interesting route to breadth.

| Source | Typical discovery |
|---|---|
| Legend | A mentor, rival, battlefield lesson, manual, ordeal, or deed worthy of imitation |
| Magic | A grimoire, planar contact, revelation, sacred site, recovered name, or dangerous experiment |
| Occultism | A creature encountered, summoned, inherited, rescued, defeated, or persuaded |
| Psionics | Training, introspection, exposure to another mind, trauma mastered, or a deliberate breakthrough |
| Superscience | A laboratory, schematic, specimen, reverse-engineered device, research program, or collaborating expert |

When granting an entry through play, the GM should establish:

1. what the character must do to understand or secure it;
2. what PL and Alignment are required;
3. which coherent list it belongs to and whether that list is primary, adjacent, or newly opened by the reward;
4. what new relationship, danger, obligation, or infrastructure enters the campaign with it.

Opening a list through initiation normally makes it **adjacent**, so its entries count as one PL higher only when checking the character-level gate. Deeper apprenticeship can make it primary. PL 9–10 entries always require a specific world reward rather than an automatic level selection.

Cross-source learning is allowed when the fiction supports it. The learned entry always uses its native source engine. A Captain who learns a spell rolls to forget it; an Arcanist who makes a pact stakes a Bond; a Mentalist who learns a principle must spend hours constructing its device, and whoever operates that device supplies its Alignment. World-learned entries add to the character's known list and never replace an existing selection.

## Conversion Rules for Existing Content

The earlier catalog contains many useful names and effects, but its one-size-fits-all activation structure is retired. Convert each entry by asking what the character is actually doing.

| If the character is… | Convert the idea to… |
|---|---|
| Perfecting a core action or maintaining a martial method | A Legend stance or exploit |
| Channeling an extraplanar force into an immediate result | A Magic spell with a forget chance and possible Observances |
| Receiving a body or gift from a creature with its own interests | An Occult contract, form, or patron gift |
| Extending trained selfhood beyond bodily limits | A Psionic technique with a result ladder and Overrun |
| Applying a reproducible procedure to fabricate an output | A Superscience principle used in a device |

During conversion:

- remove all draw, hand, discard, and reshuffle language;
- do not preserve three differently named powers when one scalable psychic technique can contain them;
- do not turn Shapeshifter forms into replacement ability scores or monster stat blocks;
- do not turn Dynast patrons into passive flavor attached to an ordinary power list;
- do not let Superscience produce instant effects without a previously fabricated device;
- retain recognizable names and the gist of existing abilities whenever they fit the corrected source;
- rewrite ambiguous effects around the current core actions, conditions, damage types, and Momentum rules.

## Required Content Passes

The five engines are now defined, but the existing content must be migrated in source-sized passes:

1. Rewrite Captain and Drifter entries as stances and core-action exploits.
2. Rewrite Arcanist and Witness entries as spells with Observances and forget chances.
3. Write Dynast patron gift lists with permanent Manifestations and fully specified Shapeshifter creature contracts.
4. Expand each of the forty-five psychic techniques into a PL, Alignment cost, six-step result ladder, and Overrun clause.
5. Replace Sawbone and Tinkerer encounter powers with construction principles and sample device recipes.
6. Finish converting the class volumes, then label every supernatural species or faction entry with its native source; leave ordinary biological, cultural, Training, Standing, and Access entries as Traits.

That migration should preserve the setting's best names and images while changing the machinery beneath them.
