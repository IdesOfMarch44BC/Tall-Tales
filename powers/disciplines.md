# Powers by Discipline — Framework and Sample Lists

> **Status: design proposal.** This document reorganizes the power system around **Disciplines** — thematic lists (Pyromancy, Necromancy, Hexcraft, Divine Might, and so on) that cut across classes, replacing the current arrangement in which every power lives inside one class archetype. It defines how Disciplines work, which classes/factions/species grant access to which, and writes **four Disciplines in full** as templates. It does **not** yet migrate the existing archetype files (`class-power-lists.md`, Volumes 1–3); that is the follow-on pass, to be done once this model is approved. Until then, those files remain authoritative for play.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## What a Discipline Is

A **Discipline** is a themed list of powers organized by *what the power does* — a school of magic, a martial tradition, a bodily gift, an institutional craft. Pyromancy is fire. Necromancy is death and the dead. Hexcraft is the curse and the evil eye. Divine Might is faith made into strength. A Discipline is the same kind of object the archetype lists already are — a ladder of powers from PL 0 to a capstone — but it is not owned by a single class. Different classes, factions, and species reach into different Disciplines depending on **what they are**.

Nothing under the mechanical hood changes. Every power still carries:

- a **Power Level (PL) 0–10** whose dots total its Alignment requirement;
- an **Alignment requirement** spent from your reserves (a Discipline's theme has a cosmological home, but each power lists its own dots);
- **timing and tags** (Reaction, Stance, Once per scene, Troop, Sacrifice, and the rest);
- an **effect**, resolved exactly as printed.

The **powers-known** progression is unchanged (see *How the Game Works*): your class still sets how many powers you know at creation and how many you gain per level, and you still learn one species and one faction power at creation. What changes is the *menu* those choices draw from: your granted Disciplines, rather than a single archetype.

### Governing Alignment

Each Discipline names a **governing Alignment** (sometimes two): the plane its theme belongs to. Most of a Discipline's powers cost those dots, and the price a GM attaches to invoking that plane (Heavens collects in obligation, Shadow in trust, Wild in excess, Wyrd in strangeness, Machine in literalism) is the flavor a heavy user of that Discipline should expect to pay over a campaign. The governing Alignment is descriptive — it tells you what the list *feels* like and what it will cost you — not a second gate on top of the printed requirement.

## Access: Class, Faction, and Species

Three sources open Disciplines to a character. A Discipline no source has opened can still be learned, but only from the world — a teacher, a captured technique, a site, a deed — at the GM's discretion.

**Class grants your core Disciplines.** Each class opens a small cluster of Disciplines that expresses its calling (see the table below). These are the old archetypes, re-cut so that shared themes are shared lists: the Arcanist's blaster archetype becomes **Evocation**, a list a fire-cult faction might also teach.

- Choose **one** core Discipline as your **primary**. You learn and advance its powers at their **printed PL**.
- Your class's other core Disciplines are **adjacent**: treat each of their powers as **one PL higher** for learning eligibility (the printed requirement is unchanged). This is exactly the current cross-training rule, preserved.

**Factions and species grant additional Disciplines.** A faction or species may open a Discipline your class does not — a death-cult opens **Necromancy**, a warrior-faith opens **Divine Might**, a hedge-witch lineage opens **Hexcraft**. Unless the grant says otherwise, a faction- or species-granted Discipline is **adjacent** (+1 PL to learn), and it follows that source's tags: an **Access**-granted Discipline closes if the relationship ends; a **Training**- or species-granted one is yours for good. This is how the setting's licensed and forbidden arts enter play: raw Necromancy is not a class-core Discipline for anyone, and a character reaches it only through a faction, a lineage, or a bargain the table will remember.

**Learning from the world** works as *How the Game Works* already describes: any power may be taught by a teacher, deed, site, or event, its PL capped by your level, its requirement payable from your permanent Alignments. Reaching into a Discipline no source has opened to you is the strongest expression of that rule and should cost the most story.

# Sample Disciplines

Written to the same power budget and voice as Volumes 1–3: roughly fifteen powers each, PL 0 through a capstone, ascending PL, requirement dots totaling PL, every required Alignment part of how the power works.

## Pyromancy

*Governing Alignment: Wild (with Machine for control, Wyrd for eruption).* Fire is not a tool the Pyromancer holds; it is an appetite they agree to feed. The list runs from an ember to a season that burns on its own. Damage is **Burn** and **Blast**. The Wild collects in excess — fire overshoots, and the body remembers being let off the leash.

- **Ember** — PL 0 — Requires None — One target within 8" takes Damage 2 Burn.

- **Kindle** — PL 1 — Requires Wild 1 — Set an object or a 2-inch space alight; the fire lights the area and lasts, dealing Damage 1 Burn to any enemy that enters it or ends a surge in it.

- **Warm the Blood** — PL 1 — Requires Wild 1 — Stance — You and adjacent allies ignore cold and gain +1 die against cold and fear; your melee hits deal +1 Burn.

- **Scorch** — PL 1 — Requires Wild 1 — One target within 10" takes Damage 3 Burn; on a wound it gains Burning (1).

- **Cinderstep** — PL 1 — Requires Wild 1 — Reaction — When you would take Burn damage or be set alight, ignore it and move 3", trailing fire the way you came.

- **Fan the Flames** — PL 2 — Requires Wild 1 + Machine 1 — Spread one existing fire — yours or the room's — to fill a 4-inch space; enemies in it take Damage 2 Burn.

- **Immolate the Blade** — PL 2 — Requires Wild 1 + Machine 1 — An ally's weapon burns for the scene: +1 Burn Damage on hits, and +1 die against plant, undead, and swarming foes.

- **Cone of Fire** — PL 2 — Requires Wild 1 + Wyrd 1 — Every enemy in a 4-inch cone takes Damage 2 Burn.

- **Backdraft** — PL 3 — Requires Wild 2 + Machine 1 — Reaction — When an enemy within 6" is Burning or standing in your fire, detonate it: that creature and each enemy adjacent to it takes Damage 3 Blast.

- **Firewalker** — PL 3 — Requires Wild 2 + Wyrd 1 — Stance — You are immune to fire and heat and move through flame freely; any enemy adjacent to you at the end of your surge takes Damage 1 Burn.

- **Pyre** — PL 4 — Requires Wild 2 + Machine 2 — A 4-inch burst within 10" becomes a standing fire for the scene: Damage 3 Burn to enter or end a surge inside, and it spreads across flammable ground.

- **Wildfire** — PL 4 — Requires Wild 2 + Wyrd 2 — One target takes Damage 4 Burn; if it wounds, the fire leaps to the nearest other enemy within 4" for Damage 2 Burn, and may leap once more from there.

- **Feed It Everything** — PL 5 — Requires Wild 3 + Wyrd 2 — Sacrifice — Take Damage 2 Burn yourself; if you do, your next Pyromancy power this surge doubles one printed Damage number and one printed distance or area.

- **The Hungry Season** — PL 6 — Requires Wild 3 + Wyrd 3 — Once per scene — A 6-inch burst erupts: Damage 5 Burn to every enemy, and the ground within it burns for the scene (Damage 2 Burn to cross). It does not politely stop at the edge you intended; anything unattended and flammable is lost.

- **Conflagration** — PL 7 — Requires Wild 4 + Wyrd 3 — Once per scene — For the scene, at the end of each round every enemy that is Burning or standing in your fire takes Damage 3 Burn and spreads Burning (1) to one adjacent creature. The fire is its own thing now; the GM plays it, and it does not care whose colors you wear.

## Necromancy

*Governing Alignment: Shadow (with Wyrd for the spirit, Machine for the binding contract).* The dead do the work the living will not. Necromancy raises servants, drinks the warmth from the wounded, and wears the grave like a coat. Damage is **Shadow** and **Toxic**. Its licensed form is the Rite-Captain's leased dead, buried properly at term; unlicensed, it is a Standing you cannot take off and a debt the Shadow collects in *who saw*.

- **Deathsight** — PL 0 — Requires None — Study a corpse or a dying creature: learn how it died, how long ago, and whether its spirit still lingers nearby.

- **Chill Touch** — PL 1 — Requires Shadow 1 — A target within 6" takes Damage 2 Shadow; if it wounds, that creature cannot benefit from Recovery or healing until your next surge.

- **Raise the Fallen** — PL 1 — Requires Shadow 1 — Troop — Where a corpse or bones lie, raise one shambler: Durability 3, Attack 2, Defend 0, Move 3"; immune to fear, poison, and fatigue; crumbles at the scene's end.

- **Siphon** — PL 1 — Requires Shadow 1 — After you damage a living creature, gain Recovery 2. The dead are generous to their own.

- **Grave-Chill** — PL 1 — Requires Shadow 1 — Stance — Enemies within 4" of you take –1 die on morale and fear saves and feel the cold coming off you.

- **Deaden the Nerve** — PL 2 — Requires Shadow 1 + Wyrd 1 — One target takes Damage 2 Shadow and loses 1 die from its next action as numbness climbs the limb.

- **Bonefield** — PL 2 — Requires Shadow 1 + Machine 1 — Where corpses or bone are present, raise two shamblers (as *Raise the Fallen*); they share your orders and your reach.

- **Mantle of Dread** — PL 2 — Requires Shadow 1 + Wyrd 1 — Stance — The first time each round an enemy attacks you, it saves or is frightened. The grave looks back.

- **Drain the Warmth** — PL 3 — Requires Shadow 2 + Wyrd 1 — One target within 10" takes Damage 3 Shadow; you or an ally within 6" gains Recovery equal to the severity step of the wound dealt.

- **Command the Dead** — PL 3 — Requires Shadow 2 + Machine 1 — Reaction — Seize one undead or reanimated creature within 10" for the round; it obeys your orders (if another necromancer holds it, it saves to resist).

- **Wither** — PL 4 — Requires Shadow 2 + Wyrd 2 — Touch or hit: Damage 3 Toxic now, and for the scene the target's Moderate and Major wound thresholds each worsen by 1 — it wounds more easily than it should.

- **Rise Again** — PL 4 — Requires Shadow 2 + Machine 2 — Stance — When one of your undead troops is destroyed with any remnant left on the field, it re-forms at the start of your next surge at half its maximum Durability, rounded up. The dead are patient.

- **The Cold Legion** — PL 5 — Requires Shadow 3 + Wyrd 2 — Once per scene — Raise four shamblers at once where the dead are to be had, or restore all your undead troops to full Durability; they serve until the scene ends.

- **Steal the Last Breath** — PL 6 — Requires Shadow 3 + Wyrd 3 — Once per scene — One creature within 10" that is Dying or below its Moderate threshold saves or suffers Death; you gain Recovery 5 and one true memory it carried. The Shadow records who watched you do it.

- **Lichfrost** — PL 7 — Requires Shadow 4 + Wyrd 3 — Once per scene — For the scene, a wound that would kill you instead reduces you to Dying and stabilizes you, your undead gain +1 die, and every living enemy within 6" takes Damage 2 Shadow at the end of each round. For one scene you stand on the far side of the door, and it shows.

## Hexcraft

*Governing Alignment: Wyrd (with Shadow for malice, Heavens for the turning of just deserts).* The hex does no damage worth the name; it makes the world stop working for someone. The evil eye, the binding word, the doom pronounced with a condition attached. Its power is denial and reversal, and the Wyrd collects in strangeness — the emotion escapes its handler, sometimes yours. An outsider art: cunning-folk, the Nol behind the mask, the Enclaves' sealed shelf.

- **Ill Wish** — PL 0 — Requires None — One creature within 10" takes –1 die on its next roll. A small, spiteful nudge.

- **The Evil Eye** — PL 1 — Requires Wyrd 1 — Stance — While you hold one visible enemy's gaze, it takes –1 die on all rolls against you specifically; it may break the gaze only by spending an action to look away.

- **Fumblefingers** — PL 1 — Requires Wyrd 1 — Reaction — When an enemy within 10" makes an attack or Knack roll, its 6s count as failures for that roll. The hex catches the hand at the wrong moment.

- **Sour the Wound** — PL 1 — Requires Wyrd 1 — A creature you damaged this surge cannot benefit from Recovery or healing until your next surge.

- **Whispers** — PL 1 — Requires Shadow 1 — One target saves or is unsettled until your next surge: –1 die on its rolls, and it may take no Reactions.

- **Binding Word** — PL 2 — Requires Wyrd 1 + Shadow 1 — Speak a creature's name or true epithet: it saves or, until your next surge, cannot move closer to you or cannot use one action type of your choice (its pick of which restriction only if it saves).

- **Jinx** — PL 2 — Requires Wyrd 1 + Shadow 1 — Curse a creature; once before your next surge, force it to reroll a successful roll and keep the worse result.

- **Crawling Unease** — PL 2 — Requires Wyrd 1 + Heavens 1 — A 4-inch burst: enemies there save or take –1 die and cannot receive allies' aid until the end of the round. No one wants to stand near them.

- **The Long Curse** — PL 3 — Requires Wyrd 2 + Shadow 1 — Lay a curse that lasts the scene: choose one — the target's attacks lose 1 die, its saves lose 1 die, or its Move drops 2". Only a remove-curse effect or the curse's own stated condition ends it early.

- **Turn the Luck** — PL 3 — Requires Wyrd 2 + Heavens 1 — Reaction — When an enemy scores an unusual success or a telling blow against your side, spend the hex to reduce it to an ordinary success or hit. What the world handed them, the world takes back.

- **Wracking Hex** — PL 4 — Requires Wyrd 2 + Shadow 2 — One target takes Damage 3 Wyrd; at the start of each of its surges it loses 1 die (to a maximum of –3) until it succeeds on a save to throw off the curse.

- **Mislaid Fate** — PL 4 — Requires Wyrd 2 + Heavens 2 — Stance — Once per round, when an enemy within 10" would hit an ally, redirect that hit's misfortune onto a different enemy within 6" of the original target instead.

- **The Withering Word** — PL 5 — Requires Wyrd 3 + Shadow 2 — Once per scene — Curse one creature thoroughly: for the scene it takes –2 dice on all rolls, cannot invoke Alignments, and when it rolls badly its own powers may rebound on it, as the GM sees fit.

- **Doom Spoken** — PL 6 — Requires Wyrd 3 + Shadow 3 — Once per scene — Pronounce a doom on one creature with a named condition ("until you kneel," "before the sun sets"). While the doom holds, it cannot score more than 1 success on any roll against your side. Meeting the condition, or a remove-curse of equal weight, ends it. The Wyrd remembers the exact wording; so should you.

- **The Whole Town Curdles** — PL 7 — Requires Wyrd 4 + Shadow 3 — Once per scene — Every enemy in sight saves or takes a lingering curse of your choice (as *The Long Curse*) for the scene; any that fail badly also turn on whatever stands nearest to them for one round. The emotion escapes its handler — and the handler is you.

## Divine Might

*Governing Alignment: Heavens (with Wild for the vigor faith lends the body).* Not the healer's grace and not the lamp against the dark, but the third face of faith: strength. The blessed arm, the righteous charge, the body that endures because the spirit refuses to let it stop. Damage is **Heavens**. The Heavens collect in obligation — the mercy you must now extend, the oath the light will hold you to.

- **Blessed Arm** — PL 0 — Requires None — Your next melee attack this surge counts as Heavens-aligned and gains +1 Attack Die.

- **Girded** — PL 1 — Requires Heavens 1 — Stance — Improve your Armor save by 1; you cannot be frightened while you stand in defense of another.

- **Smite** — PL 1 — Requires Heavens 1 — Strike with +1 die; against an undead, Shadow-aligned, or oathbreaking target it deals +2 Damage as Heavens.

- **Lay On the Strength** — PL 1 — Requires Heavens 1 — Touch an ally: their next physical roll gains +2 dice, and they clear one fatigue or fear effect.

- **Unbowed** — PL 1 — Requires Heavens 1 — Reaction — When a hit would knock you prone, push you, or wound you while you defend an ally, reduce it one step or severity; you do not give ground.

- **Radiant Blow** — PL 2 — Requires Heavens 1 + Wild 1 — Strike; on a hit the target sheds light, cannot hide, and takes Damage 2 Heavens.

- **The Body Endures** — PL 2 — Requires Heavens 1 + Wild 1 — Stance — While you carry a wound, gain +1 die on all physical rolls and +1" Move. The spirit spends the flesh.

- **Shield of Faith** — PL 2 — Requires Heavens 1 + Machine 1 — Reaction — Improve an ally's Armor save by 2 against one attack; if it still wounds, take 1 step of its severity in their place.

- **Righteous Charge** — PL 3 — Requires Heavens 2 + Wild 1 — Move up to double your Move in a line and Strike: +2 dice, and on a hit the target is knocked prone; allies you pass gain +1 die until your next surge.

- **Anointed** — PL 3 — Requires Heavens 2 + Wild 1 — Stance — Your weapon counts as Heavens-aligned, ignores the Armor of undead and Shadow-aligned foes, and deals +1 Damage to them.

- **Aegis** — PL 4 — Requires Heavens 2 + Machine 2 — Once per scene — Until your next surge, you and allies within 4" reduce every hit by 1 and cannot be forcibly moved. You have set your feet in the light.

- **Wrath Made Just** — PL 4 — Requires Heavens 2 + Wild 2 — One target within 10" takes Damage N Heavens, where N is 3 — or 5 if it has harmed an ally or innocent in front of you this scene. The judgment is specific, and so is the debt.

- **The Strength of Ten** — PL 5 — Requires Heavens 3 + Wild 2 — Once per scene — For the scene, your physical rolls gain +2 dice, your melee attacks deal +2 Damage, and you may lift, break, or hold what ten could not. The grace is loud, and it is watching how you spend it.

- **Champion's Stand** — PL 6 — Requires Heavens 3 + Wild 3 — Once per scene — For the scene, the first wound each round that would make you Dying is reduced one severity, your attacks against the scene's worst evil gain +2 dice, and allies who can see you clear fear each round. Today, you are the answer that was prayed for.

- **Descend the Vapor** — PL 7 — Requires Heavens 4 + Wild 3 — Once per scene — Call the light down: a 6-inch burst deals Damage 5 Heavens to Shadow-aligned and undead enemies and Damage 2 Heavens to any others; your whole side clears fear and gains Recovery 3; and one honest oath you swear now binds the Heavens as witness. The obligation is real — the light will expect you to keep faith with what you called down.
