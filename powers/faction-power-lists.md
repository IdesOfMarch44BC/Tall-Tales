# Faction Power Lists

Faction powers represent the training an institution drilled into you (**Training** — yours forever), the person it made you to everyone else (**Standing** — retained only while the reputation survives), and the doors it opens (**Access** — which close when the relationship ends). At creation, learn one PL 0 or PL 1 power from your faction or the Unaffiliated list.

**Power format:** **Name** — PL # — Requires Alignment # — tags — effect. Mixed requirements join Alignments with a plus sign. The required dots total the PL; PL 0 powers require none.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Witnesses of the Lion

- **The Sigil Opens Doors** — PL 1 — Requires Heavens 1 — Access — Any funded church: shelter, provisions, and backing for you and companions; +2 dice on social rolls with congregations. The Open Eye is a promise, and you are it.

- **Do Not Stop Seeing** — PL 1 — Requires Heavens 1 — Training, Stance — +2 dice against fear, and no effect can make you look away, unsee, or abstract what is in front of you.

- **Keep the Vigil** — PL 1 — Requires Heavens 1 — Training — After a hard scene, your side sits together and speaks honestly of what they saw: everyone gains Recovery 2 and clears one fear or despair effect. Pretense voids it, and the power knows.

- **The Circuit Knows** — PL 2 — Requires Heavens 1 + Wild 1 — Access — Ask the Riders’ web: one true piece of intelligence about any person, place, or pattern on a circuit — because a Rider noticed, three visits ago.

- **Answered from the Vapor** — PL 5 — Requires Heavens 3 + Wild 2 — Once per scene, Standing — Pray honestly at real need. The GM sends proportionate aid: a true vision, a surge granting every ally Recovery 3, or a celestial presence at the scene’s edge.

## Noble Houses

- **Recognized Blood** — PL 1 — Requires Shadow 1 — Standing — Your name is in a book that cannot be counterfeited: +2 dice with any House, and your identity is provable anywhere the Concord’s word reaches.

- **The Demesne Stone** — PL 1 — Requires Shadow 1 — Access — Within any allied demesne: sanctuary — writs stop at the stone, pursuit stalls, and the House’s intercession covers your party while you’re its guest.

- **Call the Sworn** — PL 2 — Requires Shadow 1 + Wild 1 — Access — Once per session, retainers answer: two sworn (Durability 3, Attack 2, Defend 1, Move 8") for a scene, or one specialist service.

- **Name-Cup Courtesy** — PL 2 — Requires Shadow 1 + Wild 1 — Standing — Invoke guest-right: a House-affiliated host must shelter your party this night and offer no harm beneath their roof. Both of you will remember which of you honored it better.

- **Intercession** — PL 5 — Requires Shadow 3 + Wild 2 — Once per scene, Access — The House formally stands between you and one institution: a writ suspended, a contract stayed, a pursuit called off — followed, in due course, by the House’s own accounting with *you*, which is not a power.

## Academic Enclaves

- **The Grey** — PL 1 — Requires Machine 1 — Standing — The credential that tells a nervous room what it is looking at: +2 dice with officials and scholars, and frightened communities treat you as the answer arriving, not the problem.

- **Protocol Training** — PL 1 — Requires Machine 1 — Training — Identify a supernatural effect with a Study roll; against effects you’ve identified, you and adjacent allies gain +2 dice on Alignment saves. Vellhame is why the drills exist.

- **The Informant Web** — PL 2 — Requires Machine 1 + Wyrd 1 — Access — In any settlement: learn what the local informants know — the strange child, the cold room, the wrongness with a street address. The GM gives the true summary.

- **Registry Consultation** — PL 2 — Requires Machine 1 + Wyrd 1 — Access — Query the case files: one documented answer about any supernatural phenomenon the Enclaves have ever recorded — instantly near a chartered hall, at correspondence speed otherwise.

- **The Attendance Owed** — PL 5 — Requires Machine 3 + Wyrd 2 — Once per scene, Standing — Call in what the tradition owes you: an Enclave sends real help, once — a field Adept for a scene, the warding of a site, or the Office of Returns quietly taking one problem off your hands.

## Monastic Orders

- **The Gate Behind You** — PL 1 — Requires Wyrd 1 — Access — Any monastery of any Order: shelter, healing, and sanctuary for you and companions, absolute, fed from the first evening. The gate is open. It was always the oldest vow.

- **The Hours** — PL 1 — Requires Wyrd 1 — Training, Stance — The discipline, kept daily: +1 die against fear, deception, and mind-effects, and you wake instantly, completely, on cue.

- **Sit With It** — PL 2 — Requires Wyrd 1 + Heavens 1 — Training — Guide an ally through a quiet beat: clear one mental affliction, or grant +2 dice on their next Alignment save. Works on yourself, slower.

- **A Walker’s Introduction** — PL 2 — Requires Wyrd 1 + Heavens 1 — Access — Once per session, a walker or gate-friend of the tradition is within a day’s reach: a teaching, a message carried, or one plain favor, repaid in the tradition’s currency — which is more teaching.

- **The Examiners’ Seal** — PL 5 — Requires Wyrd 3 + Heavens 2 — Once per scene, Standing — Still Water vouches: one accusation, possession-claim, or question of mind-tampering concerning you or an ally is formally examined and settled — because they checked, and it is true, and everyone knows what their checking means.

## Landed Guilds

- **The Mark** — PL 1 — Requires Machine 1 — Standing — Your work carries a hall’s counter-stamp: +2 dice in commerce and negotiation over craft, and what you make sells at an honest price anywhere the Assay’s word runs.

- **Hall Rights** — PL 1 — Requires Machine 1 — Access — Any Guildhall: workshop and bench, materials at cost, a bed, and the notice board — the federation’s whole physical plant, open to your mark.

- **Journeyman’s Welcome** — PL 2 — Requires Machine 1 + Wild 1 — Standing — Craft-kin everywhere: tradesfolk of any guild assist, gossip, and warn you, and with proper tools you gain +1 die on Knack and Study.

- **Case-Library Letter** — PL 2 — Requires Machine 1 + Wild 1 — Access — Query the libraries: one validated-procedure answer — how to make it, fix it, neutralize it, and what it did to the last person who tried — instantly at a hall, by post otherwise.

- **The Table’s Weight** — PL 5 — Requires Machine 3 + Wild 2 — Once per scene, Access — The federation leans, once, where you point: a supplier embargo, an Assay audit, a strike-hour — against any target of Guild-dependent scale. Sell to all, sworn to none; the Table will want this favor back.

## Mercenary Companies

- **On the Rolls** — PL 1 — Requires Machine 1 — Standing — Registered colors: +2 dice negotiating any paper, and the Convention’s protections — arbitration, parley custom, the honored surrender — apply to you and yours.

- **The Company Kit** — PL 1 — Requires Machine 1 — Access — Requisition against the contract: field gear, mounts, ammunition, and rations for an engagement’s duration, quartermaster’s glare included.

- **Read the Paper** — PL 2 — Requires Machine 1 + Heavens 1 — Training — Assess any contract, job, or standoff actuarially: the GM tells you the true risk, the coverage gaps, and who actually benefits. You cannot unlearn this. Most veterans have stopped trying.

- **Comrades in the Trade** — PL 2 — Requires Machine 1 + Heavens 1 — Standing — Once per session, colors you’ve served beside are near: intelligence, one night’s reinforcement, or a covered retreat — answered for, as you’d answer for yours.

- **The Incursion Pool** — PL 5 — Requires Machine 3 + Heavens 2 — Once per scene, Access — Declare a catastrophe of the scale the Pool exists for, with evidence: the industry answers — a funded relief column within days — and your name goes in the report, for better and worse.

## Trade Companies

- **Company Papers** — PL 1 — Requires Machine 1 — Access — Free passage on your Company’s lines, freight at cost, and the station clock’s certified time as your alibi.

- **The Factor’s Desk** — PL 1 — Requires Machine 1 — Access — In any town on the flow, the factor receives you: work, the local truth, and a small budget for problems — yours, if you’re the solution.

- **Read the Spread** — PL 2 — Requires Machine 1 + Shadow 1 — Training — Prices talk to you: from a chalkboard and an hour in the market, learn a settlement’s true condition — who’s solvent, who’s desperate, what’s about to break.

- **Expense Account** — PL 2 — Requires Machine 1 + Shadow 1 — Access — Draw operational Funds (GM-capped, scaled to your standing). It is audited, eventually, and the audit has a memory and a tone.

- **The Principal’s Ear** — PL 5 — Requires Machine 3 + Shadow 2 — Once per scene, Standing — One message reaches a principal directly, and is *read*: a warning heeded, a deal tabled, a door opened at the top of the register. What you said is now policy’s problem — and so, in a small permanent way, are you.

## Bounty Registries

- **The Seal-Tag** — PL 1 — Requires Shadow 1 — Standing — Certified: +2 dice with lawfolk and officials, your testimony is evidence, and your captures are legal in every jurisdiction on your rings.

- **Board Privileges** — PL 1 — Requires Shadow 1 — Access — Read writs before posting, reserve one against competitors, and pull the inquest file on any posted name.

- **Adjudicator’s Question** — PL 2 — Requires Shadow 1 + Wyrd 1 — Access — Once per session, a bench answers one question a Registry could know: names, priors, who posted what, and when the paper moved.

- **Escrow Draw** — PL 2 — Requires Shadow 1 + Wyrd 1 — Access — Advance against active paper: pursuit Funds now, settled at collection — and the bench’s arithmetic if there is no collection.

- **Exchange Reciprocity** — PL 5 — Requires Shadow 3 + Wyrd 2 — Once per scene, Standing — Invoke the full Exchange: a name flagged at every board in reach, a safe-conduct honored across jurisdictions, or a verified writ expedited to posting in a single day. Right name, whole road — and every bench watching how you use it.

## Frontier Wardens

- **Sign-Literate** — PL 1 — Requires Wild 1 — Training — Read and leave Warden sign in full: the public strokes, the network strokes, and the weight of a hand. The *do not* glyph has saved more lives than any wall; now it reports to you.

- **The No-Iron Manner** — PL 1 — Requires Wild 1 — Training — The kit and the carriage both: in wild country, creatures’ first response to you is assessment rather than attack, and you gain +2 dice on first contact with any Wild being.

- **Relay Access** — PL 2 — Requires Wild 1 + Heavens 1 — Access — The caches and the lines: resupply in deep country, and send messages that travel *whole* — unopened, unedited, unrefused — to anywhere the network reaches.

- **Vouched at the Gather** — PL 2 — Requires Wild 1 + Heavens 1 — Standing — The network answers for you: any Warden extends working trust on sight, and once per session a guide, contact, or bed at a relay post is within reach.

- **Treaty Words** — PL 5 — Requires Wild 3 + Heavens 2 — Once per scene, Access — Invoke a standing treaty’s protocols before a sovereign’s court or creatures: hostilities pause for one parley the Wild will honor — spoken on the network’s credit, which is three centuries of taking *no* for an answer and coming back polite. Spend it like what it is.

## Unaffiliated

The unaffiliated learn from the list below. None has the Access tag: what the loose world teaches cannot be revoked, but it opens no institutional door. This is the Unaffiliated list, not the Drifter class list.

- **Beholden to None** — PL 1 — Requires Wild 1 — Training, Stance — +2 dice against coercion, recruitment pressure, and any social leverage that assumes you answer to someone. You don’t. It shows, and it costs them dice to keep forgetting.

- **Odd Jobs** — PL 1 — Requires Wild 1 — Training — You’ve done everything once: once per scene, treat a skill you have 0 rating in as rating 1, or gain +1 die on any genuinely improvised approach.

- **The Word Travels** — PL 1 — Requires Wild 1 — Standing — Your reputation is your only mark, carried mouth to mouth: +1 die on social rolls anywhere your deeds are honestly known — and the GM keeps the Reputation line, so keep the deeds worth carrying.

- **The Loose Web** — PL 2 — Requires Wild 1 + Wyrd 1 — Standing — Once per session: a fixer, tavern-keeper, road-friend, or fellow freelancer knows you — a job lead, a warning, a floor to sleep on — paid in kind, remembered honestly, owed nothing else.

- **Nobody’s Flag** — PL 5 — Requires Wild 3 + Wyrd 2 — Once per scene, Training — In a conflict between institutions, both sides accept you as the neutral hand: carry the parley, hold the stakes, walk between the lines untouched for a scene. No sworn, marked, chartered, or sigiled soul can ever use this power. It is the one thing being no one’s buys, and there are days on this frontier when it is worth more than everything it cost.

## Table Notes

**The tags are the drama.** Training/Standing/Access makes leaving a faction a mechanical event the whole table can see: the severed Lion still cannot look away (*Do Not Stop Seeing* is theirs forever), may carry a stained version of the sigil’s meaning if the Standing survives, and finds the church doors shut when Access is gone. Resolve every faction power the character actually knows on-screen.

**Access is the GM’s throttle.** Every Access power is an invitation to the faction’s chapter — *The Circuit Knows* opens the Riders’ web, *Treaty Words* opens a sovereign’s court — and using one should always cost a little story: the favor noted, the ledger updated, the institution’s interest in the party incremented. Faction powers are how the factions watch their members back.

**The capstones are the institutions at full weight.** Each PL 5 signature is the faction doing the thing only it can do — the Table leaning, the Pool answering, the Exchange speaking with one voice — and each leaves a residue the chapters price in detail. A party that has used three of them owes three institutions, and a campaign can be steered for a year on which debts come due in what order.
