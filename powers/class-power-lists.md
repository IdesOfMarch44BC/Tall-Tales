# Class Archetype Overview

## How Power Lists Work

Every class teaches more than any one member learns. A class’s repertoire is divided into **archetype lists**. Your class determines how many powers you know at creation; choose that many PL 0 or PL 1 powers from your archetype. From level 2 onward, you may cross-train into sibling archetypes of your class; treat each cross-trained power as one PL higher than printed when checking eligibility. This rule now applies to all ten classes, the three in Volume 3 included.

**Power format:** **Name** — PL # — Requires Alignment # — tags — effect. Mixed requirements join Alignments with a plus sign. The required dots total the PL; PL 0 powers require none.

Bold class concepts in these overviews describe a calling’s fiction and power themes; they are not additional tracked resources unless a stat line or power gives them an explicit rule.

An archetype is not a subclass wall; it is a center of gravity. Two characters of one class but different lists remain recognizably the same calling and unmistakably different people. The complete playable lists live in the power volumes: **Volumes 1–2** hold Witness through Sawbone, and **Volume 3** holds the Captain, Bounty Hunter, and Shapeshifter. The archetype descriptions below are the reading map for all three volumes.

## Witness (Witnesses of the Lion)

Faith made practical. Witnesses call the source of their prayer a **Spark of Faith**; it is a description, not a point reserve. Witness powers express divine auras, grace, counsel, the Circuit, endurance, miracles, and the authority earned by carrying the word from community to community. A power with **Sacrifice** gains only the stronger effect printed on that power when its stated cost is accepted.

### The Line-Holder

*The formation’s anchor — the one who is not moving, so that others don’t have to be brave.* Playstyle: defense, protection, punishing enemies who ignore you.

Line-Holder prayers project the Witness’s divine aura. Powers marked **Sacrifice** may be strengthened by accepting their stated cost.





### The Intercessor

*The formation’s honest voice — prayer as logistics, aid arriving because it was asked for truthfully.* Playstyle: support, healing, buying allies’ successes.

The Intercessor develops Touch of Grace and Counsel: faith carried through contact, truthful speech, and a willingness to bear part of another person’s hurt.





### The Lamplighter

*The burning end of witness — the one sent where the dark is thickest, to make it regret the attention.* Playstyle: striker against the Shadow’s works; light as a weapon.

The Lamplighter turns the Spark of Faith toward miracles, sacred presence, and carrying the word into places where ordinary prayer cannot reach.

## Drifter (Independent Road Tradition)

The road’s self-made legend. A Drifter’s abilities do not come from an institution, only **Deeds** already done, the **Legend** those deeds have produced, and **Stances** learned by surviving. This does not determine faction membership: a Drifter may be affiliated or unaffiliated like any other class. Drifter powers reward independence, danger sense, impossible timing, quick movement, stoicism, and the final stand; their best effects become stronger when the Drifter is isolated, outnumbered, or acting on a reputation the table has seen them earn.

### The Lone Wolf

*The survivor who learned not to expect rescue.* Playstyle: self-reliance, stoicism, and becoming harder to finish when isolated or outnumbered.





### The Living Legend

*A reputation with boots on.* Playstyle: invoke Deeds, force enemies to account for your Legend, and turn remembered exploits into present leverage.





### The Road Ghost

*Distance and bad timing have tried before.* Playstyle: danger sense, quick steps, evasion, impossible timing, and being gone before the trap admits it failed.

## Arcanist (Academic Enclaves)

Erupted power, stewarded: every Arcanist carries a volatile eruption called a **Spark** and shapes spell powers through training rather than innate safety. An Arcanist should describe an **Arcane Focus**—a battery, familiar, lens, or chained book—but it has no separate statistic unless a power gives it one. Higher powers concern ritual casting, arcane sight, efficient casting, overcasting, spell shaping, and becoming a living conduit.

### The Ward-Wright

*The useful Enclave, in one person — containment as a combat art.* Playstyle: barriers, zones, control; the battlefield does what you chalk.





### The Open Channel

*The eruption, ridden — output the protocols call inadvisable and the moment calls necessary.* Playstyle: blaster; push-your-luck damage that spends your own body.





### The Book-Bound

*The inheritance, leaned on — forty dead masters between two chained covers, consulted mid-fight.* Playstyle: versatility and flexible Alignment requirements; the answer is in the book.

## Dynast (Noble Houses)

The blood, expressed: every Dynast inherits a **House Lineage** whose **Manifestation** may be quiet or unmistakable. Dynast powers produce the bloodline’s strange anatomy and influence; there is no default on/off action or separate manifestation state unless a power creates one. Using a visible bloodline effect may still expose what the character is in the fiction.

### The Thirsting Line (Corvara-pattern)

*Speed past watching comfortably, and a debt the body collects.* Playstyle: skirmisher; hit-and-gone; blood as resource.





### The Grown Line (Ostrogar-pattern)

*Living armament — plate that thickens where you were struck.* Playstyle: juggernaut; grows harder as the fight goes on.





### The Living Iron (Ferrant-pattern)

*Flesh of frame-metal — a body that banks the charge.* Playstyle: durable battery; stored vim spent as violence or utility.





### The Drowned Line (Ophara-pattern)

*Scale, claw, and blood that fights back.* Playstyle: controller-bruiser; punishes attackers; owns the water.

## Mentalist (Monastic Orders)

The cultivated mind turned outward. A Mentalist learns **Psychic Disciplines** and manifests thought as force. Many externalize part of the practice through a psicrystal or similar focus. Their powers emphasize mind blades, telepathy, probing touch, composure, hidden manifestation, mind over matter, and the watching mind. Only a power bearing **Overload** can be overloaded, using the stronger effect and cost printed on that power; there is no general strain meter.

### The Mind Blade

*Thought given an edge.* Playstyle: manifest a psychic weapon, cut material and supernatural workings, and turn disciplined intent into close violence.





### The Watching Mind

*The inward way — the still mind, and other minds, approached lawfully.* Playstyle: telepathy, probing touch, hidden manifestation, control, and insight.





### The Composed Self

*A mind trained until flesh, fear, and deprivation become negotiable.* Playstyle: mental discipline, sustenance, resilience, mind over matter, and calm support under pressure.

## Tinkerer (Landed Guilds)

Engineering as frontier magic. A Tinkerer prepares repeatable **Principles**, chooses a technical specialization, and should describe a signature **Prototype Device**. That prototype is character fiction until equipment or a power supplies its profile. Powers cover engineering insight, rapid and spectral construction, discoveries, infrastructure networks, impossible materials, living machinery, and systems that grow from a clever tool into part of the world’s architecture.

### The Coilwright

*Energetics — vim charged, kept, and delivered on schedule.* Playstyle: zone damage, batteries, powering the party’s world.





### The Crucible-Trained

*Alchemy — matter argued into new opinions.* Playstyle: grenades, acids, transmutation; the environment is ammunition.





### The Frame-Wright

*Spagyrics — the living machine, grown, gentled, and yours.* Playstyle: companion archetype; a small chassis fights beside you.





------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Sawbone (Landed Guilds)

The Guild’s answer to the body. A Sawbone practices **Organic Science**, reads anatomy with frightening precision, and turns Discoveries into emergency treatment, surgical alterations, grafts, mutagens, hostile procedures, and constructed servants. Their work can preserve life, redesign it, or make the distinction professionally inconvenient.

### The Red Thread

*Chirurgeon-trained — surgery under fire, anatomy as a map.* Playstyle: the party’s true healer; precision offense in a pinch.





### The Preparatory

*Green Chain–trained — tinctures, stims, and poultices, brewed ahead of need.* Playstyle: pre-battle buffer; consumables as party economy.





### The Toxicologist

*Crucible-trained — the dose makes the poison, and you did the math.* Playstyle: debuffer; the enemy’s numbers quietly stop working.

## Captain (Mercenary Companies)

The tactician and the muster. A Captain builds **Legend** through Deeds and spends powers on commands: tactical coordination, swift orders, coordinated assaults, assessments, and authority that grows from a squad’s trust into strategic influence. **Prepared** powers are flashbacks—a tool, contact, route, or order established earlier because the Captain planned for this.

### The Creche Captain

*Cloned brothers — expensive, capable, and people, whatever the Rolls decide.* Playstyle: elite small squad; troops that flank, hold, and grieve.

### The Rite-Captain

*The leased dead — tireless, fearless, contract-bound, and buried properly at term.* Playstyle: attrition; the wall that does not eat, sleep, or exceed the order.

### The Verdant Captain

*The grown line — patience, thorns, and fortifications that heal.* Playstyle: area denial; the map slowly becomes yours.

### The Chimeric Captain

*The beast-lines — bred for the work, directed by the hand.* Playstyle: mobility and scouting; the pack sees first and arrives first.

### The Free Captain

*Mortal colors — a handful of volunteers, and the trust nothing made can buy.* Playstyle: leadership; no troops — the party is the muster.

## Bounty Hunter (Registries)

The certified pursuit. Every Bounty Hunter begins with a current writ and manifests a **Mind Blade** or **Mind Gun** tuned to pursuit. A creature becomes the Hunter’s quarry only when a power such as *Take the Name* designates it; “Marked Prey” is not a free universal bonus. Powers build psychic strike, the perfect draw, ghost steps, uncanny tracking, impossible pursuit, and the inescapable pressure of a hunter who can follow what a mind leaves behind.

### The Wake-Reader

*Depth as doctrine — the Hunter who knows the quarry better than the quarry does.* Playstyle: single-target supremacy; information becomes dice.

### The Taker

*Terms: to answer, to hold — the endgame specialist who delivers them breathing.* Playstyle: ambush and capture; the fight is over before it’s a fight.

### The Veiled

*The counter-hunter — warded, masked, and rude to things that swim the dreaming.* Playstyle: anti-psychic and defense; hunts what hunts by familiarity.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Shapeshifter (Frontier Wardens)

Embodied transformation. A Shapeshifter learns a **Form** from a specific creature whose heart or willing bond they captured—never a generic animal selected from a list. The form brings its movement, natural weapons, senses, strengths, hungers, and limits. Powers develop bestial instinct, rapid transformation, wild adaptation, hybrid traits, impossible anatomy, and the long road toward perfect shape and living myth.

### The Pelt-Binder

*Few shapes, deeply held — the woman who is a wolf, and has been its colleague thirty years.* Playstyle: committed transformation; the bound shape at full power.

### The Hearth-Walker

*Many light guests — the cat for the town, the hawk for the sky, the horse for the road.* Playstyle: versatility; the right shape at the right instant.

### The Face-Taker

*The town is also a landscape — humanoid shapes, under the code.* Playstyle: infiltration and social control; the network’s inside work.

## Table Notes

**Archetype is silhouette.** A list should be readable across a dark room: the Line-Holder plants, the Open Channel glows, the Rite-Captain’s wall does not breathe. Protect each archetype’s verbs when adding powers.

**The lists carry the lore.** Every archetype above is an institution’s fingerprint, and several powers deliberately point at live setting questions — the frame that chooses, the thorn line and its recall, the brother who asks, the dead man’s lease closed on-screen. GMs should let those powers be doors: a Verdant Captain using *Third Line’s Answer* has invited the Waking Made to their table, and the game intends exactly that.

**Authority.** The volume files are authoritative for all ten classes: Volumes 1–2 for Witness through Sawbone, and Volume 3 for the Captain, Bounty Hunter, and Shapeshifter. With Volume 3 written, the three newest classes learn their starting powers from their archetype’s PL 0 and PL 1 entries and cross-train under the standard rule, exactly like the others. All lists remain open to playtest tuning.
