# Class Archetype Overview

> **Conversion index:** This chapter preserves the classes' themes and old archetype silhouettes while their entries are rewritten for the five power sources. The three volume files are legacy effect banks, not current mechanical authority. Use [Power Sources and Disciplines](disciplines.md) for the rules each class now follows.

## How to Read the Old Archetypes

An archetype is a content lane, not a separate power source or subclass wall. Its names, images, and playstyle remain conversion guidance. Its old universal costs, Overload text, instant preparations, and generic activation rules do not.

| Source | Classes | Converted entries |
|---|---|---|
| Legend | Captain, Drifter | Stances and core-action exploits |
| Magic | Arcanist, Witness | Spells, Observances, and forget rolls |
| Occultism | Dynast, Shapeshifter | Individual patron gifts and form contracts |
| Psionics | Bounty Hunter, Mentalist | Broad techniques with d100 result ladders |
| Superscience | Sawbone, Tinkerer | Construction principles and fabricated devices |

Every class teaches more than any one member learns. Characters choose the class's normal number of PL 0 or PL 1 source entries at creation and may learn beyond that menu through play. Two characters of one class can still use the archetypes below as centers of gravity without acquiring different subsystems.

## Witness (Witnesses of the Lion)

Faith made practical. A Witness channels extraplanar Magic through revelation, prayer, and direct attention from the Heavens. Their spells express grace, counsel, endurance, miracles, and the authority earned by carrying the word from community to community. They spend Alignment, may ignore a spell's Observances at the cost of a Twist, and risk forgetting each spell after it resolves. The higher force bears the working's physical mass, so a Witness does not accumulate Arcane Burden.

### The Line-Holder

*The formation’s anchor — the one who is not moving, so that others don’t have to be brave.* Playstyle: defense, protection, punishing enemies who ignore you.

Line-Holder prayers project the Witness’s divine aura. Powers marked **Sacrifice** may be strengthened by accepting their stated cost.





### The Intercessor

*The formation’s honest voice — prayer as logistics, aid arriving because it was asked for truthfully.* Playstyle: support, healing, buying allies’ successes.

The Intercessor develops Touch of Grace and Counsel: faith carried through contact, truthful speech, and a willingness to bear part of another person’s hurt.





### The Lamplighter

*The burning end of witness — the one sent where the dark is thickest, to make it regret the attention.* Playstyle: striker against the Shadow’s works; light as a weapon.

The Lamplighter turns the Spark of Faith toward miracles, sacred presence, and carrying the word into places where ordinary prayer cannot reach.

## Drifter (Independent Road Tradition)

The road's self-made Legend. A Drifter's abilities do not come from an institution, only Deeds already done, the reputation those deeds produced, stances learned by surviving, and exploits practiced until they look impossible. This does not determine faction membership. Drifter entries reward independence, danger sense, movement, stoicism, and the final stand, and always attach to a core action or maintained fighting method.

### The Lone Wolf

*The survivor who learned not to expect rescue.* Playstyle: self-reliance, stoicism, and becoming harder to finish when isolated or outnumbered.





### The Living Legend

*A reputation with boots on.* Playstyle: invoke Deeds, force enemies to account for your Legend, and turn remembered exploits into present leverage.





### The Road Ghost

*Distance and bad timing have tried before.* Playstyle: danger sense, quick steps, evasion, impossible timing, and being gone before the trap admits it failed.

## Arcanist (Academic Enclaves)

Erupted Magic, stewarded: every Arcanist carries a volatile eruption called a **Spark** and shapes extraplanar energy through training rather than innate safety. Each casting deposits literal matter and energy in the caster as measurable **arcane mass**, whose weight prevents Arcanists from benefiting from worn Armor. An Arcanist should describe an Arcane Focus—a battery, familiar, lens, or chained book—but it has no separate statistic unless a spell gives it one. Arcanists learn many spells, meet or defy their Observances, and risk forgetting each one after it resolves.

### The Ward-Wright

*The useful Enclave, in one person — containment as a combat art.* Playstyle: barriers, zones, control; the battlefield does what you chalk.





### The Open Channel

*The eruption, ridden — output the protocols call inadvisable and the moment calls necessary.* Playstyle: blaster; push-your-luck damage that spends your own body.





### The Book-Bound

*The inheritance, leaned on — forty dead masters between two chained covers, consulted mid-fight.* Playstyle: versatility and flexible Alignment requirements; the answer is in the book.

## Dynast (Noble Houses)

The House bargain, inherited: every Dynast belongs to a **Lineage** with access to mighty patrons. Blood makes the introduction and a personal contract opens a patron's gift list, but the patron relationship itself is a class feature rather than a source selection. Each selection learns one individual gift from an available patron list. The Dynast stakes that patron's Bond to channel its gifts, follows the borrowed source procedure, and gains a permanent physical Manifestation for every gift learned—even while the gift is inactive or suppressed. The lineage-pattern archetypes below are prompts for patron gift lists, not powers received automatically from ancestry.

### The Thirsting Line (Corvara-pattern)

*Speed past watching comfortably, and a debt the body collects.* Playstyle: skirmisher; hit-and-gone; blood as resource.





### The Grown Line (Ostrogar-pattern)

*Living armament — plate that thickens where you were struck.* Playstyle: juggernaut; grows harder as the fight goes on.





### The Living Iron (Ferrant-pattern)

*Flesh of frame-metal — a body that banks the charge.* Playstyle: durable battery; stored vim spent as violence or utility.





### The Drowned Line (Ophara-pattern)

*Scale, claw, and blood that fights back.* Playstyle: controller-bruiser; punishes attackers; owns the water.

## Mentalist (Monastic Orders)

The cultivated mind turned outward. A Mentalist learns broad **Psychic Disciplines** and manifests thought as force. Many externalize part of the practice through a psicrystal or similar focus. Each technique spends Alignment, then rolls d100 plus a chosen Push up to level (maximum 20); approaching 100 strengthens the manifestation, while exceeding it produces the technique's Overrun.

### The Far Hand

*Thought extended into the material world.* Playstyle: psychokinesis, barriers, constructs, and force applied with monastic precision. The Bounty Hunter's Mind Weapon is a separate class feature.





### The Watching Mind

*The inward way — the still mind, and other minds, approached lawfully.* Playstyle: telepathy, probing touch, hidden manifestation, control, and insight.





### The Composed Self

*A mind trained until flesh, fear, and deprivation become negotiable.* Playstyle: mental discipline, sustenance, resilience, mind over matter, and calm support under pressure.

## Tinkerer (Landed Guilds)

Engineering as frontier superscience. A Tinkerer learns repeatable **Principles** and combines them into devices. Each application requires real inputs, appropriate permanent Alignment, and hours of work; the completed apparatus supplies the encounter effect when an operator spends or commits its Operating Requirement. Principles cover energy, alchemy, frames, impossible materials, living machinery, wayfinding, and systems that grow from a clever tool into infrastructure.

### The Coilwright

*Energetics — vim charged, kept, and delivered on schedule.* Playstyle: zone damage, batteries, powering the party’s world.





### The Crucible-Trained

*Alchemy — matter argued into new opinions.* Playstyle: grenades, acids, transmutation; the environment is ammunition.





### The Frame-Wright

*Spagyrics — the living machine, grown, gentled, and yours.* Playstyle: companion archetype; a small chassis fights beside you.

## Sawbone (Landed Guilds)

The Guild's answer to the body. A Sawbone practices **Organic Superscience**, reads anatomy with frightening precision, and applies learned principles to treatment, grafts, mutagens, hostile preparations, and constructed servants. The work requires real inputs and hours to fabricate; medicine used under fire comes from a device, dose, organism, or procedure prepared beforehand.

### The Red Thread

*Chirurgeon-trained — surgery under fire, anatomy as a map.* Playstyle: the party’s true healer; precision offense in a pinch.





### The Preparatory

*Green Chain–trained — tinctures, stims, and poultices, brewed ahead of need.* Playstyle: pre-battle buffer; consumables as party economy.





### The Toxicologist

*Crucible-trained — the dose makes the poison, and you did the math.* Playstyle: debuffer; the enemy’s numbers quietly stop working.

## Captain (Mercenary Companies)

The tactician and the muster. A Captain builds **Legend** through Deeds, maintained command stances, and exploits that improve Aid, Move, Strike, Shoot, Defend, assessment, and coordinated action. A muster may explain who carries out an order, but Legend directs existing allies; creating cloned, dead, grown, or chimeric troops belongs to the contract, access, or device that supplied them.

### The Creche Captain

*Cloned brothers — expensive, capable, and people, whatever the Rolls decide.* Playstyle: elite small squad; troops that flank, hold, and grieve.

### The Rite-Captain

*The leased dead — tireless, fearless, contract-bound, and buried properly at term.* Playstyle: attrition; the wall that does not eat, sleep, or exceed the order.

### The Verdant Captain

*The grown line — patience, thorns, and fortifications that heal.* Playstyle: area denial; the map slowly becomes yours.

### The Chimeric Captain

*The beast-lines — bred for the work, directed by the hand.* Playstyle: mobility and scouting; the pack sees first and arrives first.

### The Free Captain

*Mortal colors — a handful of volunteers, and the trust nothing made can buy.* Playstyle: leadership; no troops — the party is the muster.

## Bounty Hunter (Registries)

The certified pursuit. Every Bounty Hunter begins with a current writ and the **Mind Weapon** class feature, a stable blade-or-gun expression that requires no power selection or manifestation roll. Their learned Psionics may include the Read, an impossibly convincing demand, ghost steps, or convergence on a quarry. Each technique spends Alignment and uses the same pushed d100 control rule as a Mentalist's more visibly psychic practice.

### The Wake-Reader

*Depth as doctrine — the Hunter who knows the quarry better than the quarry does.* Playstyle: single-target supremacy; information becomes dice.

### The Taker

*Terms: to answer, to hold — the endgame specialist who delivers them breathing.* Playstyle: ambush and capture; the fight is over before it’s a fight.

### The Veiled

*The counter-hunter — warded, masked, and rude to things that swim the dreaming.* Playstyle: anti-psychic and defense; hunts what hunts by familiarity.

## Shapeshifter (Frontier Wardens)

Embodied bargain. A Shapeshifter makes a **form contract** with a specific, relatively weak creature whose heart, name, service, or willing bond they earned—never a generic animal selected from a list. The Shapeshifter stakes the contract's Bond to become it and accepts its movement, natural weapons, senses, adaptations, instinct, Terms, Tell, and Breach.

### The Pelt-Binder

*Few shapes, deeply held — the woman who is a wolf, and has been its colleague thirty years.* Playstyle: committed transformation; the bound shape at full power.

### The Hearth-Walker

*Many light guests — the cat for the town, the hawk for the sky, the horse for the road.* Playstyle: versatility; the right shape at the right instant.

### The Face-Taker

*The town is also a landscape — humanoid shapes, under the code.* Playstyle: infiltration and social control; the network’s inside work.

## Table Notes

**Archetype is silhouette.** A list should be readable across a dark room: the Line-Holder plants, the Open Channel glows, the Rite-Captain’s wall does not breathe. Protect each archetype’s verbs when adding powers.

**The lists carry the lore.** Every archetype above is an institution’s fingerprint, and several powers deliberately point at live setting questions — the frame that chooses, the thorn line and its recall, the brother who asks, the dead man’s lease closed on-screen. GMs should let those powers be doors: a Verdant Captain using *Third Line’s Answer* has invited the Waking Made to their table, and the game intends exactly that.

**Authority.** The five source procedures are authoritative. The three volume files preserve names, Alignment footprints, effect ideas, and archetype silhouettes for conversion; their universal spend-and-activate rule is obsolete. A converted entry should keep the old idea where it fits and replace its machinery with the class's source format.
