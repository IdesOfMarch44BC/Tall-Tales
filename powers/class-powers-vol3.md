# Class Power Lists, Volume 3

> **Legacy conversion bank:** The entries below preserve names, Alignment footprints, and effect ideas, but they are not playable under the five-source rules as written. Convert Captain entries to Legend stances or exploits, fold Bounty Hunter entries into broad Psionic techniques and result ladders, and replace Shapeshifter powers with creature contracts and deeper form clauses. Sibling cross-training and universal activation are obsolete.

Several archetypes command **Troops**; the troop profile (Durability / Attack / Defend / Move), morale, routing, and re-muster rules live in *Combat and Wounds*. Moving or attacking with one troop costs 1 Momentum unless a power says otherwise, and a power that deploys a troop does not grant it a free action.

## Captain

The Captain's powers are commands, and most spend on the muster. **Prepared** powers are flashbacks — a tool, contact, route, or order established earlier because the Captain planned for this; they cannot contradict an established fact. A Captain's **Legend** is fiction, not a tracked reserve, unless a power gives it a rule.

## Bounty Hunter

Every Bounty Hunter begins with a current writ and the **Mind Weapon** class feature, manifested as a Mind Blade or Mind Gun tuned to pursuit without consuming a selection, Alignment, or a d100 roll. A creature becomes the Hunter's **quarry** only when a converted technique such as *Take the Name* designates it; being someone's quarry is a specific, powered relationship, not a free universal bonus. Powers marked **Psychic** are mental supernatural effects and can be suppressed by anti-psychic wards.

- **The Itch** — PL 1 — Requires Wyrd 1 — Learn your quarry's bearing and rough distance; through walls, weather, and lies.

- **Anticipation** — PL 2 — Requires Wyrd 1 + Shadow 1 — Stance — You act before your quarry whenever you would both act in the same surge, and it gains no benefit from surprising you.

- **Deeper Than It Knows Itself** — PL 5 — Requires Wyrd 3 + Shadow 2 — Once per scene — Ask the GM what your quarry most wants, most fears, and is about to do; you gain +3 dice when acting on any of these answers this scene.

## Shapeshifter

A Shapeshifter learns a **Form** from a specific creature whose heart or willing bond they captured — never a generic animal from a list. A form brings its movement, natural weapons, senses, strengths, hungers, and limits; use the form's statline for the traits it governs and your own for the rest. Unless a power says otherwise, shifting into or out of a form is a Move action.

- **Don the Pelt** — PL 1 — Requires Wild 1 — Shift into a bound form as part of any Move; while worn, that form's rolls gain +1 die.

- **Bound Deep** — PL 1 — Requires Wild 1 — Stance — In a bound form you keep your own mind and speech, and gain +1 to one of the form's traits — Move, Defend, or an Attack Die — chosen when you learn this power.

- **The Skin Holds** — PL 2 — Requires Wild 1 + Shadow 1 — Reaction — When a hit would wound you in a form, reduce that hit's Damage by 2 before determining severity, and remain shifted.

## Table Notes

**The samples are unchanged.** Every marquee power from *Class Archetype Overview* — the Muster lines, *Ninth of His Line*, *Wages to the Heirs*, *Third Line's Answer*, *The Closing*, *The Mask's Old Purpose*, *Old Colleagues*, *The Code's One Exception*, and the rest — appears here word for word, now surrounded by the low- and mid-PL powers that make each archetype playable from level 1. Where a sample sat at PL 6 or 7 with nothing beneath it, the intervening powers were written to lead the eye up to it.

**Archetype is still silhouette.** Read across a dark room: the Creche Captain's brothers cover each other, the Rite-Captain's wall does not breathe, the Verdant line roots and thorns, the pack sees first and arrives first, and the Free Captain has no troops at all — only the party and the trust nothing made can buy. The Wake-Reader turns knowing into dice, the Taker ends the fight before it starts, the Veiled is rude to things that swim the dreaming. The Pelt-Binder is the wolf at full power, the Hearth-Walker is the right shape at the right instant, the Face-Taker is the town's inside work.

**These lists carry live questions.** *Old Colleagues* walking a captured heart out as a soldier is a thing the Enclaves would want to know about; *The Code's One Exception* wears a willing friend and logs it after; *Wages to the Heirs* closes a dead man's lease on-screen. Let those powers be doors, the way Volumes 1–2 intend theirs to be.

**Playtest priorities.** Watch the Captain's Momentum economy first: troop actions cost Momentum, and the powers that hand out free troop actions (*The Squad Moves as One*, *No Brother Left*, *Loose Them All*, *Living Palisade*) are the archetypes' payoff and their likeliest outliers. Watch the Free Captain's action-granting (*You Heard the Captain*, *Press the Advantage*, *The Captain's Eye*, *All of You, Now*) against party size. For the Bounty Hunter, confirm that quarry-gated bonuses stay quarry-gated. For the Shapeshifter, the open variable is the power of a player's chosen Form; the GM sets each form's statline, and these powers assume forms built to the guidance in *Character Creation*, not to the ceiling.
