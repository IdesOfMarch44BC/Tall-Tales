# Mentalist

The Mentalist is a psychic ascetic who has turned the discipline of the  
mind into a supernatural weapon. Unlike Arcanists, who impose meaning on  
the world through impossible truths, the Mentalist turns inward,  
mastering perception, instinct, cognition, memory, and emotional  
control until thought itself becomes power.

A Mentalist does not cast spells. They push their own mind beyond safe  
limits.

Their abilities are subtle, dangerous, and intensely personal. A  
Mentalist can read emotion from posture, suppress pain through focus,  
project thoughts into another mind, or briefly perceive the world before  
it happens. But every use of psychic power risks overload. The deeper  
the Mentalist pushes into impossible cognition, the more likely the mind  
begins to fracture under the strain.

The traditions that produce Mentalists vary enormously. Some are monks,  
some philosophers, some emotionless assassins, some wandering mystics.  
What unites them is the same realization:

> The mind is not merely thought. It is a force.

## Game Rule Information

- **Primary Ability:** Intelligence
    
- **Secondary Ability:** Wisdom
    
- **Alignment:** Any
    
- **Hit Die:** d6
    

### Class Skills (4 + Int modifier)

Autohypnosis, Bluff, Concentration, Diplomacy, Gather Information,  
Insight, Knowledge (history), Knowledge (psionics), Listen, Sense  
Motive, Spot

### Weapon and Armor Proficiency

Mentalists are proficient with all simple weapons and no armor.

Armor interferes with psychic focus. A Mentalist wearing armor suffers a  
penalty to all psychic manifestation rolls equal to the armor’s armor  
bonus.

# Class Features

All of the following are class features of the Mentalist.

## Psychic Powers

Mentalists wield psychic powers through focus, emotional discipline, and  
controlled mental escalation.

A Mentalist knows a limited number of psychic powers. These powers are  
organized into disciplines representing different approaches to psychic  
mastery.

Manifesting a psychic power requires the Mentalist to roll:

> d100 + Mentalist level + Intelligence modifier

The result determines the strength and stability of the manifestation.

The closer the result approaches 100, the more intense the power  
becomes.

If the result exceeds 100, the Mentalist overloads.

### Overload

When a Mentalist overloads:

- The manifested power still occurs.
    
- The Mentalist loses access to all psychic powers for the remainder of  
    the encounter.
    
- The Mentalist becomes mentally strained, suffering narrative symptoms  
    appropriate to their discipline:
    
    - migraines
        
    - dissociation
        
    - hallucinations
        
    - emotional collapse
        
    - bleeding from the nose or eyes
        
    - temporary memory fragmentation
        

Some powers, feats, disciplines, and items increase the Mentalist’s safe  
threshold beyond 100.

## Psychic Disciplines

At 1st level, choose a psychic discipline.

Examples include:

- Telepathy
    
- Clairsentience
    
- Psychometabolism
    
- Psychoportation
    
- Telekinesis
    

The discipline determines:

- bonus powers,
    
- passive abilities,
    
- and the emotional or philosophical framework through which the  
    Mentalist understands their abilities.
    

At 7th, 12th, and 18th level, the Mentalist gains additional discipline  
features.

---

## Psicrystal

At 1st level, the Mentalist forms a psicrystal.

The psicrystal is a crystallized fragment of the Mentalist’s psyche,  
given physical form.

The psicrystal:

- possesses a personality reflecting some aspect of the Mentalist,
    
- communicates telepathically with its creator,
    
- and serves as both companion and psychological mirror.
    

A damaged or unstable Mentalist often develops a correspondingly altered  
psicrystal.

The psicrystal’s exact mechanics are determined later in the psionics  
chapter.

---

## Mental Discipline (Ex)

At 2nd level, the Mentalist develops powerful internal control.

The Mentalist gains:

- a +2 bonus against fear, charm, and compulsion effects,
    
- and advantage on rolls to maintain concentration during pain,  
    distraction, or emotional stress.
    

Additionally, attempts to read the Mentalist’s thoughts reveal only  
surface impressions unless the intruder succeeds on an opposed mental  
check.

---

## Sustenance (Su)

At 3rd level, the Mentalist learns to sustain the body through psychic  
focus alone.

The Mentalist may survive without food or water for a number of days  
equal to their Intelligence modifier without suffering penalties.

During this time, they enter brief meditative states instead of normal  
meals.

---

## Bonus Feat

At 4th, 9th, 14th, and 19th level, the Mentalist gains a bonus feat.

This feat must be:

- a psychic feat,
    
- a focus feat,
    
- a manifestation feat,
    
- or another feat related to mental discipline.
    

---

## Probing Touch (Su)

At 5th level, the Mentalist may physically breach another creature’s  
surface thoughts.

By touching a willing, helpless, or unaware creature and concentrating  
briefly, the Mentalist may learn:

- emotional state,
    
- immediate intentions,
    
- surface memories,
    
- or active fears.
    

Against unwilling targets, this requires an opposed mental check.

The target often experiences the intrusion as:

- sudden dizziness,
    
- déjà vu,
    
- emotional exposure,
    
- or the sensation of being watched internally.
    

---

## Refined Mind (Ex)

At 6th level, the Mentalist’s psychic control improves.

Once per encounter, the Mentalist may reroll a psychic manifestation  
roll before determining overload.

The second result must be used.

---

## Resilient Mind (Ex)

At 8th level, the Mentalist becomes immune to the dazed condition.

Additionally:

- weak charm effects cannot control them,
    
- and mundane interrogation techniques become almost useless against  
    them.
    

---

## Expanded Consciousness (Su)

At 10th level, the Mentalist’s awareness expands beyond ordinary human  
perception.

The Mentalist constantly senses:

- nearby minds,
    
- emotional disturbances,
    
- psychic pressure,
    
- and supernatural hostility.
    

This sense is imprecise but immediate.

The Mentalist may briefly perceive danger before it fully manifests.

---

## Shadow Mind (Su)

At 11th level, the Mentalist’s psicrystal develops increasing autonomy.

The psicrystal may:

- operate independently at great distance,
    
- relay sensory information,
    
- and occasionally act on instinctive understanding of the Mentalist’s  
    desires.
    

The bond between Mentalist and psicrystal becomes increasingly difficult  
to define as separate consciousnesses.

---

## Hidden Manifestation (Su)

At 13th level, the Mentalist learns to conceal psychic exertion.

Manifesting powers no longer creates obvious supernatural signs unless  
the power itself visibly alters the environment.

Observers cannot easily distinguish whether the Mentalist is:

- thinking,
    
- observing,
    
- or actively manipulating reality through psychic force.
    

---

## Mind Over Matter (Ex)

At 15th level, the Mentalist’s mental discipline reinforces physical  
survival.

The Mentalist gains:

- resistance against pain,
    
- resistance against exhaustion,
    
- and the ability to continue functioning through injuries that would  
    incapacitate ordinary people.
    

Additionally, the Mentalist may substitute Intelligence for physical  
resistance checks where pure force of will could plausibly apply.

---

## Perfect Focus (Ex)

At 16th level, the Mentalist may deliberately approach overload without  
crossing it.

Once per encounter, after rolling a manifestation result, the Mentalist  
may adjust the final total upward or downward by up to their  
Intelligence modifier.

---

## The Watching Mind (Su)

At 17th level, the Mentalist may establish a lingering psychic focus on:

- a person,
    
- a place,
    
- or an event.
    

For the next 24 hours, the GM provides occasional impressions whenever  
significant developments occur involving that focus.

These impressions are symbolic, emotional, or fragmented rather than  
literal visions.

---

## The Composed Self (Ex)

At 20th level, the Mentalist achieves complete integration of thought,  
emotion, and psychic capability.

The Mentalist becomes immune to:

- fear,
    
- charm,
    
- compulsion,
    
- and psychic domination.
    

Additionally:

- overload no longer disables the Mentalist,
    
- though overload effects still occur narratively,
    
- and the Mentalist may continue manifesting powers after exceeding  
    their safe threshold.
    

The mind has become something stronger than ordinary human cognition —  
not emotionless, but fully self-aware and impossible to control against  
its will.
