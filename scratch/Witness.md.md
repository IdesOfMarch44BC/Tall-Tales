# Witness

The Witness walks the long road between settlements carrying prayer,  
judgment, memory, and hope.

Unlike the Arcanist, who imposes impossible truths upon reality through  
understanding, the Witness calls upon something beyond themself. Their  
power comes not from mastery, but relationship — a bond with a divine,  
celestial, ancestral, or transcendent force that answers when invoked.

The first impossible thing an Arcanist learns is true is a spell.

The first impossible thing a Witness learns is true is that something  
answered.

Witnesses are not merely priests. They are riders, protectors,  
travelers, confessors, healers, executioners, and living connections  
between isolated communities. They carry news across impossible  
distances, remember the dead, bless the living, and stand between  
civilization and the darkness beyond it.

Their magic is not immediate. Prayer takes time. Conviction takes  
sacrifice. Miracles are built through endurance, suffering, ritual,  
devotion, and presence.

A Witness who loses faith does not lose power immediately.

They lose the ability to be heard.

# Game Rule Information

- **Primary Ability:** Soul
    
- **Secondary Ability:** Presence
    
- **Alignment:** Any
    
- **Hit Die:** d8
    

### Class Skills (4 + Soul modifier)

Concentration, Diplomacy, Gather Information, Heal, Insight,  
Intimidate, Knowledge (history), Knowledge (religion), Listen, Ride,  
Sense Motive, Survival

### Weapon and Armor Proficiency

Witnesses are proficient with:

- all simple weapons,
    
- light and medium armor,
    
- shields except tower shields.

# Class Features

All of the following are class features of the Witness.

## Spark of Faith

Every Witness begins with a revelation.

Your Spark was the first impossible thing you learned was true:

- that prayer could heal,
    
- that the dead could answer,
    
- that mercy had power,
    
- that judgment was real,
    
- that something heard you when you cried out.
    

Choose a Spark.

Your Spark determines:

- the tone of your miracles,
    
- what kinds of spells you may learn,
    
- the signs that accompany your prayer,
    
- and how your divine presence manifests in the world.
    

A Witness whose Spark is Mercy may radiate warmth and soft light.

A Witness whose Spark is Judgment may cause silence, ringing bells, or  
the smell of smoke before miracles occur.

As the Witness grows stronger, these manifestations become more intense.

---

## Prayercasting

Witnesses cast miracles through prayer, sacrifice, ritual, and divine  
attention.

Witnesses do not prepare spells from fixed lists.

Instead, the Witness may learn any miracle thematically connected to  
their Spark if:

- the GM agrees it fits,
    
- and the Witness has encountered, studied, witnessed, or received the  
    miracle somehow in play.
    

To cast a miracle:

- the Witness chooses a spell,
    
- the spell has a Casting Value,
    
- and each round the Witness rolls:
    

> Soul + modifiers

The total accumulates until the Casting Value is reached.

Once the Casting Value is met, the miracle manifests.

### Sacrifice

A Witness may accelerate prayer by offering:

- blood,
    
- exhaustion,
    
- vows,
    
- offerings,
    
- relics,
    
- confession,
    
- public declarations,
    
- or other meaningful sacrifices.
    

These grant bonuses determined by the GM.

Divine magic responds strongly to sincerity.

## Divine Aura (Su)

A Witness radiates supernatural conviction.

The strength of this aura increases with level:

- Faint
    
- Weak
    
- Moderate
    
- Strong
    
- Overwhelming
    

The aura:

- identifies the Witness spiritually,
    
- affects supernatural creatures,
    
- and empowers certain class features.
    

Hostile supernatural creatures often instinctively recognize a Witness  
before understanding why.

---

## Touch of Grace (Su)

At 2nd level, the Witness may heal through touch.

The Witness may:

- close wounds,
    
- reduce fear,
    
- ease pain,
    
- or stabilize the dying.
    

This healing is supernatural but exhausting.

Repeated healing in a short time visibly strains the Witness:

- trembling hands,
    
- bleeding eyes,
    
- emotional fatigue,
    
- whispered prayers,
    
- or visible spiritual burnout.
    

The exact mechanics are determined later.

---

## The Circuit (Ex)

At 3rd level, the Witness establishes their Circuit.

Choose several settlements, congregations, shrines, roads, or  
communities you regularly travel between.

Within your Circuit:

- people recognize you,
    
- hospitality is easier to obtain,
    
- and information travels toward you unexpectedly quickly.
    

When entering a Circuit settlement:

- someone knows you,
    
- remembers you,
    
- or has heard of you.
    

At higher levels, the Circuit becomes almost supernatural:

- messages reach you through rumor,
    
- danger within the Circuit disturbs your sleep,
    
- and communities begin organizing themselves around your return.
    

---

## Aura Strength (Ex)

At 4th, 8th, 14th, and 18th level, your Divine Aura increases in  
strength.

Your aura becomes increasingly difficult for:

- undead,
    
- fiends,
    
- corrupt spirits,
    
- and hostile supernatural entities  
    to endure directly.
    

---

## Counsel (Su)

At 5th level, the Witness may spend time speaking sincerely with another  
creature.

After at least one minute of honest conversation, the Witness may grant  
that creature:

- courage,
    
- clarity,
    
- resolve,
    
- or spiritual reassurance.
    

Later, the target may invoke this Counsel for a bonus determined by the  
system.

A creature may only carry one active Counsel at a time.

---

## Bonded Mount (Su)

At 6th level, the Witness gains a Bonded Mount.

The mount may be:

- a horse,
    
- a great beast,
    
- a biollurgical chassis,
    
- or another appropriate creature.
    

The mount:

- understands the Witness instinctively,
    
- can travel impossible distances,
    
- and often displays subtle supernatural traits.
    

A Witness and their mount quickly become impossible to emotionally  
separate in the eyes of the communities they serve.

---

## Hardened Spirit (Ex)

At 7th level, the Witness gains resistance against:

- fear,
    
- despair,
    
- emotional manipulation,
    
- and supernatural intimidation.
    

The road has taught them endurance.

---

## Endure Hardship (Ex)

At 9th level, the Witness ignores:

- mundane exhaustion,
    
- harsh weather,
    
- sleep deprivation,
    
- and long-distance travel penalties.
    

The Witness becomes capable of surviving journeys that would destroy  
ordinary people.

---

## Miracle (Su)

At 10th level, the Witness may perform true miracles.

A Miracle is not merely a spell.

It is:

- a prayer answered unexpectedly,
    
- impossible survival,
    
- divine intervention,
    
- or a moment where reality bends toward meaning.
    

Miracles are intentionally rare and dramatic.

The exact mechanics are determined later.

---

## Witness’s Strength (Su)

At 11th level, when protecting:

- a member of the Circuit,
    
- an innocent,
    
- or someone placed under the Witness’s care,
    

the Witness may refuse collapse through sheer spiritual conviction.

The Witness remains standing when they otherwise should fall.

This often appears terrifying to observers.

---

## Greater Counsel (Su)

At 12th level, Counsel may be invoked retroactively.

If the Witness sincerely spoke with someone earlier in the day, they may  
declare:

> “Remember what I told you.”

The target immediately gains the benefit of Counsel.

---

## The Living Road (Su)

At 13th level, roads, travelers, shrines, and settlements begin carrying  
the Witness’s presence.

People:

- dream of the Witness before arrival,
    
- leave offerings along the road,
    
- or speak of hearing distant bells before the Witness appears.
    

The Circuit becomes spiritually real.

---

## Greater Authority (Su)

At 15th level, the Witness may invoke overwhelming spiritual authority.

Undead, fiends, corrupted beings, and hostile supernatural creatures may  
be:

- driven back,
    
- silenced,
    
- forced to kneel,
    
- or spiritually overwhelmed.
    

The exact effect depends on:

- the Witness’s Spark,
    
- the target,
    
- and the narrative context.
    

---

## Sacred Presence (Su)

At 16th level, merely remaining near the Witness begins affecting the  
world around them.

Examples:

- nightmares weaken nearby,
    
- fear becomes harder to sustain,
    
- wounds close faster,
    
- arguments quiet,
    
- candles ignite spontaneously,
    
- or hostile spirits avoid entering the area entirely.
    

---

## Carry the Word (Su)

At 17th level, truth travels with the Witness.

When the Witness shares news they personally know to be true,  
communities instinctively recognize its sincerity.

This does not force agreement.

It does make disbelief difficult.

---

## Final Benediction (Su)

At 19th level, the Witness may perform a final blessing before death.

This may:

- heal allies,
    
- banish darkness,
    
- reveal truth,
    
- restore courage,
    
- or sanctify a place forever.
    

The effect should feel mythic.

---

## The Hand on the Bridle (Su)

At 20th level, the Witness becomes a living conduit for divine presence.

Once per day, when the Witness would die:

- they instead remain standing,
    
- divine force erupts around them,
    
- and their allies immediately feel the presence of something greater.
    

For a brief time:

- fear cannot touch nearby allies,
    
- hostile supernatural creatures falter,
    
- and the Witness acts with impossible clarity.
    

Afterward, the Witness is left permanently changed by the experience.

No Witness describes this transformation the same way twice.
