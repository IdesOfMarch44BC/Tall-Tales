# Dynast

The Dynast is the inheritor of an ancient bloodline touched by  
something inhuman. Some Houses descend from impossible unions, others  
from generations of occult refinement, selective breeding, or ancient  
pacts whose original terms have long since been forgotten. Whatever  
their origin, the blood of the Houses is no longer entirely mortal.

A Dynast does not learn techniques or cast spells. They inherit traits.

As they grow in power, more of the bloodline awakens within them:  
unnatural instincts, hereditary mutations, ancestral memories, and the  
presence of old nobility that ordinary people feel instinctively even  
when they cannot explain why.

Every Dynast belongs to a House. The House determines the shape of their  
inheritance.

# Game Rule Information

- **Primary Ability:** Presence
    
- **Secondary Ability:** Body
    
- **Alignment:** Any
    
- **Hit Die:** d8
    

**Class Skills (4 + Int modifier):** Bluff, Diplomacy, Gather  
Information, Intimidate, Knowledge (history), Knowledge (nobility),  
Listen, Sense Motive, Spot

**Weapon and Armor Proficiency:** Simple weapons, light armor, noble  
weapons appropriate to their House

---

# Class Features

All of the following are class features of the Dynast.

## House Lineage

At 1st level, choose a Noble House.

Your House determines:

- your hereditary appearance,
    
- your Influence,
    
- your Bloodline Traits,
    
- your social reputation,
    
- and the nature of your inhuman inheritance.
    

The GM may create additional Houses appropriate to the setting.

---

## Manifestation

A Dynast may suppress or express their bloodline.

### Suppressed

While suppressed:

- your supernatural traits are muted,
    
- you appear mostly human,
    
- and your Influence is reduced.
    

Minor hereditary signs usually remain:  
unusual eyes, pale skin, sharpened teeth, unnatural posture, and similar  
traits.

### Expressed

As a swift action, you may fully express your bloodline.

While expressed:

- all active Bloodline Traits function,
    
- your appearance becomes visibly inhuman,
    
- and your Influence intensifies.
    

The exact appearance depends on your House:  
predatory features, corpse-like flesh, impossible beauty, reflective  
eyes, animalistic traits, and similar manifestations.

Creatures that witness your transformation must make a Will save  
(DC 10 + 1/2 level + Presence) or become Shaken until the end of the  
scene.

A creature that succeeds is immune for 24 hours.

---

## Influence

Every Bloodline Trait carries an Influence:  
an instinct, desire, emotional tendency, or behavioral compulsion tied  
to the House’s inhuman nature.

Examples include:

- predatory fixation,
    
- territorial aggression,
    
- emotional detachment,
    
- obsession with hierarchy,
    
- hunger for violence,
    
- compulsive curiosity,
    
- possessiveness,
    
- or inability to perceive ordinary morality normally.
    

Influence is not mind control.

The Dynast always chooses their actions, but the Influence shapes how  
the world feels to them.

At higher levels, some Influences may become permanently integrated or  
suppressed.

---

## Menacing Presence

A Dynast carries the weight of old blood.

You gain a bonus to:

- Intimidate,
    
- noble etiquette,
    
- and social dominance checks
    

equal to half your Dynast level.

Common people instinctively recognize that you belong to something old,  
dangerous, and powerful.

---

## Bloodline Traits

As you level, your inheritance unfolds.

Each Bloodline Trait occupies a body locus associated with part of the  
Dynast’s being.

Typical loci include:

- Eyes
    
- Mouth
    
- Skin
    
- Blood
    
- Heart
    
- Hands
    
- Mind
    
- Legs
    
- Voice
    

A House provides one Bloodline Trait for each locus.

Example traits include:

- predatory vision,
    
- supernatural regeneration,
    
- venomous blood,
    
- psychic beauty,
    
- impossible memory,
    
- unnatural resilience,
    
- corpse-flesh,
    
- or inhuman speed.
    

Bloodline Traits only function while your bloodline is Expressed unless  
otherwise stated.

---

## House Favor

Beginning at 3rd level, your House recognizes you as a legitimate member  
of the lineage.

Once per session, you may call upon:

- House intelligence,
    
- political leverage,
    
- safehouses,
    
- retainers,
    
- transportation,
    
- or financial support.
    

The exact form depends on:

- the House,
    
- the region,
    
- and your standing within the lineage.
    

The GM determines the extent of the favor.

---

## Strange Anatomy

At 4th level, your biology no longer fully resembles mortal anatomy.

While Expressed:

- critical hits against you have a 5% chance per Dynast level to become  
    normal hits,
    
- you gain resistance to poison and disease,
    
- and mundane anatomy-based medicine becomes unreliable.
    

Your body may contain:

- redundant organs,
    
- altered bone structures,
    
- strange blood,
    
- or partially nonhuman systems.
    

---

## Expressed Form

At 6th level, maintaining your bloodline becomes effortless.

You may:

- Express or Suppress your bloodline as a free action once per round,
    
- and maintain minor manifestations even while Suppressed.
    

---

## Unnatural Instinct

At 8th level, your House’s instincts sharpen.

Once per scene, you may ask the GM one of the following:

- “Who here fears me most?”
    
- “Who here threatens my House?”
    
- “Who here is hiding weakness?”
    
- “What instinct does my bloodline detect?”
    

The answer must be truthful, though it may be incomplete or symbolic.

---

## Lesser Integration

At 10th level, choose one Bloodline Trait.

That trait becomes permanently integrated:

- it remains active even while Suppressed,
    
- and its Influence no longer affects you negatively.
    

---

## Twisted Mind

At 12th level, your psychology becomes increasingly alien.

You gain:

- resistance to fear,
    
- resistance to mental intrusion,
    
- and immunity to mundane lie detection.
    

Creatures attempting direct psychic contact with you experience:

- ancestral echoes,
    
- predatory instincts,
    
- inherited memories,
    
- or overwhelming emotional bleed-through.
    

---

## Rapid Manifestation

At 14th level, your body reshapes itself instinctively.

Once per scene, you may:

- swap an active Bloodline Trait,
    
- alter your visible manifestations,
    
- or partially reconfigure your physiology
    

as a swift action.

---

## Otherworldly Presence

At 16th level, your bloodline presence becomes overwhelming.

When you enter a social scene while Expressed, the GM must immediately  
tell you:

- who holds power,
    
- who resents your House,
    
- and who wishes to impress or deceive you.
    

You instinctively perceive noble hierarchy and social weakness.

---

## Naught Morality

At 18th level, ordinary morality no longer fully maps onto your psyche.

You become:

- immune to magical emotional manipulation,
    
- immune to forced alignment alteration,
    
- and difficult to judge through supernatural means.
    

Your morality becomes personal, hereditary, and alien.

Different Dynasts experience this differently:

- cold detachment,
    
- predatory nobility,
    
- obsessive loyalty,
    
- or aristocratic transcendence.
    

---

## Lineage Eternal

At 20th level, death no longer fully ends your existence.

When you die, your consciousness may reincarnate within another living  
member of your House.

This process may take:

- days,
    
- months,
    
- or years.
    

The reincarnated Dynast retains:

- fragments of memory,
    
- ancestral instincts,
    
- House loyalties,
    
- and portions of their former personality.
    

No House ever fully loses its greatest members.
