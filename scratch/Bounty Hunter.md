# Bounty Hunter

The Bounty Hunter is a relentless tracker, psychic duelist, and hunter  
of dangerous prey. Unlike the Mentalist, whose psionics are broad and  
philosophical, the Bounty Hunter learns focused psychic techniques  
designed for pursuit, survival, and violence.

Every Bounty Hunter manifests a **Mind Blade**: a weapon forged from  
focus, instinct, and psychic force.

Some hunt criminals.  
Some hunt monsters.  
Some hunt rogue psychics or escaped Dynasts.  
Some simply hunt whoever the Registry points them toward.

No matter the target, the principle is the same:

> Once marked, prey does not escape.

The Bounty Hunter is a fast, aggressive psychic martial class focused  
on:

- tracking,
    
- dueling,
    
- mobility,
    
- precision strikes,
    
- and relentless pursuit.

# Game Rule Information

- **Primary Ability:** Mind
    
- **Secondary Ability:** Reflex
    
- **Alignment:** Any
    
- **Hit Die:** d10
    

## Class Skills (6 + Mind Modifier)

Autohypnosis, Balance, Bluff, Climb, Concentration,  
Diplomacy, Disguise, Escape Artist, Gather Information,  
Hide, Intimidate, Jump, Knowledge (Local),  
Knowledge (Psionics), Listen, Move Silently,  
Search, Sense Motive, Spot, Survival,  
Swim, Tumble

## Weapon and Armor Proficiency

The Bounty Hunter is proficient with:

- all simple weapons,
    
- all martial weapons,
    
- light armor,
    
- shields (except tower shields),
    
- and their own Mind Blade.
    

---

# Class Features

All of the following are class features of the Bounty Hunter.

---

## Mind Blade (Psi)

As a Move Action, the Bounty Hunter may manifest a weapon formed from  
solidified psychic force.

This weapon may take the form of:

- any melee weapon,
    
- any thrown weapon,
    
- or later, ranged weapons through Mind Gun.
    

The weapon is considered magical for overcoming resistance and counts as  
a weapon the Bounty Hunter is proficient with.

If released, the weapon dissolves immediately.

The Mind Blade gains enhancement bonuses as the Bounty Hunter levels:

|Level|Enhancement|
|:--|:--|
|4|+1|
|8|+2|
|12|+3|
|16|+4|
|20|+5|

The Bounty Hunter may reshape the appearance of the blade whenever it is  
manifested.

---

## Marked Prey

At 1st level, the Bounty Hunter may mark a creature they can perceive.

The mark represents:

- psychic fixation,
    
- predictive analysis,
    
- obsessive pursuit,
    
- and combat pressure.
    

The Bounty Hunter gains advantages against marked prey, including:

- tracking bonuses,
    
- pursuit benefits,
    
- and access to certain class abilities.
    

Only one creature may normally be marked at a time.

The mark lasts until:

- the target dies,
    
- escapes for an extended duration,
    
- or the Bounty Hunter dismisses it.
    

---

## Wild Talent

At 1st level, the Bounty Hunter gains latent psychic ability.

The Bounty Hunter counts as psionic and may interact with psionic items,  
effects, and abilities.

---

## Psychic Powers

The Bounty Hunter learns a limited number of psychic powers.

Unlike the Mentalist, the Bounty Hunter does not study entire psychic  
disciplines. Instead, they learn isolated techniques useful for:

- pursuit,
    
- combat,
    
- survival,
    
- mobility,
    
- and psychic warfare.
    

The Bounty Hunter learns individual powers as shown on the class table.

These powers use the Psychic Overload system.

When manifesting a psychic power:

- roll d100 + up to your class level,
    
- higher results strengthen the effect,
    
- but exceeding 100 overloads the mind.
    

If overloaded:

- the power still manifests,
    
- but the Bounty Hunter loses access to psychic abilities for the rest  
    of the encounter.
    

---

## Mind Gun (Psi)

At 2nd level, the Bounty Hunter may shape their Mind Blade into:

- bows,
    
- firearms,
    
- thrown weapons,
    
- or other ranged weapons.
    

Psychic ammunition replenishes automatically.

Thrown Mind Blades return after striking.

---

## Psychic Strike (Psi)

At 3rd level, the Bounty Hunter learns to channel destructive psychic  
force through their Mind Blade.

After focusing psychic energy, the next successful strike deals bonus  
psychic damage.

Psychic Strike deals:

- +1d6 damage at 3rd level,
    
- increasing every four levels thereafter.
    

Psychic Strike only affects living creatures with minds.

---

## Knife to the Heart (Psi)

Beginning at 5th level, the Bounty Hunter may sacrifice Psychic Strike  
damage to inflict ability damage instead.

This represents precision psychic disruption targeting:

- muscles,
    
- reflexes,
    
- cognition,
    
- or emotional stability.
    

Higher-level Bounty Hunters may affect mental abilities as well as  
physical ones.

---

## Evasion (Ex)

At 6th level, the Bounty Hunter may avoid effects entirely through  
extreme agility and psychic reflexes.

If the Bounty Hunter succeeds on a Reflex save for half damage, they  
instead take no damage.

---

## Free Draw (Psi)

At 8th level, the Bounty Hunter may manifest their Mind Blade as a Free  
Action.

---

## Uncanny Tracker

At 9th level, the Bounty Hunter becomes extraordinarily difficult to  
mislead or evade.

The Bounty Hunter may:

- follow supernatural trails,
    
- identify signs others overlook,
    
- and continue pursuit through difficult terrain or urban environments.
    

---

## Expanded Mark

At 10th level, the Bounty Hunter may maintain multiple marked targets at  
once.

---

## Ghost Step (Psi)

At 13th level, the Bounty Hunter learns to move through battle with  
impossible precision.

Their movement becomes unnaturally fluid and difficult to pin down,  
often appearing almost supernatural.

---

## Improved Evasion (Ex)

At 14th level, the Bounty Hunter takes only half damage even on failed  
Reflex saves against effects that normally allow half damage.

---

## Impossible Pursuit

At 17th level, distance becomes almost meaningless to the Bounty Hunter.

The Bounty Hunter always knows:

- the approximate direction of marked prey,
    
- whether they are alive,
    
- and whether they recently used teleportation or dimensional travel.
    

The Bounty Hunter may pursue targets across:

- nations,
    
- planes,
    
- or realities.
    

---

## Perfect Draw (Psi)

At 18th level, the Bounty Hunter may manifest their Mind Blade instantly  
without thought or effort.

The weapon may appear:

- in response to danger,
    
- during movement,
    
- or as part of another action.
    

---

## Inescapable Hunter

At 20th level, no marked prey can truly escape.

Against marked targets:

- concealment fails,
    
- ordinary disguises become transparent,
    
- and teleportation alone cannot guarantee safety.
    

The Bounty Hunter's pursuit transcends ordinary barriers.
