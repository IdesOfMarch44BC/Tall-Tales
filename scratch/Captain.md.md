# Captain

## Fantasy

The Captain is a battlefield commander, strategist, and survivor whose
greatest weapon is momentum.

Unlike spellcasters, the Captain does not reshape reality through
supernatural force. Instead, they control the flow of conflict through
timing, preparation, leadership, and instinct. A Captain knows where to
stand, when to strike, when to retreat, and how to keep a desperate
situation from collapsing entirely.

At low levels, this appears as tactical awareness and battlefield
coordination. At high levels, a Captain's intuition becomes so refined
that it borders on prophecy. They arrive prepared for situations they
should not have anticipated, survive impossible battles through sheer
force of will, and command allies with the certainty of someone who has
already seen how the fight ends.

Captains are commonly found among Mercenary Companies, military
formations, rail guards, pirate crews, revolutionary cells, and frontier
militias, though independent Captains are not uncommon.

---

## Role in the World

A Captain is rarely anonymous.

Even low-ranking Captains tend to accumulate followers, rivals, favors,
debts, and stories. Communities remember them. Mercenary Companies seek
them out. Trade Companies negotiate with them carefully. Other martial
fighters survive battles because a Captain was present to hold the line
when everything began falling apart.

The Captain is the class most associated with organized violence in the
civilized world. Where a Drifter survives alone and a Bounty Hunter
tracks prey through persistence, the Captain turns groups into forces
capable of surviving impossible situations together.

Many Captains eventually become:
- mercenary commanders
- military officers
- frontier marshals
- ship captains
- gang leaders
- revolutionaries
- nobles through military accomplishment
- rulers in all but name

### [[Legend]]

Captains use [[Legend]] to fuel their abilities.

Legend represents momentum, authority, morale, preparation, and control
over the flow of a conflict.

A Captain gains Legend by:
- protecting allies
- exploiting enemy mistakes
- taking meaningful risks
- succeeding through planning
- maintaining battlefield control
- turning losing situations around
- coordinating allies effectively

Captains spend Legend to activate [[Deeds]], Commands, and Preparations.

---

### Command Presence

A Captain alters the flow of battle simply by taking command.

The Captain may spend Legend to issue Commands. Unless otherwise stated,
issuing a Command does not require an action.

Commands are not supernatural. They represent leadership, tactical
timing, morale, positioning, and battlefield instinct.

The Captain cannot command creatures incapable of understanding them.

Example Commands include:
- repositioning allies
- suppressing panic
- coordinating assaults
- ordering retreats
- creating openings
- covering movement
- focusing fire
- protecting vulnerable allies

The GM is encouraged to resolve Commands generously when the fiction
supports them.

---

### Tactical Coordination

The Captain specializes in synchronizing allies and controlling the flow
of engagements.

At 1st level, as a Move Action, you may spend 1 Legend to shift a
willing ally’s action to 1 phase earlier than normal.

At 7th level, you may affect two allies simultaneously.

At 13th level, affected allies may immediately reposition up to 1 tile
without provoking reactions.

At 19th level, you may coordinate an entire allied group within 2 tiles.

The Captain cannot move actions earlier than the first phase of a round.

---

### Swift Commands

At 3rd level, issuing Commands no longer requires a Move Action.

You may issue Commands as a Swift Action.

---

### Prepared for This

At 4th level, by spending 2 Legend, you may declare a plausible prior
preparation for the current situation.

The preparation cannot directly contradict established facts.

Examples include:
- hidden supplies
- cached ammunition
- reserve mounts
- escape routes
- forged paperwork
- concealed weapons
- bribed personnel
- prearranged signals
- hidden tools
- fallback positions

The GM determines the exact scope of the preparation.

---

### Extended Command Range

At 5th level, your Commands and Tactical Coordination abilities may
target allies within 1 tile instead of adjacent allies.

At 11th level, this increases to 2 tiles.

---

### Coordinated Assault

At 7th level, when an ally damages a creature you have targeted with a
Command this round, you gain 1 Legend.

This may occur once per round.

---

### Command Authority

Captains naturally accumulate followers, influence, and operational
authority.

Choose a sphere of authority such as:
- Mercenaries
- Soldiers
- Sailors
- Rail Guards
- Couriers
- Criminals
- Hunters
- Nobility
- Revolutionaries
- Frontier Settlers

Your authority expands as you gain levels.

| Level | Influence |
|---|---|
| 1 | Local recognition and personal contacts |
| 8 | Regional reputation and logistical support |
| 12 | Institutional leverage and political influence |
| 16 | Strategic authority across multiple factions |
| 20 | The ability to mobilize armies, fleets, or regional forces |

The exact form this authority takes depends on your history,
relationships, and reputation.

---

### Free Commands

At 9th level, issuing Commands no longer requires an action.

You may issue one Command per round as a Free Action.

---

### Tactical Assessment

At 10th level, once per scene, you may ask the GM one tactical question.

The GM must answer truthfully, though the answer may be:
- incomplete
- probabilistic
- symbolic
- metaphorical
- framed as instinct or intuition

Examples include:
- “What is the enemy’s weakness?”
- “Who is actually in control here?”
- “What’s about to go wrong?”
- “What are we overlooking?”
- “What would I do if I were them?”

The answer represents your ability to rapidly analyze situations and
predict outcomes.

---

### Deed Tier III

At 11th level, you may learn and use Tier III [[Deeds]].

---

### Perfect Coordination

At 13th level, when you use Tactical Coordination, affected allies gain
a bonus to attacks, movement, or defensive positioning appropriate to
the situation.

The exact bonus is determined by the GM based on the coordinated action.

---

### Flashback Preparation (1 Minute)

At 14th level, your preparations become almost supernatural in their
thoroughness.

By spending 4 Legend, you may declare that you prepared for the current
situation up to 1 minute ago.

This may include:
- positioning allies
- planting tools
- arranging distractions
- scouting routes
- manipulating terrain
- establishing signals
- creating temporary advantages

The preparation must still be plausible.

---

### Deed

At 2nd, 6th, and 15th level, learn a new [[Deed]] or improve an
existing one.

---

### Command Authority (Strategic Influence)

At 16th level, your authority reaches across institutional boundaries.

Major factions recognize your reputation, even if they do not trust you.

You may reasonably request:
- military audiences
- ceasefires
- operational cooperation
- emergency logistics
- temporary command authority
- strategic intelligence

These requests are not guaranteed, but they are taken seriously.

---

### Flashback Preparation (1 Hour)

At 17th level, your preparations may retroactively account for actions
taken up to 1 hour ago.

The scope of possible preparations expands significantly.

---

### Deed Tier IV

At 18th level, you may learn and use Tier IV [[Deeds]].

---

### Flashback Preparation (1 Day)

At 19th level, your preparations may retroactively account for actions
taken up to 1 day ago.

At this level, the Captain begins operating on the scale of campaigns
rather than battles.

Entire operations may have been arranged in advance.

---

### Command Authority (Military Mobilization)

At 20th level, your reputation and authority become world-class.

Once per year, you may call upon a massive favor appropriate to your
sphere of authority.

Examples include:
- military reinforcement
- fleet mobilization
- evacuation support
- regional intelligence networks
- emergency logistical aid
- coordinated mercenary deployment
- temporary political alliance

The exact response depends on your reputation and prior actions.

## [[Deeds]]

Captains learn [[Deeds]] representing famous battlefield exploits,
leadership techniques, and acts of impossible survival.

Each Deed costs Legend to activate.

Captains learn one new Deed each level and may improve existing Deeds as
they gain access to higher tiers.

---

## Example Deeds

### Bloody Survival

**Cost:** Variable Legend

The first time you would die from hit point damage, you do not.

Instead, you are reduced to 1 hit point. This costs Legend equal to the
amount of negative hit points you would have reached.

#### Tier II
You may use this ability twice per encounter.

#### Tier III
This Deed may prevent death from effects other than hit point damage,
though doing so costs half your maximum Legend.

#### Tier IV
You cannot die while you possess Legend.

Any effect that would kill you instead reduces you to -1 hit point. You
lose 10 Legend at the end of each turn while below 0 hit points.

---

### On My Mark

**Cost:** 2 Legend

Choose a willing ally.

That ally may delay their action to any point later in the round,
including immediately after another creature acts.

Higher tiers may allow coordinated actions between multiple allies.

---

### Fall Back

**Cost:** 1 Legend

Choose an ally within range.

That ally may immediately move up to their speed without provoking
opportunity attacks.

---

### Hold the Line

**Cost:** 2 Legend

Choose an ally or defensive position.

Until the start of your next turn:
- forced movement against the target is reduced or negated
- morale effects against the target are weakened
- enemies attempting to push through the defended area suffer penalties

---

### Tactical Assessment

**Cost:** 1 Legend

Ask the GM a tactical question about the current situation.

The answer must be truthful, though it may be incomplete, probabilistic,
or presented through metaphor or military intuition.

Examples:
- "What is the enemy's weakest point?"
- "What are we failing to notice?"
- "Who is the greatest immediate threat?"
- "What happens if we remain here?"

## Gameplay Style

The Captain is a proactive martial support class focused on:
- momentum
- tactical positioning
- leadership
- preparation
- survival
- battlefield control

The Captain excels in:
- organized engagements
- protecting allies
- stabilizing chaotic situations
- coordinating groups
- narrative preparation
- command-oriented play

The Captain is not primarily a damage-dealing class, though experienced
Captains are fully capable combatants.
