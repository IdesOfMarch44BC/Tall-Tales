# Shapeshifter

The Shapeshifter is a master of embodied transformation. Unlike a Mage,  
they do not invoke external power, and unlike an Artisan, they do not  
construct devices. Instead, the Shapeshifter learns to overwrite their  
own flesh with the forms of other living beings.

A Shapeshifter does not become a generic wolf or dragon. They become the  
specific creature whose heart they captured, carrying its instincts,  
strengths, hungers, and limitations within themselves.

The oldest Shapeshifters eventually lose the distinction between their  
original self and the forms they wear.

# Game Rule Information

- **Primary Ability:** Wisdom
    
- **Secondary Abilities:** Constitution, Dexterity
    
- **Alignment:** Any non-lawful
    
- **Hit Die:** d8
    

**Class Skills (6 + Int modifier):** Balance, Climb, Concentration,  
Diplomacy, Handle Animal, Heal, Knowledge (nature), Knowledge (the  
planes), Listen, Sense Motive, Spot, Survival, Swim

---

# Weapon and Armor Proficiency

The Shapeshifter is proficient with simple weapons, light armor, medium  
armor, and shields.

---

# Class Features

All of the following are class features of the Shapeshifter.

## Shapeshifting

At 1st level, you gain the ability to transform into specific creatures  
whose hearts you have captured.

A creature’s heart may be captured:

- by consuming its literal heart,
    
- by spiritually bonding with it,
    
- by earning its willing acceptance,
    
- or through another mystical act approved by the GM.
    

A Shapeshifter cannot learn forms from creatures whose CR exceeds their  
level.

Transforming is a standard action unless otherwise noted.

You may shapeshift at-will.

You may only maintain one form at a time.

Changing forms immediately ends the previous transformation.

---

## Forms

A Form is a complete biological overwrite occupying all body slots at  
once.

While transformed:

- your physical ability scores become the form’s,
    
- your size becomes the form’s,
    
- your movement modes become the form’s,
    
- you gain the form’s natural weapons,
    
- you gain the form’s extraordinary and supernatural abilities,
    
- and you gain the instincts and desires natural to the creature.
    

You retain:

- your own mind,
    
- your memories,
    
- your class features,
    
- your skill ranks,
    
- and your mental ability scores unless the form’s are higher.
    

You do not gain:

- class levels,
    
- equipment proficiencies,
    
- learned spellcasting,
    
- or memories belonging to the creature.
    

---

## Learning Forms

At every level, you gain a CR budget equal to your Shapeshifter level.  
This budget may be spent to permanently learn new Forms.

For example:

- A 5th-level Shapeshifter could learn:
    
    - one CR 5 creature,
        
    - two CR 2 creatures,
        
    - or five CR 1 creatures.
        

Unused CR budget carries over between levels.

Additionally, the GM may allow Forms to be learned through significant  
story events, hunts, rituals, or supernatural encounters.

---

## Lesser Forms

At 1st level, you may learn Forms from the following creature types:

- Animal
    
- Humanoid
    
- Monstrous Humanoid
    

---

## Bestial Instinct

At 2nd level, your senses sharpen.

You gain:

- low-light vision,
    
- scent,
    
- and advantage on rolls to track, detect ambushes, or notice hidden  
    creatures.
    

---

## Rapid Transformation

At 3rd level, transforming becomes a move action instead of a standard  
action.

---

## Wild Adaptation

At 4th level, you become resistant to hostile environments.

You ignore:

- mundane weather penalties,
    
- difficult terrain caused by natural environments,
    
- and natural diseases.
    

---

## Additional Form

At 5th level and every four levels thereafter, choose one Form you know.

That Form gains one of the following permanent enhancements:

- +2 Strength
    
- +2 Dexterity
    
- +2 Constitution
    
- +10 feet movement speed
    
- Darkvision
    
- +2 natural armor
    

The same enhancement may not be selected twice for the same Form.

---

## Wild Forms

At 6th level, you may learn Forms from the following creature types:

- Magical Beast
    
- Plant
    
- Vermin
    

---

## Predatory Body

At 7th level, your transformed attacks become unnaturally deadly.

Your natural weapons:

- count as magical,
    
- ignore mundane damage resistance,
    
- and deal an additional weapon die of damage.
    

---

## Monstrous Endurance

At 8th level, your transformed body rapidly adapts to punishment.

Once per encounter, when reduced below 0 hit points, you may immediately  
transform into another Form you know.

Your hit points remain unchanged, but all ongoing poisons, diseases, and  
bleeding effects end.

---

## Persistent Shape

At 10th level, your transformations become increasingly stable.

You become immune to:

- involuntary polymorph effects,
    
- forced biological mutation,
    
- and mundane aging penalties.
    

---

## Elder Forms

At 11th level, you may learn Forms from the following creature types:

- Fey
    
- Giant
    
- Elemental
    

---

## Hybrid Transformation

At 12th level, you may partially merge Forms.

As part of transforming, choose:

- one movement mode,
    
- one natural weapon,
    
- or one sensory ability
    

from another Form you know and apply it to your current transformation.

---

## Impossible Anatomy

At 14th level, your body no longer obeys conventional biology.

Your transformed Forms gain:

- immunity to critical hits from mundane weapons,
    
- immunity to forced dismemberment,
    
- and resistance against petrification and paralysis.
    

---

## Rapid Hybridization

At 15th level, you may use Hybrid Transformation twice simultaneously.

---

## Mythic Forms

At 16th level, you may learn Forms from the following creature types:

- Dragon
    
- Outsider
    

---

## Apex Predator

At 17th level, lesser creatures instinctively recognize you as a  
supernatural hunter.

Creatures whose CR is lower than half your level:

- cannot willingly initiate combat against you unless magically compelled,
    
- and suffer disadvantage on rolls against fear effects you cause.
    

---

## Living Myth

At 19th level, your identity has become inseparable from your  
transformations.

You no longer age.

You may remain transformed indefinitely, even while sleeping.

You are always considered both your original creature type and your  
current Form’s creature type simultaneously.

---

## Perfect Shape

At 20th level, transformation becomes instinctive and absolute.

- You may transform once per round as a free or immediate action.
    
- Your Shapeshifting ability becomes Extraordinary instead of  
    Supernatural.
    
- You may maintain Hybrid Transformation permanently.
    
- You are immune to unwanted transformation effects of any kind.
