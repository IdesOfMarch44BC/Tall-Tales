# Drifter

The Drifter is a wanderer, survivor, outlaw, and impossible duelist.  
Unlike the Captain, who leads others, the Drifter survives alone  
through instinct, experience, and hard-earned legend.

Some are wandering swordsmen.  
Some are gunslingers.  
Some are escaped convicts, disgraced nobles, hunters, scouts, or  
mercenaries who outlived too many bad jobs.

The world leaves marks on a Drifter.  
Their Deeds are the stories those marks become.

The Drifter is a mobile martial class focused on:

- adaptability,
    
- survival,
    
- precision combat,
    
- mobility,
    
- and fighting while isolated or outnumbered.
    

Adapted from the original Drifter class.

# Game Rule Information

- **Primary Ability:** Reflex
    
- **Secondary Ability:** Mind
    
- **Alignment:** Any
    
- **Hit Die:** d10
    

## Class Skills (6 + Mind Modifier)

Balance, Bluff, Climb, Disable Device, Escape Artist,  
Gather Information, Hide, Intimidate, Jump,  
Knowledge (Local), Listen, Move Silently,  
Open Lock, Search, Sense Motive, Spot,  
Survival, Swim, Tumble

## Weapon and Armor Proficiency

The Drifter is proficient with:

- simple weapons,
    
- martial melee weapons,
    
- thrown weapons,
    
- light armor,
    
- but not shields.
    

---

# Class Features

All of the following are class features of the Drifter.

---

## Deeds

The Drifter learns martial techniques known as Deeds.

Unlike formal martial schools, a Drifter’s Deeds are personal survival  
methods learned through hardship, violence, and experience.

Examples include:

- impossible dodges,
    
- sudden draws,
    
- brutal counters,
    
- dirty tricks,
    
- ambush techniques,
    
- and acts of desperate endurance.
    

The Drifter learns Deeds as shown on the class table.

Deeds cost Legend to activate.

Unless otherwise specified:

- a Deed may only be used once per action,
    
- and only while the Drifter has sufficient Legend remaining.
    

---

## Legend

Legend represents momentum, reputation, confidence, and narrative  
pressure.

The Drifter gains and loses Legend dynamically during encounters.

Legend may be gained through:

- defeating enemies,
    
- surviving danger,
    
- acting dramatically,
    
- isolating stronger foes,
    
- or succeeding under pressure.
    

Legend may be lost through:

- hesitation,
    
- defeat,
    
- humiliation,
    
- fear,
    
- or losing momentum.
    

The Drifter’s maximum Legend is shown on the class table.

Legend resets after encounters.

---

## Stances

At 2nd level, the Drifter learns a combat stance.

A stance represents:

- mindset,
    
- posture,
    
- rhythm,
    
- or combat philosophy.
    

Unlike Deeds:

- stances are not activated,
    
- do not cost Legend,
    
- and remain active continuously until changed.
    

Changing stance is a Swift Action.

The Drifter gains additional stances at:

- 5th,
    
- 9th,
    
- and 14th level.
    

Examples include:

- aggressive dueling stances,
    
- defensive stances,
    
- mobile gunfighter stances,
    
- ambush stances,
    
- or survival-focused stances.
    

---

## Lone Wolf (Ex)

The Drifter performs best while isolated.

If the Drifter begins their turn with no allies nearby, they gain  
additional Legend.

The exact amount is determined later during balancing.

Adapted from Lone Wolf.

---

## Danger Sense (Ex)

The Drifter develops instinctive awareness for danger.

The Drifter may:

- detect ambushes,
    
- notice traps,
    
- identify hostile intent,
    
- and react to hidden threats unusually quickly.
    

Adapted from Danger Sense.

---

## Stoicism (Ex)

The Drifter develops extraordinary mental resilience through hardship.

At higher levels, the Drifter becomes immune to certain conditions.

|Level|Immunity|
|:--|:--|
|2|Sickened, Nauseated|
|6|Fascination|
|10|Fear|
|14|Dazed|
|18|Stun|

Adapted from Stoicism.

---

## Quick Step

At 3rd level, the Drifter becomes exceptionally difficult to pin down.

The Drifter gains bonuses to:

- movement,
    
- repositioning,
    
- disengagement,
    
- and traversal through hostile terrain.
    

---

## Improved Deed

At 4th level, and every four levels thereafter, the Drifter may:

- improve an existing Deed,
    
- or learn an advanced version of one.
    

Improved Deeds become more dramatic, efficient, or dangerous.

---

## Impossible Timing

At 7th level, the Drifter learns to exploit tiny openings in combat.

The Drifter may:

- react unexpectedly,
    
- interrupt enemies,
    
- or reposition at impossible moments.
    

---

## Evasion (Ex)

At 9th level, the Drifter can avoid even magical and unusual attacks  
through extreme agility.

If the Drifter succeeds on a Reflex save for half damage, they instead  
take no damage.

Adapted from Evasion.

---

## Survivor’s Instinct

At 11th level, the Drifter instinctively senses lethal danger.

The Drifter may:

- avoid fatal mistakes,
    
- react before conscious thought,
    
- and survive situations that should kill ordinary people.
    

---

## Uncatchable

At 13th level, the Drifter becomes extraordinarily difficult to corner  
or restrain.

The Drifter gains advantages against:

- grapples,
    
- restraints,
    
- pursuit,
    
- and environmental confinement.
    

---

## Improved Evasion (Ex)

At 14th level, the Drifter takes only half damage even on failed Reflex  
saves against effects that normally allow half damage.

Adapted from Improved Evasion.

---

## Living Legend

At 15th level, stories about the Drifter spread ahead of them.

People may:

- recognize them,
    
- fear them,
    
- owe them favors,
    
- or already know stories of their exploits.
    

The Drifter may leverage this reputation socially or narratively.

---

## Hide in Plain Sight (Ex)

At 17th level, the Drifter may hide even while observed in urban or  
crowded environments.

Adapted from Hide in Plain Sight.

---

## Final Stand

At 19th level, the Drifter refuses to fall while Legend remains.

The Drifter may continue fighting through injuries that would kill or  
disable others.

The exact mechanics are determined later.

---

## Blindsight (Ex)

At 20th level, the Drifter gains blindsight out to 30 feet.

Darkness, invisibility, and concealment become largely irrelevant.

Adapted from Blindsight.

---

## Mythic Wanderer

At 20th level, the Drifter becomes something larger than mortal legend.

The Drifter always seems to:

- survive,
    
- arrive where needed,
    
- escape impossible situations,
    
- and continue wandering onward.
    

Stories about them spread endlessly across the frontier.
