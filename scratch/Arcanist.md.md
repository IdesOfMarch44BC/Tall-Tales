# Arcanist

The first spell an Arcanist casts is called their Spark.

It is not merely the first spell they learned. It is the first impossible  
thing they learned was true.

An Arcanist does not study magic in the way a scholar studies history or  
mathematics. Arcane magic erupts into the soul and permanently alters the  
person carrying it. Every spell the Arcanist learns afterward grows from  
that first revelation like branches from a tree.

Some Sparks begin as simple things:

- leaping onto a building,
    
- a object floating mid-air,
    
- a reflection moving before its owner.
    

Others begin as terror:

- fire and heat from nowhere,
    
- hearing the dead speak,
    
- watching shadows bleed.
    

Whatever form it took, the Spark changed the Arcanist forever.

# Game Rule Information

- **Primary Ability:** Intelligence
    
- **Secondary Ability:** Wisdom
    
- **Alignment:** Any
    
- **Hit Die:** d6
    

**Class Skills (4 + Int modifier):** Concentration, Craft, Decipher  
Script, Knowledge (all skills taken individually), Profession,  
Search, Sense Motive, Spellcraft

---

# Weapon and Armor Proficiency

Arcanists are proficient with clubs, daggers, crossbows, staves, and  
other simple weapons.

Arcanists are not proficient with armor or shields.

Armor interferes with the flow of arcane accumulation and increases the  
difficulty of casting spells.

---

# Class Features

All of the following are class features of the Arcanist.

## Spark

At 1st level, you gain a Spark.

Your Spark is the first impossible thing you learned was true.

Choose one 1st level spell to learn. This spell becomes your Spark and  
defines the nature of your arcane magic.

Every future spell you learn must be thematically related to your Spark as determined by the GM.

Examples:

- A Spark of _Ignition_ might lead toward heat, combustion, light,  
    explosions, stars, smoke, and destruction.
    
- A Spark of _Feather Fall_ might lead toward gravity, wind, levitation,  
    flight, or spatial manipulation.
    
- A Spark of _Speak with Dead_ might lead toward ghosts, memory, decay,  
    funerary rites, or necromancy.
    

The GM is the final arbiter of whether a spell fits your Spark.

---

## Spellcasting

Arcanists know spells rather than preparing them.

Every spell has a Casting Value determined by the GM.

To cast a spell:

- choose a spell you know,
    
- begin accumulating arcane force,
    
- and roll Intelligence once per round.
    

The total accumulates between rounds.

When the total equals or exceeds the spell’s Casting Value, the spell is  
cast successfully.

If your concentration is interrupted, the spell fails and all progress  
toward casting it is lost.

---

## Accelerants

An Arcanist may accelerate spellcasting through sacrifice.

The following may add progress toward a spell’s Casting Value:

- sacrificing hit points,
    
- consuming valuable materials,
    
- prepared ritual circles,
    
- magical catalysts,
    
- true names,
    
- rare reagents,
    
- or other occult preparations approved by the GM.
    

Arcane magic is not safe. Faster casting usually means greater danger.

---

## Arcane Manifestation

Whenever you cast a spell, your Spark manifests physically in the world.

The manifestation is chosen by the player and reflects the nature of  
your Spark and personal magical style.

Examples include:

- rising heat,
    
- frost spreading across surfaces,
    
- geometric shadows,
    
- whispers,
    
- bleeding light,
    
- distorted reflections,
    
- unnatural silence,
    
- burning eyes,
    
- floating ash,
    
- ringing bells,
    
- reversed gravity,
    
- or nearby objects vibrating.
    

These manifestations are usually cosmetic.

At higher levels, they become increasingly intense and difficult to  
suppress.

---

## Arcane Focus

At 1st level, choose one Arcane Focus.

### Battery

You store arcane force externally.

Once per encounter, you may immediately gain additional casting progress  
equal to your Intelligence modifier.

---

### Familiar

Your magical cognition is distributed between two minds.

You gain a familiar.

While your familiar is nearby:

- you gain advantage on Concentration rolls,
    
- and may perceive through its senses.
    

If your familiar dies, you cannot cast spells until you complete a long  
rest.

---

### Lens

You refine and shape magical output precisely.

Once per encounter, after casting a spell, choose one:

- increase its duration,
    
- increase its area,
    
- increase its range,
    
- or increase its numerical effects moderately.
    

The GM determines the exact increase.

---

## Ritual Casting

At 2nd level, you may cast spells as rituals.

A ritual:

- takes at least 10 minutes,
    
- requires uninterrupted concentration,
    
- and usually involves components, diagrams, chants, or preparation.
    

Ritual casting is:

- safer,
    
- more stable,
    
- and easier to amplify
    

than ordinary casting.

Many powerful spells can only be cast ritually.

---

## Arcane Sight

At 3rd level, you can perceive arcane phenomena instinctively.

You can:

- sense active magic nearby,
    
- identify magical disturbances,
    
- and recognize ongoing supernatural effects.
    

---

## Efficient Casting

At 4th level, your control improves.

Whenever you roll casting progress, treat rolls below your Intelligence  
modifier as though they were equal to your Intelligence modifier.

---

## Bonus Feat

At 5th level and every five levels thereafter, gain a bonus feat.

This feat must relate to:

- magic,
    
- rituals,
    
- arcane knowledge,
    
- spell shaping,
    
- or magical crafting.
    

---

## Overcasting

At 6th level, you may exceed safe casting limits.

You may continue accumulating progress beyond a spell’s normal Casting  
Value to empower it further.

Possible benefits include:

- larger area,
    
- increased damage,
    
- longer duration,
    
- or additional targets.
    

Every round spent overcasting risks magical backlash determined by the  
GM.

---

## Spell Shaping

At 7th level, you may alter the form of your spells while casting them.

Examples include:

- changing damage shape,
    
- excluding targets,
    
- delaying effects,
    
- muting visual manifestations,
    
- or splitting effects.
    

---

## Stabilized Spark

At 8th level, your Spark becomes harder to disrupt.

You gain:

- resistance against magical corruption,
    
- advantage on rolls against magical interference,
    
- and immunity to mundane fear caused by supernatural phenomena.
    

---

## Arcane Presence

At 9th level, reality subtly reacts to your existence.

Small magical phenomena occur naturally around you:

- lights flicker,
    
- glass hums,
    
- animals react nervously,
    
- temperatures shift,
    
- or nearby objects move slightly.
    

These effects intensify while casting.

---

## Split Concentration

At 11th level, you may maintain progress toward two spells  
simultaneously.

You still roll separately for each spell.

---

## Impossible Casting

At 12th level, you may attempt spells beyond mortal limits.

The GM may permit:

- experimental spells,
    
- unstable rituals,
    
- forgotten magic,
    
- or temporary magical effects
    

outside your normal capabilities.

These castings are dangerous and often irreversible.

---

## Persistent Ritual

At 13th level, your rituals can permanently alter locations, objects, or  
creatures.

Examples include:

- cursed ground,
    
- eternal flames,
    
- impossible architecture,
    
- haunted locations,
    
- magical weather,
    
- or dimensional instability.
    

---

## Arcane Distortion

At 14th level, your presence weakens local reality.

While casting:

- nearby mundane flames distort,
    
- gravity fluctuates,
    
- reflections behave incorrectly,
    
- and weak magical effects may fail unpredictably.
    

The exact effects depend on your Spark.

---

## Living Conduit

At 16th level, your body becomes permanently altered by arcane force.

Choose one:

- immunity to mundane disease and poison,
    
- reduced need for sleep,
    
- slowed aging,
    
- resistance to hostile magic,
    
- or another arcane mutation approved by the GM.
    

---

## Spell Fusion

At 17th level, you may combine two related spells into a single casting.

The GM determines:

- compatibility,
    
- Casting Value,
    
- and final effects.
    

---

## Reality Fracture

At 18th level, your strongest magic tears reality itself.

High-level spells may produce:

- permanent scars,
    
- magical anomalies,
    
- warped terrain,
    
- lingering manifestations,
    
- or unstable dimensional effects.
    

---

## Arcane Apotheosis

At 19th level, your Spark can no longer be fully concealed.

Your Arcane Manifestations:

- constantly bleed into reality,
    
- intensify automatically during emotional stress,
    
- and may appear even while unconscious or asleep.
    

You are permanently recognizable as something no longer entirely mortal.

---

## Perfect Spark

At 20th level, your Spark fully merges with your existence.

- You no longer require material accelerants for ordinary spells.
    
- You may cast rituals while moving slowly.
    
- Your Arcane Manifestations may physically affect the environment.
    
- Once per day, you may cast a spell instantly without accumulating  
    progress.
    
- You become immune to magical aging and involuntary transformation.
