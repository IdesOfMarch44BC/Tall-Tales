# Tinkerer

The Tinkerer is an engineer, architect, artificer, and theoretician of  
superscience. Where mages impose their will upon reality through sparks  
and rituals, the Tinkerer studies the hidden laws beneath reality and  
constructs machines that exploit them.

A Tinkerer does not cast spells. They build systems.

Through discoveries and principles of superscience, a Tinkerer creates:

- engines powered by vim,
    
- floating fortresses,
    
- dimensional gates,
    
- impossible materials,
    
- living machines,
    
- automated factories,
    
- and cities that should not exist.
    

Most people call these things magic. The Tinkerer knows better.

# Class Features

All of the following are class features of the Tinkerer.

## Principles

The Tinkerer studies the hidden laws of superscience.

Instead of spells, the Tinkerer learns **Principles**:  
repeatable engineering procedures that manipulate matter, energy,  
space, life, motion, information, and vim.

Principles are not cast. They are prepared.

Preparing a Principle requires:

- tools,
    
- materials,
    
- time,
    
- and often permanent infrastructure.
    

A Principle may:

- power a machine,
    
- alter material properties,
    
- generate energy,
    
- create dimensional effects,
    
- automate systems,
    
- or construct persistent magical technology.
    

Principles are effectively at-will so long as the Tinkerer has the  
necessary time, materials, and workspace.

The Tinkerer gains:

- 1 Principle at 1st level,
    
- and 1 additional Principle every odd level thereafter.
    

Principles are divided into fields such as:

- Alchemy,
    
- Thaumaturgy,
    
- Channeling,
    
- Biotech,
    
- Heuristics,
    
- Dimensional Engineering,
    
- Kinetics,
    
- or Environmental Manipulation.
    

The exact list of Principles is determined later.

Adapted from the Gramarie system.

---

## Specialization

At 1st level, choose a field of superscience to specialize in.

Examples include:

- energy systems,
    
- dimensional engineering,
    
- living structures,
    
- automation,
    
- advanced materials,
    
- or transportation systems.
    

The Tinkerer gains bonuses when working within their specialization and  
may unlock specialist Discoveries unavailable to others.

---

## Prototype Device

At 1st level, the Tinkerer creates a signature invention.

Examples include:

- a vim reactor,
    
- mechanical companion,
    
- dimensional lens,
    
- powered gauntlet,
    
- analytical visor,
    
- living prosthetic,
    
- or impossible firearm.
    

This device evolves as the Tinkerer gains levels.

If destroyed, the Tinkerer may rebuild it through extended work.

---

## Engineering Insight

Beginning at 2nd level, the Tinkerer can rapidly analyze machines,  
structures, and systems.

The Tinkerer may determine:

- purpose,
    
- flaws,
    
- operating principles,
    
- age,
    
- materials,
    
- power source,
    
- or weak points  
    of a device through examination.
    

The GM must answer truthfully.

---

## Spectral Construction

At 3rd level, the Tinkerer gains the ability to rapidly construct  
structures through summoned labor, automated systems, or engineered  
tools.

The Tinkerer may dramatically accelerate:

- construction,
    
- excavation,
    
- repair,
    
- reinforcement,
    
- or fabrication projects.
    

Large-scale engineering becomes feasible even in hostile environments.

Adapted from Spectroconstruction.

---

## Bonus Discovery

At 4th level, and again at 12th level, the Tinkerer gains a bonus  
Discovery from any field of superscience they have access to.

Discoveries represent major breakthroughs or inventions.

---

## Expanded Construction

At 5th level, the Tinkerer may maintain additional active structures,  
devices, or large-scale engineering projects simultaneously.

---

## Advanced Principles

Beginning at 6th level, the Tinkerer gains access to more advanced  
Principles.

These may involve:

- advanced vim manipulation,
    
- mobile infrastructure,
    
- automated systems,
    
- large-scale reactors,
    
- or dangerous experimental technologies.
    

---

## Infrastructure Network

At 7th level, the Tinkerer may link machines and structures into a  
single coordinated system.

Examples include:

- linked reactors,
    
- automated defenses,
    
- transport rails,
    
- communication systems,
    
- or distributed computational networks.
    

---

## Discovery

At 8th level, and every two levels thereafter, the Tinkerer gains a  
Discovery.

A Discovery is a major technological breakthrough or specialized  
engineering technique.

Examples include:

- self-repairing structures,
    
- living circuitry,
    
- autonomous drones,
    
- dimensional anchors,
    
- adaptive armor,
    
- or impossible alloys.
    

---

## Rapid Fabrication

Beginning at 9th level, the Tinkerer can dramatically reduce the time  
required to construct or modify devices.

Minor projects may be completed during scenes rather than downtime.

---

## Second Discipline

At 11th level, the Tinkerer gains mastery in a second field of  
superscience.

They may now specialize in two disciplines simultaneously.

---

## Master Construction

At 13th level, the Tinkerer becomes capable of constructing massive  
projects previously considered impossible.

Examples include:

- skyships,
    
- moving fortresses,
    
- underground cities,
    
- biomechanical factories,
    
- or dimensional gateways.
    

---

## Doctorate Principles

Beginning at 14th level, the Tinkerer gains access to the highest grade  
of Principles.

These are world-changing technologies that redefine civilizations.

Adapted from Doctorate Principles.

---

## Living Infrastructure

At 15th level, the Tinkerer may integrate biological and mechanical  
systems seamlessly.

Their structures may:

- heal,
    
- adapt,
    
- reproduce,
    
- or evolve over time.
    

---

## Impossible Engineering

At 17th level, the Tinkerer learns to violate accepted physical law.

Their constructions may:

- ignore gravity,
    
- exist partially outside reality,
    
- violate conservation,
    
- or function in impossible environments.
    

---

## World Machine

At 19th level, the Tinkerer may create a permanent megastructure or  
system that reshapes a region.

Examples include:

- weather engines,
    
- dimensional stabilizers,
    
- automated cities,
    
- global communication grids,
    
- or planetary transport systems.
    

---

## Grand Theory

At 20th level, the Tinkerer discovers a new foundational law of  
superscience.

Choose or create a supreme Principle representing a revolutionary  
breakthrough.

This discovery permanently changes the world’s understanding of  
technology and vim.

Adapted from Grand Theory.

---

## The Architect

At 20th level, the Tinkerer achieves perfect understanding of  
superscientific engineering.

Their creations operate with impossible reliability and precision.

The Tinkerer may:

- instinctively understand machines,
    
- perfectly execute engineering procedures,
    
- and create wonders indistinguishable from miracles.
    

Their Principles become so refined that many no longer recognize them as  
supernatural at all.

Adapted from The Architect.
